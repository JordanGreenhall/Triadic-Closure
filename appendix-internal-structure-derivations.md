# Appendix — Internal-Structure Derivations

This appendix preserves the detailed derivational chain supporting document 05. Document 05 governs every claim's standing and scope.

## 1. Same-kind carrier

The three interior moments occupy one same-kind complex carrier \(V\cong\mathbb C^3\). Same-kind means that the moments share addition, scalar multiplication, comparison, and one Hermitian pairing; they are not three role-sorted slots. Dimension three is inherited from the primitive interior triad under the This-flattening and minimality. Complex structure is inherited from the licensed rule-given \(\mathbb C\), not imported from Hilbert-space practice.

The closure verdict is scalar. A vector-valued or operator-valued verdict would leave an unclosed internal direction; a projective verdict would discard registered magnitude; a symmetric ternary verdict would fail to distinguish complete orientation. The carrier therefore bears a fixed nonzero alternating 3-form \(\varepsilon\in\Lambda^3V^*\) together with a non-degenerate Hermitian pairing \(h\).

Named residuals remain exactly those stated in document 05: cyclic formalization, the relate-through criterion, and the no-privileged-frame scope of the irreducibility argument.

## 2. Alternating closure and SU(3)

For three interior contributions \(m_1,m_2,m_3\), complete closure requires that they span \(V\). In dimension three this is equivalent to

\[
\varepsilon(m_1,m_2,m_3)=\det[m_1\ m_2\ m_3]\ne0.
\]

Exchange of two contributions reverses the closure orientation, so the form is alternating. The connected complex-linear stabilizer of \(\varepsilon\) is \(SL(3,\mathbb C)\). Requiring preservation of the registered Hermitian distinctions restricts to

\[
SL(3,\mathbb C)\cap U(3)=SU(3).
\]

The center \(\mathbb Z_3\) acts nontrivially, so the group is SU(3) rather than its projective quotient. A complete triad and a moment–antimoment pair are the neutral closure forms recognizable as baryon and meson. A single interior moment does not close independently.

## 3. The three gauge sources

The three office-sources are:

- This-flattened interior distinction: SU(3), vectorial because it does not engage the propagation arrow.
- With-flattened rotatability: \(Spin(3)=SU(2)\), chiral because orientation-faithful coupling engages the arrow.
- From-phase: U(1), the oriented valence axis.

The completeness of this source inventory is a defended posit inherited from the exactly-three office ceiling. It is not a theorem that fixes the full electroweak module, breaking pattern, mixing angle, or charge values.

## 4. Commutant and pure chiral projectors

For the minimal spinor carrier, let \(h=\sigma\cdot\hat p\) be helicity. Rotations about the propagation arrow form the U(1) generated by \(h\). A coupling compatible with that registered symmetry must lie in the commutant of \(h\). Because \(h\) is nondegenerate on the two-dimensional spinor space,

\[
\operatorname{Comm}(h)=\operatorname{span}\{I,h\}.
\]

A transverse Pauli component would register an additional direction and violate no-privileged-frame. An oriented coupling cannot be proportional only to \(I\), so it has a nonzero helicity component. Stable coherent attachment requires idempotency \(M^2=M\). The nontrivial idempotents in \(\operatorname{span}\{I,h\}\) are

\[
P_L=\frac{I-h}{2},\qquad P_R=\frac{I+h}{2}.
\]

Thus pure-projector chirality is forced. Which projector is selected remains the binary handedness selection.

## 5. Quark and lepton roles

Every elementary fermionic closure carries the interior triad in germ. It can stand in exactly two ways relative to that interior:

- internally complete: the triad closes within the elementary closure, yielding a color-singlet lepton role;
- as one unresolved interior moment: closure requires external completion, yielding a color-bearing quark role.

The distinction is structural and binary. It does not supply exact electric charges, generations, Yukawa couplings, or the quantitative QCD spectrum.

## 6. Mark geometry

After color closes, three non-interchangeable marks remain. No mark is privileged and the marks form a cyclic order. The real representation splits into a fixed line plus a two-dimensional rotation plane; no-privileged-frame excludes the fixed internal line, so

\[
e_u+e_d+e_s=0.
\]

Equal lengths and equal pairwise overlap then give

\[
3+6p=0,\qquad p=e_u\cdot e_d=-\frac12.
\]

The marks are therefore three equal vectors at 120 degrees in their own plane. This geometry is native to the marks and does not depend on importing flavor SU(3).

## 7. Proton, neutron, and the mark-access overlap

The proton and neutron share the same color-singlet closure. Their distinction lies in the mark configurations

\[
P=2u+d,\qquad N=u+2d.
\]

With \(u\cdot d=-1/2\),

\[
|P|^2=|N|^2=3,\qquad P\cdot N=\frac32,\qquad \cos\theta=\frac12.
\]

The Born-squared access weight is therefore \(1/4\). This access overlap is distinct from the normalized isospin ladder matrix element, which lives in the orthonormal doublet readout space.

The relief-valve mechanism assigns the sign of the proton–neutron splitting: the proton's outward edge can be satisfied by an external inverse partner, whereas the neutron bears its valence load internally. The exact magnitude retains the conjectured receipt-complement factor and the mass-side correction stated in documents 05 and 06.
