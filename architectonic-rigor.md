# Architectonic Rigor

Architectonic rigor governs what each securing instrument can establish and where that instrument structurally stops. A claim's standing is fixed by the route that reaches it; a different kind of claim may require a different kind of warrant.

## The completeness ceiling

Retorsion secures ineliminability: at least this condition must operate in any determination. It cannot certify an exhaustive inventory, because the act certifying the inventory would itself deploy that inventory. Construction has the same limit. This is not a weakness of one candidate inventory. It binds every claim of the form “these are all the ultimate terms.”

The completeness diagnostic identifies that ceiling. Once it fires, demanding a stronger formal derivation is a category mistake: formal proof already presupposes a fixed inventory and therefore cannot certify the completeness of the inventory it uses.

## Defended posit

A defended posit is the correct and maximal warrant for a claim at the completeness ceiling. Its defense profile has three parts:

1. **Forced floor:** collapse below the proposed inventory is blocked by retorsion.
2. **Survived examination:** candidate rivals have been examined and return as subdivisions, textures, or second-order statuses rather than genuine alternatives.
3. **Reflexive stability:** the posit, turned on itself, returns itself rather than self-destructing.

A defended posit is not unfinished proof. It is closed to stronger certification because stronger certification is structurally unavailable to every rival, while remaining open to refutation: exhibit a genuine irreducible alternative and the posit fails.

## Exactly-three

Exactly-three carries the strongest warrant any ultimate office-count can carry. The at-least-three floor is retorsion-secured; proposed fourth offices have returned as subdivisions or second-order statuses; and the defense itself is a From/With/This act. Downstream successful derivations and empirical passes provide the available upward confirmation without changing the claim's warrant type.

## Execution rule

Do not report a defended posit as apologetic, weak, provisional, or awaiting a formal proof. Do not promote it to a theorem. State its maximal warrant, its defense profile, its dependency scope, and its open refutation condition.
