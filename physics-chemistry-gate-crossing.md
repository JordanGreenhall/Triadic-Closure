## 2. D7: carbon selected by criterion

### 2.1 The rule (§8.5), applied correctly

D7 is **not** the "best atom." It is the location in the D6 totality selected by
*composition-richness in the successor's terms, together with its ambient partners*.
So the successor domain must be identified first, and its unit named.

### 2.2 The successor domain, from the residue

Charge-closure (Gate A) closes at neutrality but leaves the **shell-edge**
unclosed. Per the general handoff recognition (§2.4 below), the successor inhabits
that residue. The successor domain is **shell-edge closure-completion**; its D1
unit is the **bond** (two atoms jointly holding a standing that completes a
shell-closure for both). Noble gases = doubly-closed (charge- and shell-complete)
⇒ no residual edge ⇒ inert. **Valence = residual shell-edge.** [Registered-candidate]

### 2.3 Composition-richness resolved into three native requirements

For the successor to be a domain (to have a D3-recursion), its D7-provisioner must
maximize:

1. **multiple simultaneous edges** — ≥2 to bridge (needs p-block);
2. **give/receive balance** — only the shell **midpoint** shares rather than
   terminates (ionizes/saturates);
3. **self-composability** — edges close against their own kind (**catenation** =
   the successor's D3-recursion; the deep requirement).

Octet capacity = 2(2·0+1)+2(2·1+1) = **8** (derived: irreps × lift × Pauli).
Midpoint = **4** = group 14 (C, Si, Ge). Four edges: equidistant from empty and
octet ⇒ shares four ways.

**Selection:** octet-midpoint (from Pauli capacity) ∧ smallest-bridging-scale (from
screening) = **carbon**, in the company of its partners (H cap; O, N hetero-completers
— the "ambient partners" clause, occupied qualitatively). [Registered-candidate]

### 2.5 Bond geometry — KEYSTONE REPAIRED (audit finding)

**Original (retracted):** no-privileged-frame reorganizes 2s²2p² into four
equivalent edges → forced tetrahedron.

**Why it failed:** no-privileged-frame is licensed only over positions
**registrably identical under the basis**. s and p standings are registrably
distinct (different irreps, Casimirs, texture cost, penetration) — differences this
very walk Registered. Applying the move across a registered difference is
misapplication (held/registered violation, §2.5 discipline). **Falsifier the
over-claim earned:** forced tetrahedron makes sp/sp² impossible; graphite (120°)
and acetylene (180°) exist. The original over-derived and was dead on contact.

**Repair:** the equivalence is not in the atom's internal s/p structure; it is in
the **composite closure** — bonds to identical partners. Partners ARE registrably
identical ⇒ no-privileged-frame applies at the **partner level** with full license.
Equal standings maximally separated through three isotropic degrees (rule-given):

- 2 partners → 180°
- 3 partners → 120° (**literally the mark-metric argument, one altitude up**)
- 4 partners → 109.47° (tetrahedron)

sp/sp²/sp³ are the 2/3/4-partner closures of **one** derivation. Hybridization is
demoted to basis-relative bookkeeping of the composite closure read back onto the
atom (internal s/p = the *held*; bond geometry = what the closure *registers*). The
falsifiers become derived cases.
