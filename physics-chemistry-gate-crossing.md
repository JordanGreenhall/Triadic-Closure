# Physics–Chemistry Gate-Crossing — Historical Proposal and Process Shadow

The canonical claim, grade, dependency, and frontier record is [X1 Physics Maturity and Chemistry Entry Gate](x1-physics-maturity-and-chemistry-entry-gate.md). This page no longer carries current theoretical warrant.

## What the original deposit contributed

The July 2 deposit was the first detailed attempt to walk from physics into chemistry. It proposed:

- an atomic/periodic physics ground;
- shell-edge closure-completion as the chemistry constraint;
- the bond as successor D1;
- a three-gate route through binding, interaction form, and periodic ordering;
- carbon as D7 by multiple edges, balance, self-composability, and ambient partners;
- a general residue/recursion-maximization handoff rule.

It also performed a useful adversarial repair: the claimed forced tetrahedral geometry was withdrawn at the atom's internal `s/p` level and relocated to a conditional equal-partner composite argument. That episode remains valuable evidence for the no-privileged-frame license boundary.

## Current disposition

X1 has now audited the proposal against the normalized F4 and P6–P29 owners.

- The atom, electronic shells, periodic landscape, and shell-edge object are not currently constructed as framework-standing physics.
- P15 signed relational valence is not by itself an electronic shell edge.
- P19's static proton/electron ratio is not a dynamical slow/fast or Born–Oppenheimer theorem.
- The P20 relief bridge needed by the binding interpretation remains unresolved.
- Shell-edge closure-completion has not yet met F4 validity and nonemptiness.
- Bond construction belongs to CH1 and may not be read backward as proof of the gate.
- Carbon selection depends on the missing atomic landscape and later chemistry catenation work.
- One proposed case does not establish a universal handoff law.

The original detailed text remains recoverable in repository history at the recorded blob. It is retained as candidate research, failed-route control, and developmental provenance rather than duplicated into the living corpus.
