# Cross-Domain Handoff Mechanism

## Mature status

Status: **Registered-candidate as the generalization of one exhibited gate-crossing.**

This is a cross-domain result. It is not a chemistry-only observation.

## Mechanism

> **The successor domain inhabits the residue the predecessor's closure did not consume, and its D7 element is the one that maximizes the successor's recursion.**

The predecessor's mature closure does not hand over an arbitrary object. It leaves a determinate residue: a standing capacity or edge that its own closure did not consume. That residue supplies the successor domain's operative field. The successor's D1 unit is the minimal closure of that residue, and the predecessor's D7 selection is the content that most strongly supports the successor's D3 recursion.

D7 therefore cannot be selected by prominence inside the predecessor domain. The successor constraint must first be identified from the residue; only then can composition-richness be evaluated in the successor's terms.

## Exhibited gate-crossing: physics to chemistry

Physics' charge-closure closes at neutrality but leaves the shell-edge unconsumed. Chemistry inhabits that residue as shell-edge closure-completion, with the bond as its D1 unit. Chemistry's recursion is catenation. The physics D7 provisioner is therefore the element that maximizes catenation under the successor's native requirements: carbon, together with its ambient partners—hydrogen as cap and oxygen and nitrogen as hetero-completers.

This single completed gate establishes the mechanism in one instance. It licenses the cross-domain generalization at Registered-candidate grade; it does not yet seal a universal law of all domain transitions.

## Operational rule for future gates

1. Identify the residue left unconsumed by the predecessor's closure.
2. State the successor domain constraint from that residue.
3. Name the successor's D1 unit as the minimal closure of the residue.
4. Identify the successor's D3 recursion.
5. Select the predecessor's D7 content by maximal support for that recursion, including the required ambient partners.

## Boundary

- The physics-to-chemistry crossing is the only fully exhibited instance presently in the corpus.
- The proposed chemistry-to-biology continuation remains Recognition only: stable molecular closure may leave reactive, configurational or informational residue, but no biology gate has yet been walked.
- This result does not replace the general domain-entry method. It supplies the transition rule by which one completed domain provisions the next.
