# P10 Quark–Lepton Split and Color Exposure

P10 asks how an elementary fermionic closure may stand relative to the P9 interior color triad. Its exact unit is the distinction between an externally exposed interior moment and an internally closed color triad, together with the two resulting routes to color neutrality.

P10 does not derive the P9 carrier or SU(3), the dynamical law of confinement, exact electric charges, weak representations, chirality, flavor, masses, generations, hadron taxonomy, or the completeness of the Standard Model matter spectrum.

## 1. Inherited structure

P10 inherits at their existing grades:

- the P3 fermionic/antisymmetric holding sector;
- the P8 one same-kind internal carrier at M6's selected complex-extension grade;
- the P9 alternating closure form and native SU(3), Registered but not Sealed;
- the P9 closure-floor: a single exposed interior moment is not by itself a color-neutral ternary closure.

P10 does not re-prove any of these premises. Its own burden is to classify the elementary closure's standing relative to the inherited interior triad.

## 2. Two exhibited standings relative to the interior triad

### 2.1 Internally closed standing

The interior triad may be closed within one elementary fermionic standing. The triad then remains constitutive but is not externally resolved into a color-bearing part-role.

This is the framework core licensed by the name **lepton**:

> an elementary fermionic closure whose interior color triad is closed within the standing and therefore presents no exposed color distinction.

Standing:

- the internally closed case is **Secured by construction** relative to P8/P9;
- `lepton` is **Registered, not Sealed** for this colorless-whole core;
- freedom from strong interaction is licensed only as absence of exposed P9 color role, not as a complete dynamical theorem.

### 2.2 Exposed one-moment standing

An elementary fermionic standing may instead present one resolved interior moment as its externally registered color role. Relative to the ternary closure-floor, that one-moment standing is incomplete as a color-neutral closure.

This is the framework core licensed by the name **quark**:

> an elementary fermionic closure standing as one exposed moment of the inherited interior triad and therefore carrying an unresolved color role.

Standing:

- the exposed one-moment case is **Secured by construction** relative to P8/P9;
- `quark` is **Registered, not Sealed** for this color-bearing part-role core;
- the need for additional color-completion is structural; the full dynamical content of QCD confinement is not supplied by P10.

## 3. Is the split exhaustive and binary?

The corpus claims an elementary binary:

1. the triad is internally closed within one standing;
2. one interior moment is externally exposed.

A standing presenting two exposed moments is naturally read as a composite two-part color structure rather than a third elementary kind. That reading is coherent with the P9 ternary floor, but the exclusion of every primitive two-moment or mixed-resolution elementary countermodel has not been formally completed.

Therefore:

- the two cases are **Registered as the exhibited quark/lepton distinction**;
- exhaustiveness of the elementary binary is **Registered, not Sealed**;
- a complete classification of primitive standings relative to the interior triad remains a P10 seal residual.

This avoids converting the observed two-family matter classification into the proof of its own framework exhaustiveness.

## 4. Two routes to color neutrality

P10 distinguishes two routes:

### 4.1 Unresolved internal closure

The interior triad closes without being externally resolved. The resulting elementary standing is color-neutral in the P10 sense.

### 4.2 Resolved recombination

Exposed color moments may combine so that the P9 alternating closure form is completed. A complete triad gives the baryonic route; a moment paired with the corresponding opposite-oriented color role gives the mesonic route where that conjugate construction is separately licensed.

The structural distinction between elementary internal neutrality and composite recombined neutrality is **Registered**. Exact hadron populations, binding energies, flux-tube dynamics, asymptotic freedom, and decay channels are outside P10.

## 5. Confinement claim boundary

P10 secures the following structural statement:

> an exposed single color moment is not itself a complete color-neutral closure under the P9 ternary floor and therefore requires completion in any independently standing color-neutral composite.

This may be called **confinement in structural essence** only with the boundary explicit. P10 does not derive:

- the Yang–Mills field equations;
- a linearly rising potential;
- flux tubes;
- an area law;
- the confinement scale;
- absence of every conceivable isolated colored state under all dynamics.

The mature QCD dynamical theorem remains Open.

## 6. Charge and weak-structure limits

The one-of-three versus closed-triad distinction makes thirds-versus-integers a recognizable structural pattern, but it does not derive electric charge values or the charge operator. Statements such as `2/3`, `-1/3`, `-1`, and `0` belong to later gauge-source and representation units.

Likewise, the fact that weak doublets cut across the quark/lepton distinction belongs to the later SU(2), chirality, and module construction. It is not part of P10's warrant.
