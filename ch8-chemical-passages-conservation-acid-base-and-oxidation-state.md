# CH8 — Chemical Passages, Conservation, Acid/Base, and Oxidation State

## Standing summary

CH8 owns the first normalized chemistry passage-and-invariant package. Its boundary is exact: chemistry re-apportions shell-edge and charge sites while preserving element identity; reaction classes are sorted by the site re-apportioned; Brønsted and Lewis acid/base descriptions are reconstructed as two site-readings of closure transfer; oxidation state is bookkeeping over held ownership, while transfer at a passage boundary is the registrable event.

The package is **Conjectured-strong and Unregistered as a completed passage theory**. Element and charge conservation inherit stronger parents; the mass ledger is near-exact in the stated maintenance sense. Passage occurrence, rates, mechanisms, equilibrium, thermodynamics, and catalysis are not derived here.

## Unit boundary

CH8 owns:

1. chemistry's no-transmutation boundary;
2. element, charge, and near-exact mass ledgers;
3. shell-edge-site versus charge-site passage taxonomy;
4. Brønsted/Lewis acid-base unification by site;
5. oxidation state as held bookkeeping and transfer registration.

CH8 does not own:

- CH6 functional groups or group carriage;
- CH7 cyclic delocalization or aromatic closure;
- kinetic occurrence, mechanisms, rate laws, activation barriers, equilibrium, thermodynamics, or catalysis;
- empirical pKa scales, solvent leveling, redox potentials, electrochemical series, or reaction catalogues.

## Chemical passage boundary

Within ordinary chemistry, admitted passages preserve nuclear/element role identity while re-apportioning electronic shell-edge and charge-site standing. Transmutation is therefore outside the chemistry passage class and belongs to predecessor-domain physics.

This result remains conditional on X1's still-unfinished construction of the chemistry domain and its passage class. It is not upgraded merely because empirical chemistry preserves elements.

## Conservation ledgers

### Element ledger

Element count per kind is exact inside the stipulated chemistry passage class because element identity is part of that class's preserved boundary.

### Charge ledger

Total charge conservation is inherited from P15/U(1)-side physics at its recorded standing. CH8 spends but does not re-own it.

### Mass ledger

Mass is near-exact, not absolute, because maintenance loads may be re-apportioned across a passage. The structural claim is that deviations are of the scale of chemistry's maintenance relief relative to the much larger inherited closure mass. No numerical defect, calorimetric conversion, or reaction-specific mass change is derived.

## Passage taxonomy by site

- **Shell-edge-site passages:** edge re-partnering, including the structural families later recognized as substitution, addition, and elimination.
- **Charge-site passages:** transfer creating, consuming, or relocating charge edges, including the structural family later recognized as redox.
- **Mixed passages:** coupled changes at both sites.

The taxonomy is architectural. Familiar reaction names may test it after construction; they do not supply the native partition.

## Acid/base unification

### Brønsted face

The transferred unit is H+, treated as the limiting shell-less closure: a nucleus carrying no held electronic shell interior. The structural claim concerns site character and transfer. Mobility, proton-transfer rate, solvent behavior, and pKa remain outside CH8.

### Lewis face

A whole held pair is donated into an engageable edge, completing a shell-edge standing. The donor/acceptor ordering is a candidate From-causation reading, not a derived mechanism or temporal rate law.

### One passage, two site-readings

Brønsted describes the charge-site face; Lewis describes the shell-edge-site face. Their unification is therefore structural and site-based, not numerical and not a claim that every empirical acid/base event is exhausted by one simple mechanism.

## Oxidation state

Oxidation state is integer bookkeeping imposed on held pair ownership. It is not a literal statement of atomic charge inside a composite. Fractional, mixed, or ambiguous assignments are expected where held ownership is not uniquely localizable.

What registers physically is transfer across a passage boundary. Redox is the passage; oxidation numbers are notation for tracking the ownership ledger.

## Smuggle-watches

1. **Reaction names are not the taxonomy.** Substitution, addition, elimination, and redox vocabulary cannot reverse-install the site partition.
2. **Acid/base labels are not scales.** pKa, solvent leveling, proton affinity, Lewis acidity numbers, and mechanisms remain outside the unit.
3. **Oxidation number is not charge density.** Bookkeeping does not establish literal constituent charge.
4. **Conservation is not occurrence.** Ledgers constrain realizable passages; they do not make any passage actual.
5. **Near-exact mass is not quantitative chemistry.** No numerical mass defect follows without a native maintenance functional and calibrated scale.
