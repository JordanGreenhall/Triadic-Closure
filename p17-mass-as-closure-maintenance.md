# P17 Mass as Closure Maintenance

## Scope

P17 owns four linked claims:

1. mass ontology;
2. closure loading;
3. the distinction between mass and energy;
4. the bounded relation between mass-bearing closure and horizon formation.

P17 does not own the closure-inherited metric or `2 pi^5` measure (P18), the electron ruler or proton/electron ratio (P19), the proton-neutron relation (P20), decay (P21), or horizon entropy and `A/4` (P27).

## 1. Mass ontology

A physical standing persists only by maintaining the closure through which it is the same standing. The recurrence is not an additional event imposed on an already-complete object. It is the standing's continued constitution.

Mass is the framework name for this self-directed closure-maintenance: the recurrent loading required for an identity-constituting closure to remain actual through successive passages.

Framework state: **Secured at structural scope**.

Semantic registration: **Registered, not Sealed** as mass.

The result does not yet establish that every physical use of mass is exhausted by this construction.

## 2. Closure loading

A closure can bear more or less constitutive work. Its mass-loading is the burden of maintaining the full set of relations required for that standing to recur as itself.

This supplies the qualitative basis for mass comparison: a standing carrying a more extensive or more deeply articulated closure-maintenance burden is more massive relative to a standing carrying less. P17 establishes this order-form only. It does not yet select a metric, measure, ruler, or numerical ratio.

The following are therefore later-owned:

- metric selection and closure measure;
- `2 pi^5` and `6 pi^5`;
- electron normalization;
- proton/electron and proton/neutron numerical relations;
- absolute units.

## 3. Mass and energy are distinct readings

Mass is the maintained closure-load of a standing. Energy is the standing read with respect to admissible change: the capacity, requirement, or realized passage by which its state differs, advances, or is reconfigured.

They are related because changing or transporting a closure bears on the same standing whose maintenance is mass. They are not identical by definition.

The This-From face is therefore the relation in which mass enters an energy relation as a term; it is not an additional productive factor that generates mass. The mature energy-momentum relation may be used as recognition of this structural distinction, not as its warrant.

Framework state: **Secured at conceptual-structural scope**.

Semantic registration: **Registered, not Sealed** for the mass/energy distinction.

Quantitative energy-mass conversion, dispersion relations, and calibrated coefficients remain outside P17.

## 4. Horizon relation

A horizon is the limiting case in which closure-maintenance becomes total with respect to an exterior: the standing's constitutive return no longer leaves an outwardly traversable continuation available across the relevant boundary.

This gives a structural relation between mass-loading and horizon formation. Increasing closure-load increases the demand borne by the surrounding relational field; at the limit of total self-closure, an exterior horizon appears.

P17 registers only that architectural relation:

> mass is partial self-closure maintenance; a horizon is the total-self-closure limit with respect to an exterior boundary.

Framework state: **Conjectured at the physical-identification level**, with the closure relation **Secured architecturally**.

P17 does not derive a Schwarzschild radius, `2GM`, area law, entropy, temperature, or `A/4`. Those require the later gravity and horizon units.

## Quarantine

P17 does not derive:

- a metric or measure on closure space;
- the `2 pi^5` or `6 pi^5` factors;
- a native electron ruler;
- any particle mass ratio;
- absolute masses or external-unit calibration;
- Higgs or Yukawa mass generation;
- black-hole radius, entropy, temperature, or area law.

## Frontiers

1. **P17-F1:** formalize closure loading as an order before metric selection.
2. **P17-F2:** prove or bound the mass/energy distinction in the selected physical calculus.
3. **P17-F3:** construct the bridge from increasing closure-load to the total-self-closure horizon limit without importing later GR formulae.

## Verdict

Mass is Registered, not Sealed, as closure maintenance. Closure loading supplies a qualitative order-form but no measure. Mass and energy are distinct structural readings of one standing. The horizon relation is architecturally available as the total-self-closure limit, while its physical identification and every quantitative horizon formula remain later-owned.
