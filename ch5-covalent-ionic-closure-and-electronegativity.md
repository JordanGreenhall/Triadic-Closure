# CH5 — Covalent–Ionic Closure and Electronegativity

## Standing summary

CH5 owns the proposed unification of covalent and ionic bonding as two limiting sites of one closure-relief architecture under registrable partner difference.

- **Covalent limit:** shell-edge completion remains jointly held across the partners; identical partners require symmetric apportionment at the level licensed by CH4 partner identity.
- **Ionic limit:** apportionment approaches transfer to one partner; the resulting opposite charge edges then require charge-site closure.
- **Electronegativity:** the effective screening texture presented by each partner at the shared standing; partner difference in that texture is the proposed source of apportionment asymmetry.

This architecture is **Conjectured-strong and Unregistered as a completed chemical theorem**. It is the selected framework-native CH5 reconstruction, but its native interpolation parameter, explicit occupants, quantitative screening map, and lattice/molecule discriminator remain unfinished.

## Unit boundary

CH5 owns:

1. joint shell-edge closure as the covalent limiting site;
2. transfer plus opposite charge-site closure as the ionic limiting site;
3. the proposed continuous apportionment variable between those limits;
4. screening texture as the conceptual reconstruction of electronegativity;
5. the qualitative directional-shell-site / isotropic-charge-site distinction.

CH5 does not own or derive:

- X1 scale separation, Born–Oppenheimer control, or the fixed-nuclei description;
- quantitative electronegativity values or Pauling-scale numbers;
- an exact transfer threshold or a proof that the continuum is exhaustive;
- Madelung constants, lattice energies, bond energies, dielectric response, polarization, or solvation;
- universal claims that ionic structures cannot be directional or that covalent structures cannot be extended;
- CH6 delocalization, recursion, aromaticity, or later chemistry.

## Structural reconstruction

### Covalent limiting site

For partners whose relevant screening textures are identical or sufficiently near, the selected candidate is one jointly held shell-edge completion. No separate charge edge is created in the exact symmetric limit. This inherits CH1 joint standing, CH2 composite ownership, and CH4 partner-level symmetry only at their recorded grades.

### Ionic limiting site

For sufficiently asymmetric partners, held apportionment approaches transfer to one center. The proposal has two stages:

1. shell-edge completion by transfer;
2. closure of the resulting opposite charge edges at the inherited charge site.

The second stage may spend P15 charge only at its recorded scope. P15 does not by itself prove ionic bonding, transfer, or a lattice.

### Continuum and site variable

The proposed covalent–ionic continuum is not a continuum between two substances. It is a continuum in where the closure-maintenance burden is carried: jointly at the shell-edge site or after transfer at the charge site.

No native scalar coordinate on this continuum has yet been constructed. “Percent ionic character,” dipole moment, and empirical electronegativity differences may test such a coordinate after construction; they cannot supply it.

### Electronegativity

Electronegativity is provisionally reconstructed as partner-relative screening texture evaluated at a shared standing. Difference in screening texture is proposed to bias held apportionment. The concept is **Conjectured-strong**; numerical scales, periodic values, environment dependence, oxidation-state dependence, and quantitative prediction are Open.

### Directionality and aggregation

The shell-edge site is proposed to preserve directional partner structure; the charge site is proposed to support more isotropic surround closure. This makes molecule-like counted composites and lattice-like packing a qualitative consequence candidate.

The tendency is not an absolute dichotomy. Mixed bonding, directional ionic interactions, extended covalent networks, and environment-dependent polarization prevent promotion of a universal lattice/molecule theorem.

## Adversarial distinctions

1. **Charge exists is not ionic bonding.** P15 charge does not construct transfer or paired charge-site closure.
2. **Joint standing exists is not covalent realization.** CH1 still lacks an established occupant.
3. **A continuum label is not an interpolation parameter.** The native coordinate and comparison rule remain owed.
4. **Screening is not an electronegativity table.** Conceptual reconstruction does not derive values.
5. **Directionality is not exclusivity.** The site distinction supports tendencies, not universal material classes.
6. **Mass separation is not CH5.** It remains owned by X1 as a chemistry-entry antecedent.

## Local research frontiers

### CH5-F1 — explicit two-site occupants

**Standing:** Open and Unregistered.

**Current strongest:** two limiting structural forms are stated.

**Missing:** one admitted joint-shell occupant and one admitted transfer-plus-charge-site occupant in a common framework representation.

**Dependencies:** X1, CH1-F2, CH3-F1, P15.

**False-completion warning:** familiar H2 and NaCl do not substitute for framework constructions.

**Next legitimate research action:** construct the two smallest finite comparison cases under one common state description.

### CH5-F2 — screening-to-apportionment map

**Standing:** Open and Unregistered.

**Current strongest:** screening-texture difference is the selected source of asymmetry.

**Missing:** a partner screening object, evaluation rule at the standing, monotone apportionment map, and sign theorem.

**Dependencies:** X1 atomic/shell work, CH2 ownership, CH3 functional.

**False-completion warning:** Pauling values cannot be reverse-installed as the native map.

**Next legitimate research action:** define a dimensionless two-partner screening contrast and test whether it orders apportionment.

### CH5-F3 — site character and aggregate form

**Standing:** Conjectured qualitatively; exact discriminator Open.

**Current strongest:** shell-edge closure is directional and charge-site closure is proposed as more isotropic.

**Missing:** aggregation rule, persistence criterion, mixed-site treatment, and proof of when counted molecules or extended lattices result.

**Dependencies:** CH4 geometry, CH5-F1, later recursion/aggregation units.

**False-completion warning:** empirical molecule/lattice vocabulary is not a theorem.

**Next legitimate research action:** prove one bounded aggregation consequence for each site without universalizing it.

## Downstream use rule

Later chemistry may use covalent and ionic as the two selected limiting site descriptions only with their Conjectured status attached. Electronegativity may be used only as the proposed screening-texture concept. No later unit may cite CH5 as deriving transfer thresholds, numerical electronegativity, lattice energies, bond polarity magnitudes, or an exhaustive binary classification.

## Bottom line

CH5 is the covalent–ionic keystone and electronegativity reconstruction. Its architecture is coherent and strongly specified, but the actual occupants, interpolation coordinate, screening map, and quantitative consequences remain unregistered.
