## Exact algebraic boundary

For a Hermitian pairing in the convention `h(y,x)=conj(h(x,y))`,

`Im h(y,x) = - Im h(x,y)`.

This establishes antisymmetry. It does not establish nonvanishing. In the complex carrier, choose nonzero real-aligned vectors `x=(1,0,0)` and `y=(2,0,0)` under the standard Hermitian pairing. Then `Im h(x,y)=0`. Thus complexness is compatible with zero antisymmetric value on a selected pair.

For a phase potential `psi` on vertices, the exact edge phase `phi(u,v)=psi(v)-psi(u)` has zero oriented sum around every closed loop. Therefore a selected loop with nonzero oriented phase sum would prove that selected `phi` is not exact. P26 has not constructed the required postcosm vertices, edge phases, carrier-to-edge map, or nonzero loop value. Antisymmetry of an abstract pairing is not that construction.
