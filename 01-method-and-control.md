# Method and Control

This document governs claim standing, domain entry, source authority, and execution discipline for the Triadic Closure corpus.

## Epistemic warrant and semantic registration

Use two independent axes.

**Framework epistemic state**

- **Open** — no framework warrant yet secures the exact claim.
- **Conjectured** — a serious proposal with stated support and unresolved debts.
- **Secured** — established within scope by a named warrant route and reach.

Every Secured claim states its scope, conditions, and debts.

**Semantic registration for inherited names**

- **Native** — framework-native concept; no inherited-name mapping is at issue.
- **Unregistered** — an inherited name is not yet licensed.
- **Registered** — an inherited name is licensed over an explicitly mapped, framework-secured core; all legacy surplus remains quarantined.
- **Registered–Sealed** — bidirectional content identity within a declared scope, with no legacy surplus or framework remainder.

Registration contributes no warrant. `Conjectured-strong` is warrant-strength metadata; `Registered-candidate` is workflow metadata that must be decomposed; `Defended posit` names a warrant profile; and `Dissolved` is a disposition.

`Locked actual` is deprecated. Use [claim-status-vocabulary](claim-status-vocabulary.md).

## Domain entry

Every domain must:

1. name the inherited whole;
2. identify its native constraint;
3. quarantine familiar vocabulary until rebuilt;
4. derive the minimal first content;
5. record every imported premise, normalization, empirical identification, and unresolved bridge.

Each mature domain is organized as: entry, D-series walk, deep dives, current standing, and open frontier.

## Execution

Work proceeds hinge-first. The next task is the unresolved premise or bridge carrying the greatest downstream load, not the most impressive downstream result.

Every document must distinguish structural derivation, rule-given closure, values-side identification, and recognition after the fact.

## Source authority

1. Canonical documents in this repository.
2. Current Google Drive control and result pages, interpreted through later audits.
3. Conversation ledgers and compiler instructions used for adjudication.
4. Raw, generated, archived, and superseded files used for provenance only.

## Editorial rule

Refactoring must preserve dependency routes, status distinctions, negative results, dissolved pseudo-problems, unresolved hinges, source lineage, and corrections to prior overstatement while consolidating duplicated material.
