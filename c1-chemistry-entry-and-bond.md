# C1 Chemistry Entry and the Bond as D1

C1 enters chemistry from the successor side. It asks what additional constraint selects chemistry within the inherited physical ground, whether that constraint is valid and nonempty, and what minimal content satisfies it.

## 1. Inherited ground

Chemistry inherits the relevant physical whole at its existing grades: persistent atomic and subatomic standings, charge and shell structure where established, exchange-mode and holding constraints, spatial structure where spent, and the P7 handoff. No physics result is re-proved merely because chemistry spends it.

The atomic and periodic-landscape parent remains partly unnormalized. Every C1 claim that spends shell structure, screening, or detailed atomic configuration therefore retains the owning parent conditions. Empirical atoms and bonds secure observational nonemptiness but do not erase framework-native parent debts.

## 2. Boundary correction

P7 proposed **shell-edge closure-completion** as chemistry's successor constraint. C1 confirms that this is a valid chemistry entry route, but rejects it as the exhaustive chemistry boundary.

Chemistry includes at least two exterior reconfiguration sites:

1. **shell-edge co-completion** — atomic standings jointly terminate residual shell capacity in one shared standing;
2. **charge-site completion after transfer** — redistribution changes the standing valence profile and the resulting oppositely conditioned standings close relationally.

The common essential form is therefore:

> **Chemistry is the domain of reconfiguration and joint closure of persistent atomic exterior relation-capacities, under preservation of nuclear identity.**

Here `exterior relation-capacities` includes shell-edge and charge-site structure without treating either as the whole domain. Preservation of nuclear identity distinguishes ordinary chemistry from nuclear transmutation; it is a boundary condition, not a claim that nuclei are chemically inert in every descriptive respect.

### Standing

- The broader chemistry boundary is **Secured conditionally by construction** from the inherited atomic ground, the two exhibited closure sites, and the ordinary-chemistry preservation condition.
- The boundary is **empirically nonempty**.
- **Chemistry** is **Registered, not Sealed** for this essential-form core. Mature chemistry surplus remains quarantined.

## 3. D1: the bond

The minimal nonzero chemistry content is a **bond**:

> one joint standing in which two or more atomic exterior relation-capacities are mutually terminated, redistributed, or held as one composite closure while the participating nuclear identities remain standing.

This definition does not posit a substance or stick between pre-complete atoms. The bond is the composite standing itself.

The definition is broad enough to include:

- covalent joint shell-edge holding;
- ionic relational closure following charge redistribution;
- partial and asymmetric sharing as interior texture of one standing;
- one-electron bonds such as `H2+`, because capacity and occupancy are distinct.

It does not yet derive metallic bonding, multicenter bonding, bond order, orbital hybridization, geometry, energy magnitude, or rate. Those require later chemistry units and their own inherited parents.

### Minimality and nonemptiness

A bare edge-bearing atom is inherited ground with an unresolved exterior capacity, not yet a chemistry D1 closure. A completely exterior-closed atom supplies the chemistry zero relative to the active boundary. The first nonzero content is a joint atomic standing satisfying the boundary. Bonded matter supplies empirical nonemptiness.

### Standing

- Bond as the minimal joint chemistry standing is **Secured conditionally** from the broader boundary and inherited atomic persistence.
- **Bond** is **Registered, not Sealed** at this essential-form scope.
- The covalent two-center bond is one D1 occupancy, not the definition of D1 as such.

## 4. Two-center results and their limit

For one shared spatial mode over two centers, with the inherited antisymmetric complete-state constraint:

- capacity is two;
- occupancy may be one or two;
- the two-center combination space has symmetric and antisymmetric modes.

These are conditional scoped results. They must not be generalized into claims that every bond contains exactly two occupants or exactly two modes. Multicenter standings have their own mode count and closure structure.

## 5. Ownership and apportionment

The bond belongs to the composite closure. Constituent assignment, polarity, and sharing degree describe the held interior texture of that closure. They may register real asymmetries, but they do not convert the bond into a substance owned in pieces by otherwise independent atoms.

This licenses covalent/ionic differentiation as real texture and passage history while rejecting the claim that they must be two ontologically unrelated bond kinds.

## 7. Frontiers

### C1-F1 — Atomic parent normalization

Normalize the atom and periodic landscape wherever C1 spends detailed shell or charge structure.

### C1-F2 — Boundary exhaustiveness

Adversarially test the broader boundary against metallic, multicenter, radical, weak-interaction, and excited-state chemistry. A counterexample should broaden or split the boundary rather than be excluded by familiarity.

### C1-F3 — D1 population

Survey the full population of minimal bond standings—covalent, ionic, multicenter, one-electron, and other candidates—without turning the first convenient example into the whole D1.

## 8. Use rule

Use this page as the canonical chemistry-entry and D1 control. Use P7 for predecessor-side handoff and the chemistry source deposit for later D2–D4 constructions. Do not cite a two-center covalent model as the definition of chemistry, and do not demand later quantitative chemistry before recognizing a bounded D1 standing.
