# With-to-This Closure as Realizability Collapse

How a previously-standing With (a holding among constituents) is closed over by a new This. The result addresses, within one tested baryon model, why a complete-closure configuration is selected rather than merely available, why the result is category-level, and why the modeled closure first appears at the closure-form's arity.

This is a later closure-mechanism result and a candidate consumer of [P6](d6-persistence.md). It does not govern the generic D6 phase-form and does not by itself establish physics D6 occupancy.

## The question

A standing With becomes a new This in the modeled baryon case: three quark-color factors, mutually conditioning through the selected color interaction, close into a color-neutral standing that may then be tested as a relatum for a later stratum.

The sharp question is how this is possible at all. In mathematics a holding closes by evaluation: given three vectors and the alternating form, the determinant is the value of the form on them. In physics the same closure-form is proposed, but the three color-moments are dynamic and re-selected each step. The closure therefore cannot be a one-time evaluation; it must be produced through the realizability-to-actuality structure.

## The mechanism

The field of mutual conditionings among the constituents is treated as a realizability field: the coherent constraint that standing actuals place on the next actual. Each modeled step produces a next state under the selected flow. The proposed selection interpretation uses coherence-participation weighting and therefore inherits every condition and debt of [Realizability Weighting Law](realizability-weighting-law.md), including the Open frequency bridge.

A configuration on which the closure-form is non-zero is a complete closure. Under the tested flows, the complete-closure singlet is maximally self-participating and re-selects itself as a fixed point; perturbations in the tested state space return to it. Within that model, **the new This is the tested attractor of the With-field**.

This wording is intentionally local. A fixed point is not an attractor without a specified perturbation and return dynamics, and an attractor under tested flows is not automatically persistent across the complete P6 variation-class.

### Evaluation versus collapse

The mathematical closure is read by evaluation. The physical model repeatedly re-selects the closure configuration. The proposed mass interpretation identifies the maintenance burden of such repeated self-closure with mass. That interpretation belongs to [Mass as Self-Closure](mass-as-self-closure.md); this page supplies no absolute scale and no P6 stable-ruler result.

### Why a whole category

The closure-condition is category-level rather than tied to three named color vectors. The modeled field selects the gauge-equivalence class of triad-spanning singlet configurations. What actualizes in the model is being-a-complete-closure as such, which supports treating the baryon as a candidate relatum-type for later work.

This does not establish every mature property of baryons or prove that the category persists under every physically relevant passage.

### Why at a certain level

The closure-form has an arity, and the complete-closure fork first appears when the field can span it. Below triality zero there is no singlet closure fork in the selected representation. At triality zero the fork appears and the tested flows reach it.

The stronger equation “confinement and baryon formation are one event” is licensed only for the modeled closure/non-closure mechanism. Universal QCD confinement dynamics, running, flux tubes, hadron spectrum, and every higher-body alternative remain with their owning units.

## Computation

Using the pairwise color-overlap operator `H = Σ_{i<j} T_i·T_j` on `C³ ⊗ C³ ⊗ C³`, the spectrum has eigenvalues `−2` (singlet), `−1/2` (octets), and `+1` (decuplet). Under the two representative binding-coherence and conditioning-degree flows reported in the source work, tested random starts flow to the singlet. Perturbations within those tested flows return to the singlet while octet and decuplet components drain toward it.

This supports the singlet as the unique tested attractor for the modeled operator and flows. It is finite/model-specific evidence, not a theorem over every admissible realizability dynamics.

## What this establishes

- A concrete With-to-This closure mechanism is exhibited for the modeled baryon case.
- The complete-closure singlet is the unique attractor under the reported tested flows.
- The closure is category-level and first available at the selected closure-form's arity.
- Attractor, rather than merely algebraic maximum, is the load-bearing characterization inside the tested model.
- The result supplies a candidate for later P6-F1 testing; it does not itself complete P6-F1.

## P6 boundary

To use this candidate as the basic persistent physical closure, a later research task must still:

1. state the baryon closure's identity-constituting invariant;
2. state the full relevant admitted D5 passage-class, not only the two tested flow families;
3. show preservation across that class;
4. separate structural persistence from perturbative stability;
5. supply any quantitative transition, frequency, or calibration premises before stating a decay rate or lifetime.

Until then, the local tested attractor result remains Registered at its model scope and physics D6 occupancy remains Open.

## Mass-ratio boundary

This result fixes a modeled closure configuration and its tested selection. It does not determine the magnitude of the resulting closure's self-bearing. That measure belongs to [Particle Mass from the Three Faces of the Closure](mass-derivation-three-faces.md).

`m_p / m_e = 6 pi^5 [1 + c(3 pi^4)^-2]`, with `3/2 <= c <= 9/4`.

The standalone `6 pi^5` is the exact With-This color-seating factor within the ratio, not the full ratio itself.
