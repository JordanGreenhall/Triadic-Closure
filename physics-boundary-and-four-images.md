## Boundary participation

The selected rule is not a borrowed definition of traditional physics. It asks whether a mathematical candidate participates in all three ways:

- **From — self-transforming:** the candidate bears an internal source or rule of change rather than being moved only by an external background;
- **With — coherent:** its moments are held in a mutually constraining relation;
- **This — invariant:** a determinate closure remains identifiable across the transformation.

Passing this three-part test is necessary under the selected rule, not sufficient for physical occupancy. P1 still requires a construction showing that the candidate actually participates in the physics lineage.
