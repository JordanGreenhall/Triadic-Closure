## Boundary participation

The selected rule is not a borrowed definition of traditional physics. It asks whether a mathematical candidate participates in all three ways:

- **From — self-transforming:** the candidate bears an internal source or rule of change rather than being moved only by an external background;
- **With — coherent:** its moments are held in a mutually constraining relation;
- **This — invariant:** a determinate closure remains identifiable across the transformation.

Passing this three-part test is exactly membership in the selected physics domain. P1 audits the scope and grade of that claim; it does not add a second participation or occupancy test.

The F3 office genealogy, the marked/flattened basis distinction, and the four images' conditional co-presence are framework remainder rather than ordinary semantic content of any one registered name. That remainder, together with the quarantined legacy surplus, is why none of the four registrations is sealed.

The exact `3+1` and Lorentzian-signature claims belong to P4. Boson/fermion and holding statistics belong to [P3](boson-fermion-and-holding-statistics.md). Propagation and invariant velocity belong to P5. Persistence belongs to P6. Continuum and manifold recovery belong to P7. None is spent to make P2 pass.
