# CH4 — Ideal Local Molecular Geometry

## Standing summary

CH4 owns the repaired local-geometry claim for multiple partners around one center. The governing claim is not that internal orbital labels force tetrahedral carbon. That route is retracted. The retained claim is partner-level and conditional:

> For `N=2,3,4` registrably identical partners represented by directions from one center in three isotropic spatial degrees, if the selected rule is maximal mutual separation, the ideal configurations are linear, trigonal planar, and tetrahedral.

The corresponding pair angles are:

- `N=2`: `180°`;
- `N=3`: `120°`;
- `N=4`: `arccos(-1/3) ≈ 109.47°`.

The finite geometry result is **Secured conditionally** on the stated representation and maximal-separation premise. The framework claim that chemical closure selects maximal mutual separation is **Conjectured and Unregistered**. No-privileged-frame supplies equal standing among identical partners; it does not by itself supply the optimizing functional.

## Unit boundary

CH4 owns only:

1. the repaired license for no-privileged-frame at the partner level;
2. the exact ideal configurations for two, three, and four identical partner directions under maximal separation;
3. the distinction between an exact conditional spherical configuration and its physical selection;
4. the bounded near-family status of mixed-partner and lone-pair deviations.

CH4 excludes:

- orbital hybridization as a derived mechanism;
- mixed-partner angle magnitudes;
- lone-pair repulsion magnitudes or ordering;
- quantitative distortion energies;
- extended molecular conformations;
- covalent/ionic structure, electronegativity, recursion, aromaticity, chirality, reactions, kinetics, thermodynamics, and catalysis;
- CH5 and later chemistry.

## Authority and repair

The earlier route from `2s²2p²` to four equivalent edges was explicitly retracted because no-privileged-frame cannot erase registered differences among unlike internal labels. The repaired source moves the symmetry license to the partner positions: when the partners are registrably identical, none may be privileged as a partner.

That repair establishes a symmetry condition, not a physical objective. A unique ideal family requires the additional rule that the directions maximize mutual separation. CH3 does not currently supply a native maintenance functional proving that rule. Therefore the exact configurations and the physical selection must remain separately graded.

## Exact conditional configurations

Let `u_i` be unit directions from one center in Euclidean three-space.

### Two partners

Maximal separation gives `u_1·u_2=-1`, hence antipodal directions and angle `180°`.

### Three partners

The equal-separation optimum satisfies `u_i·u_j=-1/2` for `i≠j`, giving three coplanar directions separated by `120°`.

### Four partners

The equal-separation optimum satisfies `u_i·u_j=-1/3` for `i≠j`, giving the vertices of a regular tetrahedron as seen from its center and angle `arccos(-1/3) ≈ 109.47°`.

These are exact finite geometry statements. They do not determine bond lengths, energies, occupancies, or whether an actual chemical system realizes the premises.

## Adversarial distinctions

1. **Equal standing is not optimization.** No-privileged-frame forbids arbitrary partner privilege; it does not choose a unique arrangement without a selection rule.
2. **Conditional geometry is not chemical realization.** The exact spherical configurations do not prove an actual molecule satisfies the assumptions.
3. **Partner identity is not internal orbital identity.** The retracted `2s²2p² → four equivalent edges` route may not be revived.
4. **Ideal family is not universal geometry.** Mixed partners, lone pairs, multiple bonds, and environmental constraints lie outside the exact result.
5. **Angle is not energy.** CH4 does not derive a distortion potential or bond-angle stiffness.
6. **Geometry is not hybridization.** Textbook orbital labels may describe a realized geometry but are not derived here.
