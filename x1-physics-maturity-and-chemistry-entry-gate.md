# X1 — Physics Maturity and Chemistry Entry Gate

## Standing summary

X1 is the canonical owner for the physics-to-chemistry interface: the inherited physics ground, the proposed shell-edge successor constraint, the scale-separation condition for ordinary chemistry, and the proposed D7 provisioner. Its positive result is an authority-bounded **interface adjudication**, not a completed chemistry entry theorem.

The generic F4 handoff form is Secured: a D6-persisting ground may provision a successor when a determinate constraint is valid and nonempty in that ground; the valid nonempty constraint is the boundary, and its extension is successor D1. X1 does not presently exhibit the domain-specific premises needed to instantiate that form for chemistry.

Accordingly:

- bounded electron, proton, neutron, baryon, charge/valence, mass, and spatial structures retain their owning physics grades;
- a framework-native persistent atom, electronic shell structure, periodic landscape, and shell-edge object are **Open and Unregistered**;
- shell-edge closure-completion is a **candidate constraint, Conjectured and Unregistered**; it is not yet a valid nonempty chemistry boundary;
- the P19 proton/electron ratio supplies a static internal load hierarchy, not a dynamical slow/fast theorem, adiabatic separation, or Born–Oppenheimer construction;
- the ordinary-chemistry no-transmutation scope is a selected classification boundary, not a framework theorem until a passage class and scale criterion are constructed;
- carbon as the physics D7 provisioner is **Conjectured and Unregistered**; the candidate route depends on atomic-shell work and on chemistry's later catenation construction;
- the universal claim that every successor inhabits predecessor residue and that D7 maximizes successor recursion is **Conjectured and Unregistered** from one proposed case.

X1 itself is **Secured by authority and grade audit** and **Registered only as a control boundary**.

## 2. Atomic and periodic ground adjudication

The candidate handoff source presupposed a physics D6 object described as an atom plus periodic landscape. Current physics does not contain that result at wiki-standing grade.

A promotable atomic ground would require at minimum:

1. a framework-defined composite carrier or state category containing licensed nuclear and electron roles;
2. the exact holding relation by which the composite stands as one closure;
3. an invariant identifying the atom across an admitted passage class;
4. an electronic variation-space and the operations admitted in it;
5. a shell or equivalent decomposition constructed from those objects;
6. occupancy consequences at their actual P3/P12 scopes;
7. persistence or stability evidence for at least one admitted atomic standing;
8. a route from individual standings to a periodic organization if that organization is spent.

None is presently complete as one closed construction. Empirical atoms and the periodic table may orient the work or test a completed construction; they do not supply framework warrant.

The prior complete-return, fixed-point, `1/r`, Rydberg, screening, and Madelung routes are therefore candidate research material. X1 records their dependency location but does not adjudicate them upward or perform the missing physics.

## 3. Candidate shell-edge constraint

F4 requires a domain-specific entry record with standing ground, determinate candidate constraint, validity, and nonemptiness.

### Standing ground

The proposed ground is a persistent atomic totality with electronic configuration structure. That ground is **Open and Unregistered** as a completed framework object.

### Candidate constraint

The strongest current proposal is:

> admit persistent physical configurations whose residual electronic-shell relation can be jointly completed without changing the nuclear role identity of the participating standings.

This is a candidate chemistry admissibility constraint stated as closely as possible in current framework terms. “Shell,” “residual,” “jointly completed,” and “nuclear role identity” still depend on constructions not yet jointly present.

### Validity

Validity is **Conjectured and Unregistered**. P15 signed valence establishes an outward/inward relational orientation at bounded scope, but signed charge-valence is not itself a shell-edge. P17 maintenance language does not prove that satisfying a candidate shell relation lowers intrinsic load. The P20 valence-to-maintenance sign bridge remains unresolved and cannot be silently spent as chemical binding.

### Nonemptiness

Nonemptiness is **Open and Unregistered** as a framework construction. Empirical molecules show that familiar chemistry has occupants; they do not exhibit a framework-native atomic ground, shell edge, or admitted joint closure. CH1 must construct at least one bond candidate only after X1 supplies a usable ground and constraint.

### Boundary and D1 verdict

Because validity and nonemptiness are not established, shell-edge closure-completion is **not yet a chemistry boundary**, and its proposed extension is not yet framework D1. X1 does not manufacture an additional gate event; it records that the antecedent F4 conditions have not yet been met.

This is not a demand for a second kind of occupant. It is the ordinary F4 requirement that the proposed rule be valid and nonempty in the actual standing ground.

## 4. Scale separation and ordinary-chemistry scope

The P19 internal ratio gives a heavy proton-role relative to the electron ruler within the registered construction. It can support a qualitative expectation that nuclear composites and electron standings respond differently once a common dynamics is supplied.

It does not determine:

- an update or evolution law for an atom;
- nuclear and electronic response frequencies;
- an adiabatic parameter;
- a Born–Oppenheimer approximation;
- error bounds or a regime of validity;
- the energy threshold separating chemical from nuclear passages.

The ordinary-chemistry convention—electronic rearrangement while nuclear/element role identity is preserved—is a useful selected scope rule. To become a framework domain boundary it requires an admitted passage class, a criterion for nuclear-role preservation, a scale or structural exclusion rule for transmutation, and at least one constructed admitted process. Until then, qualitative mass separation is **Conjectured** and quantitative time-scale separation is **Open and Unregistered**.
