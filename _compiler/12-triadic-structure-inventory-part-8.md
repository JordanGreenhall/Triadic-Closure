## TSR-0801 — A domain opens through gate, boundary, and bootstrap

**Kind:** domain-entry structure

**Content:** A domain comes to be when a gate crosses from standing ground to a new With whose determinate content is a boundary. The gate is the From bridging from what stands to the new admissibility condition. The boundary is the With’s inclusion/exclusion rule. The domain is what lies within that boundary and unfolds under it.

**Parents:** closure; From; With as holding-on-a-basis; constraint-selects; modal engine.

**Spent by:** every successor-domain walk.

**Standing:** forced by the gate/boundary/With distinction.

---

## TSR-0802 — Domain bootstrap recapitulates primordial form under a new boundary

**Kind:** domain bootstrap rule

**Content:** A newly opened domain may possess a boundary before it contains internal actuals. Its first unfolding runs the triad under boundary-plus-triad. This repeats the primordial unfolding in form, not content: different boundaries produce different first actuals.

**Parents:** TSR-0801; primordial unfolding; invariance of triadic form across strides.

**Spent by:** D1 of every domain.

**Standing:** forced.

## TSR-0811 — Dependency, form, and occupancy must remain distinct

**Kind:** categorical distinction

**Content:** Forced dependency means a later phase cannot be occupied without the earlier phases it spends. Forced form means that, if a phase occurs, its shape is constrained. Forced occupancy would mean the phase must occur. The D-series claims the first two and denies the third.

**Parents:** TSR-0803 through TSR-0810.

**Spent by:** every domain status ledger.

**Standing:** forced control distinction.

## TSR-0814 — The phase dependencies are strict

**Kind:** dependency theorem

**Content:** D2 requires D1 because holdings require standings. D3 requires D2 because turn-over takes an actual holding. D4 requires D3 because invariance requires passage across a lineage-bearing build. D5 requires D4 for registered invariant-content. **Standing:** forced dependency trace.

## TSR-0817 — Occupancy is established by construction and backward trace

**Kind:** verification method

**Content:** To show that a domain occupies several phases, exhibit an object carrying the relevant structures and decompose it backward, verifying that each phase-form is present in dependency order and that no later content was imported. A later object is a site of verification, not a license to skip the trace.

**Parents:** architectonic construction; dependency discipline; phase-schema.

**Spent by:** all concrete domain occupancy claims.

**Standing:** forced method for sufficiently rich domains.

**Content:** A proposed phase walk fails by adjacency failure if a required intermediate is missing; by import failure if a phase spends unearned content; or by arbitrariness failure if competent independent operators cannot materially reproduce the walk from the same priors.

## Dependency and preservation effects

1. No domain may be reported as occupying a phase merely because the phase-form is available generically.
2. No phase may be promoted from specimen-level occurrence to domain-wide occupancy without the pacing rule of TSR-0822.
3. D7 must remain a selected successor-relative handoff location; it is not a universal terminus or intrinsic ranking.
4. Terms such as symmetry, group, metric, geometry, conservation, time, and endurance remain sealed where the phase walk has earned only a more primitive form.
5. The seven-phase schema must never be represented as a complete inventory of all possible phases.
6. All concrete occupancy claims require construction and backward trace under TSR-0817.
