## Preservation rule

This source governs entry into successor domains. It is compiled only for living method. Examples specific to physics are preserved as examples, not promoted into universal primitives.

---

## DEM-001 — Domain entry must not borrow the successor domain's unconscious metaphysics

**Kind:** governing method

**Content:** Triadic Closure enters a successor domain from the inherited foundational and mathematical object without treating that domain's unexamined presuppositions as load-bearing.

**Parents:** Architectonic Rigor; Triadic Structure of Relating.

**Spent by:** every successor-domain reconstruction.

**Standing:** current governing method.

---

## DEM-002 — Domain reconstruction begins by clarifying presuppositions architectonically

**Kind:** execution rule

**Content:** Before reconstructing a domain, identify and clarify the concepts the domain ordinarily presupposes. In physics these include metric, causation, individuation, possibility, actuality, particle, field, space, time, and force.

**Parents:** DEM-001.

**Spent by:** domain-specific entry audits and smuggle detection.

**Standing:** current governing method.

---

## DEM-003 — Apparent inherited problems are tested for dissolution, not accepted on their inherited terms

**Kind:** methodological criterion

**Content:** The purpose of clarification is not automatically to solve a domain's inherited problem inside its inherited conceptual frame, but to determine whether rebuilding the presupposition from the framework dissolves the problem.

**Parents:** DEM-001, DEM-002.

**Spent by:** phantom-problem diagnosis and domain-entry validation.

**Standing:** current governing method.

---

## DEM-004 — Domain entry begins by naming the inherited presupposition and its smuggle

**Kind:** procedure step

**Content:** Identify the domain's ordinary version of the relevant presupposition and make explicit the extra load it imports.

**Parents:** DEM-002.

**Spent by:** DEM-005 and every concrete domain-entry audit.

**Standing:** current procedure.

## DEM-005 — The inherited version is refused as load-bearing

## DEM-010 — The From of a successor domain is the full prior object inherited whole

**Kind:** inheritance invariant

**Content:** A successor domain receives the complete prior closure as its From. The prior object is not a collection of optional features from which the successor may select only convenient handles.

**Parents:** Triadic Structure of Relating; closure inheritance.

**Spent by:** every domain walk and every test of whether a successor-domain step has ignored an inherited characteristic.

**Standing:** current governing method.

## DEM-012 — A successor domain is selected by its own domain constraint

**Kind:** domain-selection rule

**Content:** The inherited prior object does not by itself determine which successor domain is entered. The successor domain is selected by the specific constraint under which the inherited object is read and developed.

**Parents:** constraint selects rather than adds; DEM-010.

**Spent by:** gate specification and domain identity.

**Standing:** current governing method.

## Dependency and preservation effects

1. Domain-entry method depends on Architectonic Rigor and the foundational structure of relating.
2. The general method must remain distinct from its physics worked example.
3. No successor domain may inherit only a selected subset of the prior closure without explicit justification.
4. A domain is identified by its selecting constraint, not by importing the mature vocabulary of the target discipline.
5. Downstream domain documents must preserve the sequence: expose smuggle → refuse inherited load → rebuild → concretize → test dissolution → deploy.
