# Domain Entry Method — Adjudicated Semantic Inventory

Status: corpus-wide adjudicated compiler artifact. Checked against `entering-a-new-domain.md`, the later compact domain-entry page, the physics entry, Standard Model smuggle audits, GR/QM work, chemistry entry, and downstream domain walks.

## DEM-001 — Do not borrow the target domain's unexamined metaphysics

A successor-domain walk must begin from the inherited framework rather than allowing the target discipline's ordinary ontology to determine the reconstruction.

**Disposition:** retained.

## DEM-002 — Clarify the target domain's presuppositions before using them

Identify the concepts the target domain operates with but ordinarily leaves architectonically unexamined. The list is domain-specific; physics examples do not define the universal list.

**Disposition:** retained and scope-clarified.

## DEM-003 — Test inherited problems for dissolution

Reconstruction must ask whether a traditional problem survives once its presuppositions are rebuilt. Some inherited problems dissolve; others remain as genuine framework-native work.

**Disposition:** corrected. The earlier inventory could be read as presuming dissolution.

## DEM-004 — Identify the inherited concept and its surplus load

State the ordinary domain concept precisely and identify what it imports beyond what has been earned.

**Disposition:** retained.

## DEM-005 — Refuse only the unearned load

The target discipline's formulation is not rejected wholesale. What is refused is its use as an unexamined load-bearing premise. Empirical content, successful formal relations, and later recognitions may remain available after their status is separately earned.

**Disposition:** corrected. The former wording “the inherited version is refused” was too total.

## DEM-006 — Rebuild from the full inherited framework

Use the prior closure whole and all relevant registered characteristics, not a hand-picked toolkit. From/With/This, the modal triad, quantity/quality, J, rho, registration, and other developed structures are available insofar as they have already been earned.

**Disposition:** retained and strengthened against selective inheritance.

## DEM-007 — Test the reconstruction in concrete cases

A reconstruction must cash out in at least one concrete domain instance sufficient to expose hidden smuggles, ambiguity, or failure. This is a validation demand, not a requirement that every semantic statement contain an example.

**Disposition:** retained with scope clarification.

## DEM-008 — Validate by dissolution, reconstruction, and explanatory performance

A reconstructed concept is successful when it removes imported pseudo-problems where appropriate, preserves genuine problems in framework-native form, and supports concrete downstream work without reintroducing the refused assumptions.

**Disposition:** substantively repaired. Dissolution alone is not the universal acceptance criterion.

## DEM-009 — A validated reconstruction governs downstream use within its scope

Once adjudicated, the framework-native concept replaces the unexamined imported formulation for the claims it actually resolves. It remains defeasible under later detailed repair.

**Disposition:** retained with claim-sensitive scope.

## DEM-010 — The From of a successor domain is the prior closure inherited whole

A successor domain receives the developed prior object as its From. It does not begin from isolated handles selected for convenience.

**Disposition:** retained.

## DEM-011 — Every live characteristic of the prior object constrains the walk

All living, relevant characteristics of the inherited whole remain constraints unless an explicit dependency or scope analysis shows that a characteristic is inapplicable. Superseded or merely historical characteristics are not inherited as live constraints.

**Disposition:** corrected by adding the living/relevance qualification.

## DEM-012 — The domain constraint selects the successor domain

The inherited whole does not uniquely determine the next domain. A domain is the closure produced when that whole is read under a specific selecting constraint.

**Disposition:** retained.

## DEM-013 — Physics is one worked domain entry

Physics is entered under the constraint that the inherited mathematical object is self-transforming, invariant, and coherent. The From/With/This readings of causation are internal articulations of that physics entry, not universal primitives for all domains.

**Disposition:** retained.

## DEM-014 — A domain is a closure, not merely a procedure or subject label

The domain constraint closes a territory of the horizontal build into a determinate domain. This is the same foundational sense of closure used elsewhere, applied at domain scope.

**Disposition:** added from later clarification; prevents a false structural/procedural duality of closure.

## DEM-015 — Domain entry and corpus adjudication are distinct operations

Domain entry reconstructs a target discipline from inherited framework resources. Corpus adjudication determines which version of those resources is currently living. A domain walk may not rely on an early framework formulation without first resolving later repairs.

**Disposition:** added because the original compiler conflated correct method extraction with current semantic authority.

- DEM-014 and DEM-015: added from later corpus development and the compiler failure analysis.
