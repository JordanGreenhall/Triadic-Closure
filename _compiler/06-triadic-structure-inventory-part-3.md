## TS-035 — The offices read modally as admissible, realizable, and actual

**Content:** From corresponds to the admissible field opened by a constraint; With corresponds to the realizable field given all that currently stands; This corresponds to the arrived actual. The actual is nested within the realizable, and the realizable within the admissible. A legitimate move travels inward through these registers and selects from what is realizable here rather than leaping from possibility-at-large into actuality.

**Source:** §3.1.

**Parents:** TS-012 constraint-selects; TS-014 gerund principle; TS-017 From; TS-020 With; TS-024 This.

**Spent by:** state and adjacency; anti-smuggling rule; stride; admissibility unlock; domain-entry method.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-036 — Possible, realizable, and actual Withs must be distinguished

**Content:** A possible With is an admissible candidate basis or field not yet admitted by the present standing field. A realizable With is admitted by the current realizability-With and is adjacent. An actual With has a fixed basis and can include and exclude. The same candidate content may be read as a realizable This or realizable With depending on whether it is taken as an arrived expression or as field-structure.

**Source:** §3.2.

**Parents:** TS-020 With; TS-035 modal registers.

**Spent by:** realizability as actual With; domain bootstrap; field re-texturing.

**Standing:** Forced bookkeeping.

## TS-037 — Realizability relative to a standing state is itself an actual With

**Content:** For a standing state S, Realizability(S) is the actual holding-field whose rule includes what stands in S and what is reachable from S, while excluding what is not reachable from here. It is neither admissibility under another name nor the sum of actuals. It is structured coherence as a field of potential actuality conditioned by S and is re-read after every determination.

**Source:** §3.3.

**Parents:** TS-020 With; TS-035; TS-036.

**Spent by:** adjacency; stride; primordial second standing; double output; D5 variation-space.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-038 — A state includes both standing actuals and the field they open

**Content:** A state S is the standing ground of determined actuals together with the field those actuals open. A closure opens an admissible field; S constrains it to what is realizable; selection actualizes one realizable region; the new actual changes S and requires the field to be re-read.

**Source:** §3.4.

**Parents:** TS-035; TS-037; closure units from Part 2.

**Spent by:** adjacency, stride, all later domain walks.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-039 — Adjacency means realizable-from-S and not already actual

**Content:** A region is adjacent to S exactly when it is realizable from S and not already actual in S. “One move away” is not metric distance and not a countable step unless later earned. The working partition is: standing actuals; adjacent-proximal material; distant possibles.

**Source:** §3.4.

**Parents:** TS-035; TS-037; TS-038.

**Spent by:** validity of moves; D-series adjacency tests; open joint on realizable-from as primitive.

**Standing:** Forced by modal nesting.

## TS-040 — The smuggle presents merely admissible content as realizable here

**Content:** A smuggle occurs when surplus from an analogy, borrowed name, named discipline, or later construction is used as though the current state had generated it. The fault is not borrowing language as such; it is allowing unearned surplus to do work.

**Source:** §3.5.

**Parents:** TS-012; TS-035; TS-039.

**Spent by:** all construction audits; sealed recognition; domain-walk falsification surfaces.

**Standing:** Forced as movement discipline.

## TS-041 — A valid selection must satisfy three conditions

**Content:** A valid move must be a From/With/This act rather than a fourth kind of operation; the selected region must be admissible under the relevant boundary; and it must be realizable from the current actual, hence adjacent rather than merely somewhere in the unbounded field. The third condition is the decisive gate.

**Source:** §3.5.

**Parents:** TS-035; TS-039; TS-040; reflexive method-office check from Part 1.

**Spent by:** every domain stride; D-series walk; anti-smuggling compiler checks.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-042 — Actuals condition realizability rather than selecting from possibility-at-large

**Content:** Once a sequence or other standing state exists, the next determination is selected from Realizability(S), not from the unrestricted admissible field. The linguistic and LLM examples are recognitions of this structure and do not force it.

**Source:** §3.5.

**Parents:** TS-037; TS-038; TS-041.

**Spent by:** sequence-conditioned examples; successor-domain walk discipline.

## TS-043 — A stride is one completed determination, not a separate architecture

**Content:** A stride is the procedural form in which a standing whole conditions a move, the field re-holds what now stands, and a new actual arrives. In shorthand: This is handed forward as From; With re-textures the field; a new This stands. Older stride terminology is retained only in this procedural sense.

**Source:** §3.6.

**Parents:** TS-024 This; TS-037; TS-038; TS-041.

**Spent by:** primordial unfolding; domain walks; horizontal build history.

**Standing:** Retained procedural language.

---

## TS-044 — Every stride has a standing reading and a building reading

**Content:** Every stride is a horizontal event. Its standing reading checks the new actual against the admissibility cascade and records that nothing foreign was added. Its building reading records that the actual has changed S and re-textured the realizability-field. These are two readings of one event, not two further dimensions.

**Source:** §3.6.

**Parents:** TS-030 vertical/horizontal distinction; TS-043.

**Spent by:** symmetry of form and asymmetry of history; emergence analysis; axis mapping.

**Standing:** Forced within the two-dimension canon.

## TS-045 — Admissibility is field-grade; actualization is per-stride

**Content:** Actualization is expensive and walked one determination at a time. Admissibility is opened by a constraint over an entire region and does not require per-element actualization. A construction program that actualizes everything it uses confuses the ledgers.

**Source:** §3.7.

**Parents:** TS-012; TS-035; TS-043.

**Spent by:** mathematical-domain economy; integer and continuum unlocks; successor-domain construction.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-046 — The admissibility unlock certifies a region once

**Content:** A structural region stands at admissibility grade when its generating keystone has been actualized by an exhibited walk, its closure rules are admissible passages under the standing boundary, and its generation spends no excluded content. Thereafter particular objects are actualized on demand by finite pulls of certified moves; the region is not re-audited element by element.

**Source:** §3.7.

**Parents:** TS-041; TS-045; architectonic construction discipline.

**Spent by:** mathematics walk; integer, rational, and rule-given continuum regions.

**Standing:** Forced as application of construction discipline at region grade.

## TS-047 — Determinate content is the traceable content a closure can contribute onward

**Content:** Determinate content is the bounded specificity, composition, composability, source-conditioning, and field-conditioning fixed in an actual closure and therefore available to further determination. It is neither substance nor arbitrary descriptive richness. Actualization gathers form-with-field, and that gathered content can condition the next From.

**Source:** §4.1.

**Parents:** TS-024 This and its four faces; TS-037; closure units from Part 2.

**Spent by:** primordial first content; facetness; trace-back; domain construction.

**Standing:** Forced clarification.

## TS-048 — With is structured coherence as potential actuality

**Content:** “Content-potential” names the real possibility that something can become actual under structured coherence; it does not name a hidden indeterminate object. At the origin, From contributes determination, With contributes Realizability as potential actuality, and This is their arrival as determinate content. Later Realizability(S) is textured by actuals but is not reducible to them.

**Source:** §4.1.

**Parents:** TS-017 From; TS-020 With; TS-024 This; TS-035; TS-037.

**Spent by:** primordial unfolding, first actual With, later field re-texturing.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-049 — Facetness is the bounded incompatibility of an actual

**Content:** An actual has edges: some predicates, qualities, relations, and continuations fit it and others do not. This bounded incompatibility makes determinate content unfabricable. Facetness does not imply poverty; an actual may sustain unboundedly many true features, but each used feature must fit and trace to the actual.

**Source:** §4.2.

**Parents:** TS-047; determinateness face of This.

**Spent by:** universal under-registration of bases; anti-smuggling; mathematical gate seam.

**Standing:** Forced by determinateness.

## TS-050 — Trace-back is the test for legitimate use of a feature

**Content:** A proposed feature is legitimate when its dependency can be exhibited from the constitutive fact of the construction. A feature may do argumentative work only after it has been generated, selected under a boundary that admits it, or recognized with its surplus sealed. Failure of trace-back is a smuggle.

**Source:** §4.3.

**Parents:** architectonic dependency and construction; TS-040; TS-047; TS-049.

**Spent by:** every semantic inventory; domain-phase audits; migration verification.

**Standing:** Forced methodological rule.

## TS-051 — Sealed recognition permits names only after structure is earned

**Content:** The earned structure must be articulated in native terms before a familiar disciplinary name is attached. The name carries no load-bearing content beyond what has already been generated. The local question is always which aspects of the borrowed term have been earned and which surplus remains sealed.

**Source:** §4.4.

**Parents:** architectonic sealed recognition; TS-040; TS-050.

**Spent by:** all mathematical and physical vocabulary; later recognitions of space, topology, logic, gauge groups, and related structures.

## TS-052 — Actualization gathers form-with-field rather than merely instantiating form

**Content:** The chess-knight example exhibits that an actual standing carries its type together with its position, relation to the standing field, and effects on Realizability(S). Only content traceable to the relevant rule-field and actual position is legitimate in that domain; physical, market, or sentimental surplus remains sealed unless another boundary admits it.

**Source:** §4.5.

## Preservation obligations opened by Part 3

1. Do not collapse admissibility, realizability, and actuality into generic possibility language.
2. Preserve Realizability(S) as an actual With, not as a mere set of options or a sum of actuals.
3. Preserve adjacency as realizable-from-S and explicitly non-metric unless a later domain earns metric structure.
4. Preserve the three-part validity rule, especially the distinction between admissible somewhere and realizable here.
5. Preserve stride as procedural language over the existing architecture, not as a rival architecture.
6. Preserve standing/building as readings of one horizontal event, not additional dimensions.
7. Preserve the admissibility unlock and its three conditions; it governs the economy of later domain construction.
8. Preserve determinate content as form-with-field and the full five-part trace: bounded determinateness, composition, composability, source-conditioning, and field-conditioning.
9. Preserve facetness, trace-back, and sealed recognition as distinct controls.
10. Recognition examples must never be allowed to inherit the standing of the framework claims they illustrate.
