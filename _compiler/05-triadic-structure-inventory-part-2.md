## TS-012 — The noise/sound/grammar sequence is an illustration, not a proof

**Kind:** recognition example

**Content:** Noise, sound, articulated sound, and grammar illustrate successive constraint as narrowing. Grammar does not add new sound; it selects arrangements satisfying the grammar. The example carries no forcing load.

**Source:** §1.3.

**Parents:** TS-011 (`constraint does not add; it selects`).

**Spent by:** exposition of constraint-narrowing.

**Standing:** Recognition.

---

## TS-013 — The offices must be read gerundively

**Kind:** foundational interpretive rule

**Content:** From, With, and This are offices of an act, not static entities in a stock. They are to be read as conditioning-from, holding-with, and arriving-as-this. Treating an office as a thing introduces a substrate behind the act.

**Source:** §2.1.

**Parents:** TS-004 through TS-007.

**Spent by:** all office definitions, vertical composition, closure, domain walks.

**Relations:** the primitive is verbal; the offices are articulations of relating itself.

**Standing:** Forced within the source argument.

---

## TS-014 — Closure is the triadic act standing as one

**Kind:** foundational ontological definition

**Content:** The triad is one act with three irreducible offices. Closure is that act standing as one. The offices are not relata assembled into a relation.

**Source:** §2.1 and §2.7.

**Parents:** TS-005, TS-006, TS-013.

**Spent by:** all claims concerning actuals, standings, units, domains, atoms, and higher closures.

**Relations:** closure carries the legitimate work formerly assigned to substance without existential independence.

**Standing:** Forced by the closure-form.

---

## TS-015 — Each office is vertically composed of the other two

**Kind:** foundational structural claim

**Content:** From is the relating of With and This; With is the relating of From and This; This is the relating of From and With. Each office takes the other two as relata and is their relation.

**Source:** §2.2.

**Parents:** TS-005, TS-006, TS-013.

**Spent by:** vertical constitution, first determinate content, the two-dimensional canon, domain transpositions.

**Relations:** this is composition at full strength, not metaphor or mutual implication.

**Standing:** Forced by the gerund principle plus non-compositeness.

---

## TS-016 — Vertical composition is mutual and baseless

**Kind:** structural clarification

**Content:** No office is made from prior entities or parts. Each office is composed of the other two, which are themselves composed in the same way, without a non-relational base. The regress is not vicious; it is the form of the act.

**Source:** §2.2.

**Parents:** TS-015 and the defended posit that nothing stands beneath relating.

**Spent by:** coinductive recognition, composed-all-the-way-down claims, distinction between vertical and horizontal composition.

**Standing:** Forced within the source route; later coinductive vocabulary is sealed recognition.

---

## TS-017 — Vertical composition is invariant across every closure

**Kind:** cross-scale invariant

**Content:** Every From, With, and This at every stage is composed through the same office relation. Vertical composition does not accrue, vary, or acquire a history. The first This and every later This are vertically composed in the same sense.

**Source:** §2.2.

**Parents:** TS-015, TS-016.

**Spent by:** primordial construction, domain inheritance, distinction between office-constitution and layer nesting.

**Standing:** Forced.

---

## TS-018 — From is determinate distinction and conditioning

**Kind:** office articulation

**Content:** From marks a difference, holds the distinguished sides as distinguished, and conditions what may follow. It contributes determinative cut, position, and direction, not material content.

**Source:** §2.3.

**Parents:** TS-013, TS-015.

**Spent by:** polarity, pairing, lineage, gate formation, domain boundary cuts.

**Standing:** Forced office articulation.

## TS-019 — From is one-way and contentless

**Kind:** office property

**Content:** A cut conditions from one side toward another and has no inverse built into it. If From carried content of its own, it would be another standing thing rather than the conditioning office.

**Source:** §2.3.

**Parents:** TS-018.

**Spent by:** non-commutative lineage, directionality, successor conditioning, orientation.

**Standing:** Forced.

---

## TS-020 — Polarity and non-degenerate pairing are two faces of From

**Kind:** generated structural consequence

**Content:** From's distinguishing face yields side and complement; its holding-in-relationship face yields non-degenerate pairing. A side is a side only against what it is not. These are not separate inventions.

**Source:** §2.3.

**Parents:** TS-018, TS-019.

**Spent by:** admissibility-axis polarity, mathematical J, pairing structures, later physics inheritance.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-021 — From has two native directions

**Kind:** office distinction

**Content:** From conditions both a successor and a medium, but these are different relations. From→This is lineage or parental conditioning of an arrived determinate. From→With is coherence-conditioning of the field in which realization can occur.

**Source:** §2.4.

**Parents:** TS-018 through TS-020.

**Spent by:** domain handoff, seed/soil fit, field conditioning, closure succession.

**Standing:** Forced distinction.

## TS-022 — From→With is relative medium-conditioning, not a property of the medium in isolation

**Kind:** positive account of field-conditioning

**Content:** The same From that determines a seed's determinate possibility conditions what counts as an appropriate medium for that possibility. Fertility or suitability is a relation of fit, not an isolated property of the With.

**Source:** §2.4.

**Parents:** TS-021.

**Spent by:** chemistry realizability, domain-specific media, field/occupant relations.

**Relations:** seed/soil, egg/embryo/womb are recognition examples, not foundations.

**Standing:** Forced account clarified by recognition examples.

## TS-023 — With holds multiplicity on a basis without unifying it

**Kind:** office articulation

**Content:** With holds a multiplicity as multiplicity under a rule, criterion, or coherence-condition. Inclusion and exclusion are the same office viewed from opposite sides. With is not an empty container or thin set.

**Source:** §2.5.

**Parents:** TS-013, TS-015.

**Spent by:** domains, predicates, bases, realizability fields, self/other relation, actual holdings.

**Standing:** Forced.

---

## TS-024 — Held standings and basis-registered character are distinct

**Kind:** foundational distinction

**Content:** What a With holds are actual standings individuated by genesis and lineage. What its basis registers is the respect under which those standings are included, compared, same, or different. A basis need not register everything true of what it holds.

**Source:** §2.5.

**Parents:** TS-023, determinate basis.

**Spent by:** quantity/quality split, mathematics gate, comparison, predication, identity conditions.

**Standing:** Forced.

---

## TS-025 — Every determinate basis under-registers its held standings

**Kind:** structural theorem

**Content:** A determinate basis has facetness: it is one respect rather than every respect. A basis registering everything true of its held would exclude along no respect and would therefore fail to be a determinate constraint. Thus the held always exceeds the registered.

**Source:** §2.5, with dependency on §4.2.

**Parents:** TS-011, TS-023, TS-024, facetness.

**Spent by:** mathematics gate universality, basis-relative sameness, quantity/quality constitution.

**Residual:** the application of facetness to bases is the explicitly named pressed link.

**Standing:** Forced.

---

## TS-026 — With and its occupants co-condition one another

**Kind:** field/occupant relation

**Content:** A field is changed by what stands in it, and a standing is partly conditioned by the field in which it realizes. Neither absorbs the other.

**Source:** §2.5.

**Parents:** TS-022, TS-023.

**Spent by:** Realizability(S), double output, physical field recognition, chemistry environment dependence.

**Relations:** ecosystem and gravitational-manifold examples are recognition only.

**Standing:** Forced at framework level.

## TS-027 — This is actuality at full strength

**Kind:** office articulation

**Content:** This is the arrived determinate standing: the does, as distinct from the merely may and the realizable-given-what-stands. It is not exhausted by thin determinateness.

**Source:** §2.6.

**Parents:** TS-013, TS-015.

**Spent by:** actualization, closure, state, stride, domain occupancy.

## TS-028 — Every actual This has four intrinsic faces

**Kind:** articulation of actuality

**Content:** Every actual This is determinate, composed, composable, and conditioned. Determinateness gives bounded specificity. Composedness includes vertical office-composition and possible horizontal sub-standing composition. Composability makes the closure available to further Withs. Conditionedness carries both From-lineage and With-context.

**Source:** §2.6.

**Parents:** TS-015 through TS-027.

**Spent by:** primordial first content, turn-over, nested closure, domain succession.

**Standing:** Forced clarification of This at full strength.

## TS-029 — A This is a genuine unit without being a substance

**Kind:** ontological consequence

**Content:** A This stands as one because closure has arrived as one. It is not simple, sealed, independent, or self-grounding: it is composed, composable, and conditioned.

**Source:** §2.6.

**Parents:** TS-014, TS-028.

**Spent by:** replacement of substance, relatively stable closure, objecthood and persistence.

**Standing:** Forced within the closure account.

## TS-030 — Actual, standing, and closure are readings of one triadic unit

**Kind:** terminological relation

**Content:** A closure is From-With-This standing as one. An actual is closure read under arrival. A standing is closure read under availability for further determination. A completed closure may be taken as a unit at higher altitude.

**Source:** §2.7.

**Parents:** TS-014, TS-027 through TS-029.

**Spent by:** turn-over, layering, domain gate-ground, atom/domain recurrence.

**Standing:** Forced.

## TS-031 — Relatively stable closure replaces substance

**Kind:** sealed recognition and ontological replacement

**Content:** A relatively stable closure can bear properties, support predication, remain individuated, and persist through change without claiming existential independence. Unit-status does not require frozen simplicity.

**Source:** §2.7.

**Parents:** TS-029, TS-030.

**Spent by:** object-language, persistence, stable structures in physics and chemistry.

**Relations:** replacement of substance is recognition; the closure account itself is forced.

**Standing:** Recognition over forced structure.

## TS-032 — The architecture has two dimensions of composition

**Kind:** canonical control distinction

**Content:** Vertical is synchronic office-constitution, mutually baseless and invariant across all closures. Horizontal is diachronic accumulation of actuals, re-texturing of realizability, strides, turn-overs, and nesting of closures as units.

**Source:** §2.8.

**Parents:** TS-015 through TS-017, TS-028, TS-030.

**Spent by:** all layer, domain, emergence, J/rho, and primordial distinctions.

## TS-033 — Layer and altitude are horizontal, not vertical

**Kind:** terminological guard

**Content:** A layer is a completed closure taken as a unit at the next horizontal stage. Altitude is position in that layer tower. The tower may look vertical spatially, but every layer participates in vertical office-constitution identically.

**Source:** §2.8.

**Parents:** TS-032.

**Spent by:** cell-to-ecology examples, domain strata, turn-over, emergence.

**Standing:** Canonical terminological decision.

## TS-034 — Modal nesting, layer nesting, and reading stances must not be conflated

**Kind:** control distinction

**Content:** Actual-within-realizable-within-admissible is modal nesting. Closure-over-closure is horizontal layer nesting. Standing and building are two readings of a horizontal stride, not additional dimensions. The vertical itself has no diachronic reading.

**Source:** §2.8.

**Parents:** TS-032, TS-033.

**Spent by:** modal engine, strides, emergence, admissibility/realizability axes.

## Preservation obligations opened by Part 2

1. Preserve `closure` as one foundational concept recurring at different scales; do not introduce separate object-closure and domain-closure concepts.
2. Preserve the full-strength claim of vertical mutual composition; do not weaken it to implication, analogy, or mutual dependence.
3. Preserve the distinction between From→This and From→With.
4. Preserve held versus registered and the theorem that every determinate basis under-registers.
5. Preserve all four faces of This; none may be silently reduced to determinateness.
6. Preserve relatively stable closure as the replacement for substance without importing existential independence.
7. Enforce the vertical/horizontal, layer/altitude, modal-nesting, and reading-stance distinctions in every later compiler pass.
