# Epistemic Warrant and Semantic Registration — Adjudicated Inventory

Status: corrected governing compiler artifact.

## Direct ruling incorporated — 2026-07-15

The earlier inventory incorrectly placed Open, Conjectured, Registered, and Registered and Sealed on one ladder. Jordan's direct ruling restores the framework's original distinction:

- framework warrant records what the framework has secured and by what instrument;
- registration records whether inherited conceptual language is licensed over that secured content;
- Registered is not a synonym for established, proven, stable, or well-supported;
- Registered–Sealed is correspondence closure, not the top degree of proof.

This ruling governs the exact C2 point and supersedes the former four-standing formulation in this file.

---

## ESR-001 — The epistemology has two independent axes

**Canonical formulation:** Framework standing/warrant and semantic registration answer different questions. Neither axis may be inferred from the other.

**Disposition:** corrected and governing.

## ESR-002 — Native epistemic states

**Canonical formulation:** The compact epistemic states are Open, Conjectured, and Secured.

- Open: unresolved.
- Conjectured: live proposal not secured.
- Secured: established within stated scope by a named route.

**Scope rule:** Secured always travels with route, reach, conditions, and debts. It is not generic certainty.

## ESR-003 — Warrant is route-specific

**Canonical formulation:** Retorsion, dependency, construction, selection, formal theorem, defended posit, and empirical observation secure different things. Their results are not interchangeable points on one confidence scale.

**Examples:** retorsion secures ineliminability but not exhaustiveness; selection secures licensed contingency; theorem secures consequence inside named premises and rules.

## ESR-004 — Registration is a legacy-language relation

**Canonical formulation:** Registration applies only when framework-secured content is mapped to an inherited concept.

The registration states are:

1. Native;
2. Unregistered;
3. Registered;
4. Registered–Sealed.

The historical spelling Registered and Sealed means Registered–Sealed.

## ESR-005 — Registered licenses a bounded inherited name

**Canonical formulation:** Registered means that an explicitly mapped, framework-secured core is semantically sufficient to license an inherited name for forward use. Unmapped legacy content remains quarantined and may bear no load.

**Required record:** licensed core, quarantined legacy surplus, framework remainder, and scope.

**Excluded error:** reading Registered as well-proven, mature, approximately complete, or high confidence.

## ESR-006 — Registered–Sealed is bidirectional content-identity

**Canonical formulation:** Within explicit scope, the framework object and the properly stripped inherited concept carry the same content in both directions. Neither side has a semantic remainder.

**Consequence:** downstream surplus audits are unnecessary only within that scope.

**Excluded error:** treating contextual associations outside the stripped concept as automatically sealed.

## ESR-007 — Registration contributes no warrant

**Canonical formulation:** Recognition and naming do not secure the native framework claim. A registration state cannot upgrade epistemic state, proof, confidence, or warrant.

**Source alignment:** Architectonic Rigor states that a borrowed name contributes no securing standing to the structure it labels.

## ESR-008 — Framework-native claims need no registration

**Canonical formulation:** A novel framework concept may be Secured by its own warrant route while its registration state is Native. Lack of a legacy mapping is not an epistemic deficit.

## ESR-009 — Registered use carries a mandatory surplus audit

**Canonical formulation:** Every downstream expenditure of a Registered name must show that it uses only the licensed core. If the step spends quarantined legacy content, the step is a smuggle.

## ESR-010 — The seal ambiguity must be controlled

**Canonical formulation:**

- sealed recognition means recognition with unearned surplus quarantined and yields Registered use;
- sealed registration means correspondence closure and yields Registered–Sealed.

Operational controls must not use the shared word `seal` without identifying which operation is meant.

## ESR-011 — Administrative labels remain separate

Warrant strength, inherited conditions, evidence/test state, confidence, workflow/deposit state, and disposition are metadata. They are not epistemic or registration states.

## Required normalization

- Conjectured-strong → Conjectured plus warrant-strength metadata.
- Registered-candidate → independently state epistemic state, registration state, and workflow state.
- Defended posit → warrant profile; Secured only for the exact claim once the defense is complete.
- Registered, not Sealed → Registered plus explicit surplus boundary.
- Registered conditional on X → separate the epistemic conditions from the registration state.
- Dissolved, retracted, superseded, quarantined, historical → dispositions.
- Locked actual → deprecated conflation requiring claim-specific translation.

## Compiler obligation

Every pre-C2 status matrix must be read as a legacy compound record until its owning semantic lineage separates:

1. epistemic state;
2. warrant route and reach;
3. scope, conditions, and debts;
4. registration state;
5. licensed legacy content and quarantined surplus;
6. administrative metadata.

No mechanical corpus-wide conversion is authorized. Theory-specific translations belong to the owning lineage because the semantic boundary must be reconstructed claim by claim.
