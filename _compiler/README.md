# Compiler Workspace

The `_compiler` directory contains working semantic inventories used to refactor the original Triadic Closure wiki without losing living content or silently changing claim standing. These files are intermediate compiler artifacts, not canonical theory.

## Source authority

The compiler may use only:

1. the original wiki in Google Drive;
2. the supplemental foundation-audit instruction set at its stated grades;
3. the supplemental chemistry D1–D4 deposit at its stated grades.

The nine-document reconstruction has no source authority and must not be consulted, quoted, or used to fill gaps.

## Living-content rule

Compile living semantic content only. Do not create or retain semantic identities solely to preserve:

- deprecated terminology;
- withdrawn or eliminated claims;
- retired derivations;
- superseded partial inventories;
- presentation-only material that carries no theoretical or control load.

A supersession relation belongs in the compiler only when the relation itself remains necessary to interpret a living claim.

## Standing discipline

Claim standing and warrant route are separate axes.

- Claim standing records whether a claim is Open, Conjectured, Registered, Registered and Sealed, or another explicitly authorized grade supplied by a governing source.
- Warrant route records how the claim was reached: retorsion, construction, selection, recognition, defended posit, check, argument, interpretation, and related instruments.

A warrant-route word must never be substituted for claim standing. No summary may upgrade either axis.

## Semantic identity

The authoritative identity of a semantic unit is:

`<inventory file path> + <semantic heading>`

Early inventories used several overlapping bare labels such as `TSR-053`. Those labels are local aids, not globally unique identifiers. Cross-file references must cite the inventory filename and semantic heading unless and until a global symbol table assigns a unique permanent identifier.

New inventories should use section-scoped identifiers, for example `TSR-0801`, or another demonstrably unique namespace. Do not create new unscoped `TSR-NNN` identifiers.

## Dependency rules

1. Source-specific inventories govern detailed wording, route, limitation, and standing.
2. Cross-source inventories may provide aliases and dependency crosswalks but may not replace source-specific arguments.
3. A source-level example remains an example unless it carries an explicit living clarification or falsification boundary.
4. An audit instruction remains instruction-grade until the target source and dependency route support promotion.
5. A claim belongs where it is first forced, not where it is aesthetically convenient.
6. Duplicate expressions may be merged only after semantic equivalence has been established.
7. No canonical rendering may be produced until each living source unit has a destination and each destination can be traced to its source.
