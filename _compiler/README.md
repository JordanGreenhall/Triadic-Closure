# Compiler Workspace

The `_compiler` directory is a working environment for reconstructing the living Triadic Closure corpus. Its task is not document summarization. Its task is claim-level semantic adjudication across the full source corpus.

Every existing inventory remains provisional until each semantic unit has passed the adjudication protocol below.

## Source authority

The compiler may use:

1. the original wiki in Google Drive;
2. later detailed derivations, repairs, ledgers, and downstream applications within that original corpus;
3. the supplemental foundation-audit instruction set at its stated grades;
4. the supplemental chemistry D1–D4 deposit at its stated grades;
5. explicit current rulings by Jordan Hall, which govern the precise point adjudicated and must be propagated through all affected semantic units.

The nine-document reconstruction has no source authority and must not be consulted, quoted, or used to fill gaps.

Conversation material is not automatically authoritative. A direct present ruling by Jordan is authoritative for the claim it expressly adjudicates; historical conversation prose remains evidence only at the grade established by the corpus.

## Governing compilation rule

The compilation unit is a semantic object, not a document.

Encountering a claim in one document begins the work; it does not complete it. Before a semantic unit may be marked compiler-current, the compiler must search the entire accessible source corpus for:

- earlier and later formulations;
- detailed derivations and repairs;
- objections, rebuttals, falsifiers, and negative results;
- explicit status changes;
- downstream uses that sharpen or constrain the claim;
- conflicting summaries, control pages, and shorthand;
- notation changes and replacement formulas;
- dependencies and propagation targets.

The compiler must then adjudicate those materials into one strongest coherent living formulation.

## Claim-specific authority

Authority is claim-sensitive, not document-wide. For a particular semantic object, the normal evidentiary order is:

1. the most advanced complete detailed derivation;
2. later detailed repairs or extensions;
3. explicit adjudications of objections or competing routes;
4. later downstream work that necessarily spends and sharpens the claim;
5. current status and control pages;
6. summaries, reading guides, and overview prose.

A summary may route the reader but may not override more detailed successful work. A newer document is not automatically more authoritative if it merely compresses, elides, or misstates the detailed result.

## Required semantic-unit record

A completed semantic unit must contain:

- **Canonical formulation** — the strongest current statement.
- **Warrant route** — the detailed derivation, construction, selection, recognition, defended posit, empirical input, or other route.
- **Standing** — the exact current claim grade, separate from the warrant route.
- **Scope and conditions** — where the claim applies and what it depends on.
- **Dependencies** — semantic parents actually required.
- **Expenditures** — downstream claims that use it.
- **Adjudication** — why this formulation governs competing formulations.
- **Excluded revivals** — obsolete formulations that would mislead future readers, included only as routing prohibitions.
- **Open joints** — unresolved parts that remain live.
- **Propagation targets** — every compiler or source artifact whose wording must change because of the adjudication.
- **Evidence ledger** — the corpus locations collectively supporting the result.

## Contradiction discipline

Apparent disagreement must be classified before it is resolved. The compiler must distinguish:

- genuine contradiction;
- explicit supersession;
- refinement of an earlier coarse statement;
- different conditional scopes;
- different readings of the same closure;
- different claim grades;
- harmless local shorthand;
- misleading summary compression;
- notation change without semantic change.

The compiler may neither silently harmonize genuine contradictions nor fossilize superseded formulations as living semantic identities.

## Living-content rule

Compile living semantic content only. Do not create or retain semantic identities solely to preserve:

- deprecated terminology;
- withdrawn or eliminated claims;
- retired derivations;
- superseded partial inventories;
- presentation-only material that carries no theoretical or control load.

A supersession relation belongs in the compiler only when it is required to prevent revival of a displaced claim or to interpret the living result.

Deleting an obsolete formulation requires identifying its living replacement unless the corpus explicitly establishes that the underlying claim itself was abandoned.

Absence from the partial compiler never implies absence from the framework.

## Standing discipline

Claim standing and warrant route are separate axes.

- Claim standing records whether a claim is Open, Conjectured, Registered, Registered and Sealed, or another explicitly authorized grade supplied by a governing source.
- Warrant route records how the claim was reached: retorsion, construction, selection, recognition, defended posit, check, argument, interpretation, empirical state input, and related instruments.

A warrant-route word must never be substituted for claim standing. No summary may upgrade either axis.

## Semantic identity

The authoritative working identity of a semantic unit is:

`<inventory file path> + <semantic heading>`

Legacy bare labels such as `TSR-053` are local aids, not globally unique identifiers. Cross-file references must cite the inventory filename and semantic heading until a global symbol table assigns permanent identities.

## Status of existing inventories

The current files were produced primarily by document-level extraction. They are therefore **unadjudicated or partially adjudicated working inventories**, not reliable canonical semantic outputs.

Each file must now be reviewed under the corpus-wide protocol. A unit may be retained unchanged only after the forward and lateral corpus search confirms that it is current, coherent, properly graded, and not contradicted or sharpened elsewhere.

## Review order

The repair pass proceeds in dependency order:

1. governing compiler rules and source authority;
2. foundational architectonic semantic objects;
3. domain-entry and control objects;
4. physics semantic objects, adjudicated across all detailed and downstream sources;
5. chemistry inheritance and chemistry-native objects;
6. global cross-check for contradiction, notation, status, and propagation completeness;
7. permanent symbol table and canonical rendering.

## Completion criterion

The compiler is complete only when every living semantic object has been adjudicated across the corpus, every stale formulation has a known replacement or explicit retirement, every dependency and expenditure is traceable, and every affected artifact has been repaired consistently.
