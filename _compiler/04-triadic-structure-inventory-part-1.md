## TS-001 — Architectonic argument precedes a fixed proof system
The treatise examines the conditions under which proof, derivation, domain, object, and determinate content can appear. It therefore argues by architectonic dependency rather than only within an already fixed formal system.

Parents: AR-001, AR-002.
Standing: source methodological claim.

## TS-002 — Minimal dependency placement
A claim belongs at the first level where all of its dependencies are available. Later disciplinary names, analogies, and content must remain quarantined until earned.

Parents: AR-003, AR-004, AR-012, AR-014.
Spent by: the ordering of the entire treatise and later domain walks.
Standing: source governing rule.

## TS-003 — Source tags report route, not confidence
`Posited`, `Forced`, `Recognition`, `Selected`, and `Open` identify which architectonic instrument reached a claim and where that instrument stops. They are not ordinary confidence labels.

## TS-004 — Four-part foundation with mixed standing
The foundation contains two secured roots and two completeness posits. Relating is ineliminable; determination requires at least distinction, holding, and standing. Primitiveness of relating and exactly-three are defended completeness posits.

Parents: AR-007 through AR-010, AR-015, AR-016.
Spent by: the remainder of the treatise.
Standing: mixed, exactly as stated in the source.

## TS-005 — Relating cannot be assembled from prior unrelated parts
Any assembly of supposedly unrelated parts already connects them. Connecting is relating. This secures the non-compositeness and ineliminability of relating, but does not by itself secure primitiveness or the three-count.

Parents: TS-004.
Standing: Forced by retorsion.

## TS-006 — Determination requires at least three offices
Every determinate claim uses distinction, holding, and standing. Attempts to remove one of these still perform it. The result blocks reduction to one or two offices but does not establish exact completeness.

Parents: TS-004.
Spent by: From / With / This.
Standing: Forced by retorsion.

## TS-007 — Exactly-three is a defended posit
The office inventory cannot certify its own completeness from outside itself. Exactly-three is therefore carried with a defense profile: no surviving counterexample, examined rivals reduce to secondary forms of the three, and the inventory remains stable when applied to its own defense.

Parents: TS-006, AR-015, AR-016.
Standing: Posited as a defended posit.

## TS-008 — Order of being differs from order of knowing
The floor is prior to developed logic in the order of being. The treatise is written from a late domain and uses developed inference to display backward dependency traces. Its discourse does not generate the floor.

Parents: AR-011, TS-001, TS-002.
Standing: Forced as a methodological clarification.

## TS-009 — The method must fit the three-office architecture
The treatise checks that dependency, retorsion, construction, selection, sealed recognition, and the completeness diagnostic can be recognized as office-activity rather than a fourth operation. This is a consistency check, not a derivation of the methods from the framework.

Parents: AR-003, AR-007, AR-012 through AR-016.
Standing: Recognition.

## TS-010 — Opening orientation: unbounded possible under constraint
The account begins from the unbounded possible, not from a largest object, container, or prior substance. The first move is imposition of the triadic constraint, followed by successive constraints moving from admissibility through realizability to actuality.

Parents: TS-004, TS-007.
Standing: Posited as opening orientation.

## TS-011 — Constraint selects rather than adds
A constraint does not introduce foreign content into the unbounded field. It selects by excluding what fails the boundary. This is a cross-cutting dependency for the modal engine, anti-smuggling discipline, primordial unfolding, domain phases, and later domain entry.

Parents: TS-010, AR-013.
Standing: source structural claim; exact source status continues beyond the excerpt and remains to be extracted.
