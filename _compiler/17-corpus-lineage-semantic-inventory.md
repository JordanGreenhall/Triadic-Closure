## Compilation rule

This control document is compiled for living lineage and authority rules. Historical documents are not preserved as independent semantic units merely because they are listed. A historical item is retained only where its status constrains current use.

---

## CL-001 — The corpus must be read by lineage, not by indiscriminate aggregation

**Kind:** corpus-control rule

**Content:** The wiki contains canonical, current, superseded, corrective, and historical materials. These may not be averaged into one doctrine. Each claim must be interpreted through source authority, later correction, and explicit supersession.

**Parents:** architectonic dependency discipline; claim-status control.

**Spent by:** every later compiler pass and canonical rendering.

**Standing:** current governing rule.

---

## CL-002 — The foundational dependency order precedes domain results

**Kind:** reading and warrant order

**Content:** Architectonic rigor and the canonical triadic-structure source must be read before the domain-entry method, and the domain-entry method before physics control and result modules. Later domain claims may spend the prior whole but may not retroactively define it.

**Parents:** architectonic rigor; triadic structure; domain-entry method.

**Spent by:** physics and successor-domain compilation.

**Standing:** current governing order.

---

## CL-003 — The canonical foundation and current result modules have different corpus roles

**Kind:** authority distinction

**Content:** Foundational sources establish method, offices, closure, modal structure, and the mathematical handoff. Domain-result modules apply and extend that ground under domain constraints. A result module cannot silently override the foundation; a later explicit correction may revise a result module while preserving the foundation.

**Parents:** CL-001, CL-002.

**Spent by:** source adjudication and conflict resolution.

**Standing:** current control distinction.

---

## CL-004 — Conversation ledgers are adjudicative evidence, not automatic theory authority

**Kind:** source-role distinction

**Content:** A conversation ledger may record critique, repair, approved terminology, supersession, rejected derivations, and methodological failures. It can therefore establish lineage and adjudication. Its contents do not become canonical theory merely by appearing in the ledger; the relevant claim must be integrated into a living source or independently compiled at its stated grade.

**Parents:** CL-001; source-authority discipline.

**Spent by:** use of `full-conversation-ledger.md` and similar records.

**Standing:** current control rule.

---

## CL-005 — Historical and superseded documents remain provenance, not live doctrine

**Kind:** supersession rule

**Content:** A document explicitly classified as superseded, historical, cautionary, task-forming, or provenance-only may explain how the present corpus developed but may not supply live premises unless a current source expressly re-registers the relevant semantic unit.

**Parents:** CL-001, CL-004.

**Spent by:** exclusion of package-era reconstructions, retired routes, and obsolete task documents from the living dependency graph.

**Standing:** current governing rule.

## CL-006 — Current result pages remain defeasible under higher-authority control and later explicit correction

**Kind:** result-module rule

**Content:** Active domain result pages are usable unless contradicted by the canonical foundation, a governing control page, or a later explicit supersession. Their inclusion as current does not make every sentence equally sealed.

**Parents:** CL-001, CL-003.

**Spent by:** all physics result inventories.

**Standing:** current control rule.

## CL-007 — Process and verification artifacts must be distinguished from theory claims

**Kind:** corpus-role distinction

**Content:** Checklists, deterministic checks, verification companions, and execution scripts may verify finite consequences, record falsifiers, or govern procedure. They do not independently establish the architectonic premises they test. Any reported run result is provenance tied to a specific artifact and version, not a standing theory claim.

**Parents:** architectonic rigor; CL-003.

**Spent by:** future compilation of verification and process documents.

**Standing:** current compiler clarification.

---

## CL-008 — Supersession must be explicit and claim-sensitive

**Kind:** migration rule

**Content:** Supersession is not inferred merely from age, duplication, or a newer filename. It must be established by an explicit control source or by a later source that identifies and replaces the earlier claim. Where only part of a document is superseded, unaffected living units remain eligible for compilation.

**Parents:** CL-001, CL-005.

**Spent by:** lossless migration and source-to-destination accounting.

**Standing:** current compiler rule.

---

## CL-009 — The target of compilation is the current settled state together with its open joints

**Kind:** compiler objective

**Content:** The compiler is not an archive of every historical formulation and not a consensus average. It must preserve the living architectonic, the exact standing of its claims, the dependency graph, explicit falsifiers, and unresolved joints that constrain downstream use.

**Parents:** CL-001 through CL-008.

**Spent by:** the entire refactor.

**Standing:** current governing objective.
