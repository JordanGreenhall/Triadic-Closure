## 5. Cautions (failure modes to avoid)

- **Do not infer dimension from arity.** "The coalgebra is ternary, therefore dimension 3" is invalid and is explicitly *not* the intended answer. A ternary operation lives on spaces of many dimensions. If 3 is forced, it is forced by coherence/isotropy interaction, not by counting tensor slots.
- **Do not assume the multiplicity is a vector space of a chosen dimension and read off 3.** The dimension must emerge from the constraints, not be posited.
- **Isotropy alone does not force 3** (the symmetric group acts on any dimension). The force, if any, is isotropy *together with* the ternary coherence. Locate the joint constraint.
- **The SÔéâ 2-dimensional standard representation is a real object** (three vectors summing to zero in Ôäé┬▓, permuted, no invariant line). If `dim V = 2` is to be excluded, it must be excluded by incompatibility with the ternary ╬┤ and its coherence ¤ü, not by a claim that no isotropic 3-direction structure exists in dimension 2.
