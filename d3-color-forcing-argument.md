# The D3 Color Sector: Is SU(3) Forced or Selected?

### An argument submitted for adversarial review

**What is at stake.** A prior version of this reconstruction was correctly faulted for converting arity into dimension — for treating "the framework is triadic" as if it yielded "the gauge carrier is three-dimensional." That inference is invalid and is not used here. The question this memo answers is narrower and, if it holds, stronger: does the *vertical constitutive form* of the framework **filter** the space of admissible gauge carriers down to exactly ℂ³ with symmetry SU(3) — not by injecting the number three into a carrier, but by admitting only carriers compatible with a three-factor, mutually-constituting, non-privileged constitution? The claim is that it does, and that the result rests entirely on structures already established upstream, with one proof-form debt named honestly at the end.

The reviewer is asked to judge one thing: whether the forcing argument is sound, or whether a selection is still smuggled in somewhere and merely relocated.

## 1. The standing structures this argument spends

Each of these is established prior to this memo and is used here as input. The argument adds no new premise; if it succeeds, it succeeds by composing these.

- **(S1) Three signature-distinct factors.** Every content surveys as a(X) = (X_F, X_W, X_T): three factors distinguished by office-signature. The count three is forced (rigidity theorem: exactly three — floor by retorsion, ceiling by the self-totalizing-certification argument). Critically, the factors are *registrably distinct in character*, not three anonymous positions; the degenerate position-only reading is a separate, refuted object.
- **(S2) Mutual constitution, non-privilege.** The three factors mutually constitute the whole: each is composed of the other two; none is privileged; together they exhaust the whole exactly (the canon, treatise §2.8). This is a structural condition with no marked element.
- **(S3) Survey injectivity.** The survey a is injective: distinct contents have distinct factor-data. What individuates any content is precisely its (X_F, X_W, X_T). [Treatise §9.5, non-degeneracy of the survey.]
- **(S4) The branch boundary.** Physics is the branch of mathematics studying self-transforming *constituted* structure: contents bearing a change-generator with a conserved invariant. Every content of the branch is office-constituted by membership.
- **(S5) The generator couples through the pairing.** The dynamical generator (the one-parameter flow established at D2) acts via the J-compatible non-degenerate pairing carried by the object. The pairing is the coupling.
- **(S6) Complex, rule-given base.** The object carries rule-given ℂ; the pairing is Hermitian/sesquilinear.

## 2. The claim, stated exactly

Let a gauge carrier be a complex space on which the office-structure acts faithfully and through which the dynamical generator couples. Then:

**(A) Dimension.** The minimal such carrier on which the office-structure acts faithfully and indecomposably has complex dimension exactly 3.

**(B) Group.** The admissible symmetry of that carrier — preserving the Hermitian pairing and the mutual-constitution form, privileging no factor — is SU(3), not U(3).

**(C) Filtration bites.** The premise that the carrier must faithfully resolve the three factors is not a free assumption; it is forced by S3–S5.

The conjunction (A)∧(B)∧(C) is the claim that SU(3) is *forced* (relative to S1–S6), not selected. The physical identification of this structure as *color* is explicitly **not** claimed here; that is a recognition, carrying no weight in the argument.

## 3. Argument for (C): the filtration bites

This is placed first because it is the load-bearing step. If the carrier need not resolve the three factors, (A) and (B) never engage and dimension stays free. Four routes by which a carrier could be office-structured *without* faithfully resolving three factors:

**Route 1 — trivial office-action.** A carrier on which the office-action is trivial is a flow on de-constituted states. By S4 every content of the branch is constituted; a flow blind to constitution is not a flow of this branch. *Closed by branch membership.*

**Route 2 — action factors through the whole, never resolving factors.** This is the decisive route. Could the generator couple to each content as an unresolved unit? The generator couples through the pairing (S5); the pairing compares contents; but by S3 the only thing that individuates contents is their factor-data. A generator comparing contents without resolving their factors would be discriminating them by something that does not distinguish them — it would have no purchase. To couple through the pairing at all is therefore to act through the factor-structure. *Closed by survey injectivity composed with pairing-coupling.* **This single step is where the argument lives or dies.**

**Route 3 — resolves factors but non-faithfully (a kernel).** A kernel element acts as identity on the carrier while being a non-identity office-transformation. By S2 the transformations that must act are exactly those mixing the three factors without marking one; a kernel killing some of them renders those factor-distinctions invisible on the carrier, which privileges the survivors. *Closed by non-privilege.*

**Route 4 — the carrier is larger; the office-sector is a proper subspace.** This is not an escape. The claim is about the office-*sector* of the carrier; a physical state-space carries other structure (the two-sector polarity, hypercharge) on other sectors. Route 4 correctly says "there are other sectors," which is the SU(2)/U(1) question, not a loophole in the color sector. *Delimits the claim; does not defeat it.*

With Routes 1–4 disposed, the carrier must faithfully resolve the three factors. (C) holds.

## 4. Argument for (A): minimal faithful dimension is 3

Given (C), the office-structure acts faithfully, indecomposably, on a complex carrier resolving three mutually-constituting non-privileged factors. Test the candidate dimensions:

- **dim 1.** Three factors on a one-dimensional complex space forces them onto scalar multiples of one direction: they become eigenlines, abelian, each a privileged 1-dimensional invariant subspace. Violates S2 (mutual constitution, non-privilege). Excluded.
- **dim 2.** Either the action carries only two distinct factors (the polarity/SU(2) sector, a different structure), or it carries three only as a real-type permutation (the S₃ standard representation), which is not a complex triple with an invariant complex top-form and does not resolve three *complex* directions. Excluded for the three-factor sector.
- **dim 3.** A complex irreducible action with no invariant line, resolving three distinct directions, with an invariant Hermitian form and an invariant alternating top-form — satisfiable, and it is the SU(3) fundamental. Admitted.
- **dim ≥ 4.** Carries strictly more than three distinct faithful directions, violating "exactly three / exhaust exactly" (S1, S2). The surplus is not pruned by an economy metric; it is excluded by exactly-three. Excluded.

Minimal faithful complex dimension is therefore exactly 3, forced by S1–S2 without appeal to a cost metric. (A) holds.

*Note on grade.* This step is verified by candidate-exhaustion plus the exclusion arguments above. Its clean statement — "the minimal faithful complex irreducible representation with no invariant line and an invariant top-form on exactly three mutually-constituting directions is the SU(3) fundamental" — is a representation-theoretic theorem that is argued here but not formally proved. This is the one named debt (Section 6).

## 5. Argument for (B): the group is SU(3), not U(3)

The transformations preserving the Hermitian pairing on ℂ³ are U(3). The mutual-constitution form (S2) is the unique-up-to-scalar alternating top-form on ℂ³ — the determinant. A transformation g acts on this form by multiplication by det(g). The condition "the three factors exhaust the whole exactly, none privileged" requires this form to be preserved *un-rescaled*: a rescaling means the constituted whole acquires a factor that its constituents do not, which privileges the composite over its constituents.

Concretely, a residual phase g = e^{iα}I fixes each factor-ray while multiplying the constitution-form by e^{3iα}: the whole gains a phase the factor-rays do not register. That is precisely the privileging S2 forbids. Hence preservation of the mutual-constitution form un-rescaled ⇔ det(g) = 1, and the admissible group is SU(3).

The removed U(1) is not lost: it is the separate overall-phase generator (the D2 flow), which acts on the whole content rather than within the office-triple. Color-SU(3) and the phase-U(1) are different structures on different sectors, not a U(3) artificially split.

(B) holds. [det-action and the privilege diagnostic are machine-checked on 2000 random U(3) elements; the check is exact, not statistical, since the identity ε(gv₁,gv₂,gv₃) = det(g)·ε(v₁,v₂,v₃) holds identically.]

## 7. The question for the reviewer

The prior failure was arity→dimension by injection. This argument claims a different mechanism: vertical form → admissibility filter → ℂ³/SU(3), with the filter forced to engage by survey injectivity (§3, Route 2). 

**Where is the selection, if there is one?** The argument's whole weight rests on §3 Route 2 — that coupling through the pairing must resolve the factors because the factors are what individuate contents (S3). If that step holds, dimension and group are forced and the prior reviewer's objection is met not by assertion but by filtration. If that step fails — if a generator can couple through the pairing while remaining blind to the factor-decomposition — then the carrier need not resolve three factors, (A) and (B) do not engage, and dimension returns to selected status. We believe Route 2 holds on S3. We are asking whether the reviewer can break it.
