# SM Frontier Item 5 — Gauge Architecture Beyond Color

Item 5 is not one undifferentiated proof target. It splits into four sub-capacities with different loads and different grades. Item 4 is now terminal/Registered, so Item 5 can advance past the previous blocked-preparatory state.

## 5.1 — SU(2) / weak source: Registered source-load PASS

The actual load is the gauge-source and its action: a two-state/doublet structure with a coupling domain. The exact weak module is not Item 5's load; it belongs to Item 6 and later module/charge gates.

Native route: the three With-flattened spatial degrees have rotatability (`SO(3)`). Orientation-faithful coupling is chiral by the already-Registered chiral/vectorial criterion. Orientation-faithful coupling to non-simply-connected `SO(3)` must run through its simply-connected double cover `Spin(3)=SU(2)` to be single-valued. Therefore `SU(2)` is forced as the lift required for chiral coupling to spatial rotatability, and the doublet is its minimal faithful spinor representation.

Verdict: **Registered** for source-load. Exact embedding/module remains Open and is not asked of Item 5.

## 5.2 — U(1) / From-phase: Registered source-load PASS

Item 4 Gate D strengthens this. Conservation of the closure-magnitude fixes the modulus of the same-kind closure-form, yielding the `SU(3)` determinant-one direction, while leaving the phase free. That free phase is an oriented continuous line: `U(1)`. Framework-natively it is the From-phase, because From is one-way/oriented.

Thus `U(1)` and `SU(3)` are two faces of the one closure-form: modulus gives `SU(3)`; phase gives `U(1)`. The conserved/read quantity is charge-like valence: the From-orientation count.

Verdict: **Registered** in root/form. Exact charge and hypercharge assignments remain Open under Item 8.

## 5.3 — Gauge completeness: defended posit; diagnostic-capped; non-load-bearing

Gauge completeness is not a normal proof target. “These are all the internal gauge sources” is an inventory-completeness claim made from inside the inventory; the completeness diagnostic fires exactly as it does for the exactly-three office count. It cannot be Registered and Sealed.

But it is not merely Open. The structural map gives it the standing of a defended posit:

- `U(1)` ↔ From — oriented From-phase line. Registered.
- `SU(3)` ↔ This — color as This-flattening / interior triadic distinction. Registered.
- `SU(2)` ↔ With — lifted rotatability of the coherent metric field. Recognition / softest link.

Three offices, three gauge structures, one each. “No fourth gauge source” inherits from “no fourth office,” with the same diagnostic ceiling. Rivals return as office-subdivisions: a second `U(1)` is the same From-phase, `SU(N>3)` exceeds the minimal interior 3-carrier, larger rotatability exceeds the three spatial degrees, and the enumeration turned on itself returns the From/With/This act.

Verdict: **defended-posit candidate**, capped by the completeness diagnostic and conditional on firming the `SU(2)` ↔ With / lifted-rotatability-as-With link. This sub-capacity is non-load-bearing and should not be treated as a proof target.

Falsification surface: a gauge source genuinely traceable to none of the three offices, or two genuinely distinct sources from one office.

## 5.4 — Electroweak residuals: QUARANTINED

Photon/W/Z sectors, electroweak mixing, and symmetry-breaking/residual structure depend on still-open module/value/breaking gates. Item 6 has landed for registered spin/chirality machinery; Item 8 has landed for native charge structure while leaving hypercharge/center-lock/value arithmetic open; Item 13 keeps Higgs/electroweak breaking/generations quarantined.

Verdict: **QUARANTINED**. Photon/W/Z/mixing may be used only as empirical or mature-physics comparison labels until the remaining Item 8 hypercharge/center-lock/value gates and Item 13 electroweak-breaking gates are discharged.
