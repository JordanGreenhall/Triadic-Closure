# P15 — Charge, Hypercharge, Valence, and Center-Locking

## Controlling specification

P15 contains exactly:

1. charge as an oriented relational valence;
2. hypercharge as the From-phase assignment required to distinguish chiral role-sectors;
3. the standard relation between charge, weak orientation, and hypercharge at recognition scope;
4. center-locking and charge-lattice claims;
5. the boundary to electroweak breaking and later quantitative units.

P15 does not normalize quantitative QCD dynamics, masses, baryon energetics, decay, electroweak breaking, Higgs/Yukawa structure, generations, mixing angles, or spectrum.

## 1. Charge and valence

P15 licenses **charge** only for a standing's oriented outward/inward relational valence: the signed capacity by which a role presents an externally satisfiable or internally borne edge. This is a framework-native relational core, not a primitive substance-property.

At this scope:

- valence orientation is Secured structurally and Registered;
- charge as the mature name for that signed relational core is Registered, not Sealed;
- charge magnitude and numerical assignments are not supplied by orientation alone;
- the electron/proton inverse-equal pairing is a bounded role recognition, not a derivation of the elementary charge magnitude.

## 2. Hypercharge

P11 supplies a From-phase `U(1)` source-form and P12 supplies chiral role-structure. P15 may therefore state the architectural need for a phase assignment that distinguishes role-sectors not already distinguished by the With action.

This licenses:

- hypercharge as the mature name for a sector-indexing From-phase assignment — Registered at architectural scope;
- exact one-generation assignments — Open;
- anomaly-cancellation ratios — conditional on an independently supplied chiral matter module;
- absolute normalization — Open.

The mature hypercharge table may be used as an empirical target or consistency check, never as warrant.

## 3. Charge relation

The standard relation

\[
Q = T_3 + \frac{Y}{2}
\]

is Sealed mathematics once the operators and normalization convention are admitted. P15 does not derive the physical population or numerical values of `T_3` and `Y`. Its framework status is therefore conditional recognition only.

## 4. Center-locking and the charge lattice

The corpus contains a proposed route in which compatibility among the centers of the color, lifted-With, and phase source-forms constrains allowed phase assignments. P15 records that route as **Conjectured**.

It does not currently establish:

- the exact global gauge-group quotient;
- the `1/6` charge lattice as a framework theorem;
- uniqueness of the Standard Model hypercharge pattern;
- anomaly cancellation;
- electric-charge quantization from framework primitives alone.

Any `1/6` or center-quotient result remains conditional mystery-compression until the chiral module, center action, and locking condition are positively constructed.

## 5. Nucleon valence boundary

P14 distinguishes proton and neutron by `uud` and `udd` mark patterns. P15 adds only the relational-valence reading:

- proton: an outward satisfiable valence is Registered as the bounded core of positive charge;
- neutron: no net outward valence is Registered as the bounded core of neutrality;
- quark fractional charges and the arithmetic yielding `+1` and `0` remain conditional on the unresolved assignment table.

No mass ordering, decay channel, or energetic relief mechanism is normalized here.
