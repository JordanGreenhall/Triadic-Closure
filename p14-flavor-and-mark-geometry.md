# P14 — Flavor and Mark Geometry

## Controlling specification

P14 contains exactly:

1. flavor as marking of the color-resolved role;
2. the three-mark count;
3. the cyclic/no-privilege mark geometry;
4. the pairwise mark metric.

P14 does not normalize charge, hypercharge, valence, or center-locking (P15); quantitative QCD dynamics (P16); mass relations, proton/neutron energetics, or decay (P17–P21); generations or electroweak breaking (P29).

## Dependencies

P14 inherits:

- P10 This-flattening color as three non-interchangeable qualitative standings;
- P13 quark as the color-resolved fermionic role;
- P8's selected three-dimensional complex carrier and Hermitian pairing at their recorded grades;
- the no-privileged-internal-frame discipline used in P9.

## 1. Flavor as marking

Color supplies a required threefold qualitative distinction, but bare color does not itself specify determinate standing-values within that distinction. A mark is the determinate registration by which one color-resolved standing is distinguished from another while remaining a standing of the same color-capacity.

Flavor is therefore Registered as **marked color** at role scope. This is a framework-native relation between P10 color and P13 quark-role; it is not imported flavor `SU(3)`.

The inherited names `u`, `d`, and `s` are Registered only as labels for the three primitive mark-positions. Their Standard Model charges, masses, mixing relations, and generation placement are quarantined.

## 2. Why three primitive marks

The mark count is not a second independent three. There are three primitive mark-positions because marking engages the already-established threefold non-interchangeable color distinction. Thus:

> flavor's primitive three-ness is color's three-ness under determinate marking.

This is Secured within the displayed construction and Registered, not Sealed. It would fail if determinate engagement of one threefold qualitative carrier required fewer or more primitive mark-positions than the carrier supplies.

P14 makes no claim that all mature quark flavors are exhausted by these three primitive marks, and no generation count follows.

## 3. Cyclic mark geometry

The primitive marks are:

- distinct;
- equal in standing;
- non-interchangeable in qualitative identity;
- without a privileged member.

For exactly three marks, the orientation-preserving no-privilege action is cyclic. P14 therefore Registers a `Z_3` cyclic ordering on the primitive mark-set.

This cyclic action preserves equal magnitude. The absence of a privileged fixed mark-direction removes the trivial fixed component in the declared mark representation, yielding

\[
e_u+e_d+e_s=0.
\]

The no-fixed-line step is Registered, not Sealed, because it inherits the P9 no-privileged-frame warrant and its debt. It is not licensed as a general theorem that every three-element marked set must lie in a plane or sum to zero.

## 4. Pairwise mark metric

Normalize the equal mark magnitudes to one. Cyclic symmetry makes the pairwise inner products equal; write the common value as `p`. Then

\[
0=\lVert e_u+e_d+e_s\rVert^2=3+6p,
\]

so

\[
e_u\cdot e_d=e_d\cdot e_s=e_s\cdot e_u=-\frac12.
\]

Equivalently, the three equal mark-vectors lie at mutual angle `120°` in the selected real mark-plane.

The algebraic consequence is Sealed given equal magnitude, equal pairwise overlap, and zero sum. The framework selection of those premises is Registered, not Sealed. The `-1/2` mark metric is therefore Registered, not Sealed, at P14 scope.

## 5. Boundary on proton and neutron differentiation

P14 supplies the mark configurations by which the two P13 nucleon roles may be positively distinguished:

- proton-role mark pattern: `uud`;
- neutron-role mark pattern: `udd`.

This is Registered as mark-configuration differentiation only. P14 does not derive:

- electric charge or valence;
- which role is lighter;
- stability or decay;
- a mass difference;
- a weak-transition law.

Those claims retain their later owners.
