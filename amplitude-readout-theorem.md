# Amplitude / Readout Theorem

This page records Frontier Item 2 of the SM-content smuggle frontier. It clarifies what is
currently licensed about amplitude, readout, squaring, and observed frequency.

## Mature status

Status: **Registered, not sealed**.

The framework currently licenses a normalized weighting measure tied to coherent
participation. It does **not** yet seal the stronger claim that observed long-run
frequency is identical to that measure in all cases.

## The registered core

A candidate actualization channel may receive a framework-native weight when all
of the following hold.

1. **Licensed standing.** The candidate channel has passed the physics-registration
   control rule or is explicitly marked as mathematical/empirical/conditional.
2. **Amplitude-space location.** The quantity being squared is a reach, overlap,
   or coherence-participation amplitude in the relevant state/standing space, not
   merely an angle or distance in a compact readout diagram.
3. **Pairing origin.** The amplitude is given by the licensed pairing or
   bilinear/inner-product structure for the domain.
4. **Normalization/conservation.** The competing channels are in a normalized
   frame of alternatives, so the weights can function as a measure.
5. **Status declaration.** The page states whether the result is a framework
   measure, a mathematical theorem about that measure, an empirical observed
   frequency, or a mature-physics comparison.

Under those conditions, the square of the amplitude may be used as a
**Registered weighting measure**.

## What mathematics contributes

Where the hypotheses hold, Gleason/frame-function reasoning may be used as
mathematics-as-mathematics: a normalized noncontextual measure over a sufficiently
rich Hilbert-like frame is represented by a quadratic form. In the present wiki
language, this supports the move from pairing amplitude to `|h(σ,k)|²`.

The dimensional caveat from [realizability-weighting-law](realizability-weighting-law.md) remains live:
standard Gleason-style support requires a sufficiently rich frame, classically
dimension `>= 3`; the dimension-2 gap is not closed here and must not be hidden in
downstream uses.

This is legitimate mathematics. The verifier should not reject it merely because
it resembles the Born rule.

But the mathematical theorem does not by itself prove that observed repetitions
must sample that measure as long-run frequency. That is the remaining bridge.

## The open blocker: frequency equals weight

The current blocker is the identity:

```text
observed long-run frequency = framework weighting measure
```

This remains **Open** as a sealing gate. Empirical frequencies may be cited as
empirical observations or checks. They may not be relabeled as framework-native
frequency derivations unless the bridge from measure to sampling/frequency is
proved.

Therefore decay rates, transition rates, Born frequencies, and probabilistic
claims are allowed only in one of three forms:

1. framework-native measure, not sealed as observed frequency;
2. empirical observed frequency, explicitly marked empirical;
3. mature-physics comparison, explicitly marked external/comparative.

## Amplitude geometry vs compact readout geometry

A common smuggle is to take an angle or separation in a compact readout/state
picture and square its cosine as if it were automatically an amplitude overlap.
That is not licensed.

A `cos²(theta)` calculation is admissible only if the page first establishes that:

- `theta` lives in amplitude/access geometry, not merely display/readout geometry;
- the relevant vectors or channels are normalized in the licensed pairing;
- the squared quantity is the actual reach/coherence participation being weighted.

If those conditions are not shown, `cos²(theta)` may remain a heuristic or
comparison but cannot do framework-native proof work.

## Non-physics instance still owed

The existing realizability-weighting page names a non-physics end-to-end instance,
preferably Boltzmann/statistical mechanics, as an undone sealing gate. This loop
does not complete that derivation. It records the gate as still owed.

## Verification rule for downstream pages

For any page using amplitude, Born, frequency, transition rate, decay rate, or
`cos²(theta)`, the verifier asks:

1. Is the object being squared truly an amplitude/reach/coherence-participation
   quantity?
2. Is the pairing or frame licensed?
3. Is the result used only as a measure, or is it upgraded to observed frequency?
4. If upgraded to frequency, where is the frequency=weight bridge proved?
5. If the value is empirical, is it labeled empirical rather than derived?
6. If it comes from mature physics, is it labeled external comparison?

Failure on questions 1–4 requires downgrade or quarantine. Passing questions 5–6
is acceptable; legitimate empirical anchoring and legitimate mathematics should
not be rejected.

## Frontier Item 2 decision

The correct equilibrium is:

- accept `|amplitude|²` as a Registered weighting measure when the amplitude and
  frame are licensed;
- accept empirical frequencies as empirical observations;
- reject any silent promotion from measure to observed frequency;
- reject any `cos²` argument whose angle is not first shown to be an
  amplitude-space angle.
