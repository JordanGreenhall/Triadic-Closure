# Chiral Coupling Result

A fermionic closure carries the propagation arrow and the lifted rotation sense. Their correlation is the helicity operator

> `h = sigma · p_hat`, with `h^2 = I`,

on the two-dimensional spin lift. Its nondegenerate eigenvalues `-1` and `+1` define the left- and right-helicity sectors.

## The admissible coupling algebra

The propagation arrow is the only registered direction. Rotations about that arrow form the registered `U(1)` symmetry generated by `h`. An admissible coupling operator `M` must preserve that symmetry:

> `[M,h] = 0`.

Because `h` is nondegenerate on a two-dimensional space, its commutant is exactly

> `Comm(h) = span{I,h}`.

Equivalently, in the helicity basis `h = diag(1,-1)`, every operator commuting with `h` is diagonal and therefore has the form

> `M = aI + bh`.

A transverse Pauli component would fail to commute with `h` and would select a direction not registered by the arrow. It is excluded by the no-privileged-frame constraint. The coupling algebra is therefore exhibited by the commutant theorem rather than assumed.

**Grade:** **Registered-candidate** for the commutant closure.

## Orientation and maximal chirality

A coupling is itself a From-act and therefore carries orientation. It cannot be purely orientation-blind, so an orientation-faithful coupling has `b != 0` and acts differently on the two helicity sectors. Parity violation follows structurally.

A coherently attached coupling must reproduce itself under re-coupling:

> `M^2 = M`.

For `M = aI + bh` and `h^2 = I`, idempotency gives

> `a^2 + b^2 = a`,  
> `2ab = b`.

The nontrivial oriented solutions are

> `P_L = (I-h)/2`,  
> `P_R = (I+h)/2`.

Thus coherent orientation-faithful attachment is forced to a pure helicity projector: maximal chirality, the `V-A` form. The framework forces the pure-projector form but does not select which projector; left versus right is the remaining binary selection.

**Grade:** parity violation and maximal chirality / pure-projector form are **Registered**. The commutant closure is the Registered-candidate joint through which the projector argument runs.

## Boundary

This result derives the helicity operator algebra and the pure-projector form of a chiral coupling. It does not derive the exact one-generation fermion representation, left-doublet/right-singlet multiplicities, hypercharge embedding or assignments, electroweak mixing, or the number of generations. Those remain Open or conditional in their respective ledgers.
