# Lineage 1 — Physics registration

## 1. Concept

The control rule that determines when a licensed mathematical object may be used as a framework-native standing in the physics subdomain.

## 2. Physics capacity

Established physics vocabulary packages structures, empirical values, mathematical consequences, and theoretical interpretation under single names. The capacity required here is narrower: distinguish the content earned inside the physics lineage from mathematics-as-mathematics, empirical observation, and external comparison.

## 4. Native derivation target

A candidate has framework-native physical standing only when all five conditions hold:

1. From-whole inheritance;
2. satisfaction of the self-transforming / invariant / coherent physics-domain constraint;
3. stated From/With/This constitution;
4. participation in the physics lineage rather than formal resemblance alone;
5. an explicit boundary declaration separating framework derivation, licensed mathematics, empirical observation, and comparison/quarantine.

Physics adds no new kind of actuality to mathematics. It is the subdomain selected by these characteristics.

## 5. Derivation result

The rule is carried by the domain-entry method and is applied claim-by-claim throughout the current downstream ledgers. It performs exactly the measured capacity: admission to a domain lineage plus boundary control. It does not derive the substantive content of any later physics object.

### Adversarial pass

**Strongest objection:** the rule may be circular because “physics-subdomain participation” could amount to declaring a wanted mathematical object physical.

**Result:** the objection fails only because the participation clause is not sufficient alone. From-whole inheritance, the threefold domain constraint, vertical constitution, and the source/boundary declaration must all be independently exhibited. Formal resemblance fails the gate. The rule remains a control theorem, never a content generator.

**Countermodel retained:** a mathematically available object selected solely because it resembles a particle, charge, field, or gauge representation fails conditions 1, 3, and 4 even if it satisfies a convenient invariance.

## 6. Verdict

**PASS — Registered control rule.**

This PASS is exhaustive for the control load and deliberately non-transitive: it registers no mature particle, gauge, mass, charge, decay, or spacetime bundle. Every such content claim remains governed by its own lineage and grade.

Dependent pages may cite Item 1 as compact upstream control. They must still declare the exact registered properties of every mature name and preserve all local conditions, empirical boundaries, and quarantines.
