# Lineage 7 — Particle identity

## 1–4. Concept, capacity, load, and target

Particle names must function as property-indexed ledgers. The target is adjudication of the exact native role carried by each name, never derivation of its complete mature-physics bundle.

## 5. Ledger result

- **Quark:** externally resolved interior-moment role; marked color. Exact charges, quantitative QCD, later empirical flavors, and module content parked.
- **Lepton:** internally closed/colorless whole-role. Hypercharge, Yukawa, generations, and exact module parked.
- **Electron:** native minimal mass-ruler role at the grade established by Lineage 10. Exact charge value and EW bundle parked.
- **Proton/baryon:** complete threefold interior closure and the full bracketed mass relation `6π⁵(1+ε_FW)`; exact charge and QCD mechanism parked.
- **Neutron:** same color closure with `udd` mark profile; registered sign/mechanism, conjectured magnitude residues; beta mechanism and antineutrino identity parked.
- **Neutrino/antineutrino:** no fully Registered native particle role; missing-partner/From-With readings retain their local grades.

### Adversarial pass

**Strongest objection:** empirical naming can back-propagate unearned properties even if a ledger lists them separately, especially “electron” in the ruler and “proton” in the closure volume.

**Result:** the ledger survives only as a use rule: each occurrence must be substitutable by its licensed role-description without changing the argument. If it is not, the occurrence is an empirical label or smuggle. Lineage 10 must independently carry the ruler; the name does no proof work.

## 6. Verdict

**PASS as adjudication ledger.** No new particle bundle is registered.

## 7. Integration rule

A particle name licenses only its row. Preserve the bracketed proton/electron relation; never abbreviate it into an identification of `6π⁵` with the measured ratio.
