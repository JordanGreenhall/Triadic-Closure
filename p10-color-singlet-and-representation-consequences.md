# P10 Color, Singlet Closure, and Representation Consequences

P10 contains exactly:

1. **This-flattening color**;
2. **singlet closure**;
3. **allowed representation consequences**.

P10 does not normalize particle identities or native particle-role taxonomy. Quark, lepton, electron, proton, neutron, and neutrino roles belong to P13. P10 may state only representation-theoretic consequences without assigning those consequences to named particle kinds.

P10 inherits P8's one same-kind three-dimensional carrier at M6's selected complex-extension grade and P9's fixed alternating form and native `SU(3)` at their recorded grades. It does not re-prove either unit.

## 2. This-flattening color

The foundational This-flattening is **quality / internal distinction**: three co-equal, non-interchangeable aspects of one closure. In physics, the bounded mature-name registration is **color**.

Color therefore means, at P10 scope:

> the threefold qualitative interior distinction of one physical closure, carried on the P8 same-kind carrier and transformed by the P9 native `SU(3)` structure.

This is an internal, non-spatial distinction. It is not a mediation carrier, three spatial directions, three particles, three flavors, or three empirical labels. The aspects are symmetric in standing but non-interchangeable in qualitative role.

### Standing

- The This-flattening itself is inherited as a Secured Native foundation result.
- Its deployment on the P8/P9 internal carrier is Secured by construction within those inherited conditions.
- **Color** is Registered, not Sealed, for this exact internal-distinction core. Mature QCD surplus remains quarantined.

## 3. Singlet closure

P9 supplies a fixed nonzero alternating trilinear form

`epsilon : V x V x V -> C_C`

on the three-dimensional carrier `V`, at M6's selected-extension grade. P10 reads closure through the invariant scalar produced by that form.

A **color singlet** is a composite standing whose total color transformation is trivial: the whole is invariant under the P9 `SU(3)` action even though its constituents may transform nontrivially.

For three fundamental contributions, the alternating contraction

`epsilon(v1,v2,v3)`

is invariant under `SU(3)` because `det(g)=1`. This gives the canonical ternary singlet channel. The singlet is color-neutrality as completed internal closure, not absence of interior structure.

The conjugate-pair contraction between the fundamental and antifundamental carriers gives a second singlet channel when the inherited pairing and conjugate representation are deployed.

### Scope

P10 Secures the existence and algebraic form of these singlet channels. It does not by itself prove:

- that every physical standing must be a singlet;
- a dynamical confinement theorem;
- a unique singlet attractor under every admissible dynamics;
- flux tubes, an area law, a confining potential, or a confinement scale;
- the empirical hadron spectrum.

## 4. Allowed representation consequences

Once P9 supplies native `SU(3)`, standard finite-dimensional representation theory may be used as mathematics-as-mathematics at the inherited M6 grade. P10 records only consequences generated by the fundamental carrier, its conjugate, and their minimal tensor products.

### 4.1 Fundamental and antifundamental

- the P8 carrier transforms as the fundamental representation `3`;
- its conjugate dual transforms as the antifundamental representation `3-bar`;
- a single `3` or `3-bar` is non-singlet color content;
- `3` and `3-bar` are representation types, not yet particle names.

### 4.2 Fundamental–antifundamental product

`3 tensor 3-bar = 1 direct-sum 8`.

Consequences:

- one singlet channel is allowed;
- one adjoint octet channel is allowed;
- P10 licenses the decomposition, not a claim that every physical pair occupies either channel or that the octet is dynamically realized as a free standing.

### 4.3 Three-fundamental product

`3 tensor 3 tensor 3 = 1 direct-sum 8 direct-sum 8 direct-sum 10`.

Consequences:

- exactly one totally antisymmetric singlet channel occurs;
- two mixed-symmetry octet channels occur;
- one totally symmetric decuplet channel occurs;
- the decomposition is Sealed as representation mathematics given the P9 group and P8 carrier;
- physical occupancy, stability, mass, spin-flavor assignment, and particle naming are not supplied by the decomposition.

### 4.4 Pairwise color operator

For the standard pairwise `SU(3)` generators on the three-fundamental product, the familiar color operator has representation-dependent eigenvalues:

- singlet: `-2`;
- octet: `-1/2`;
- decuplet: `+1`.

This spectrum is an allowed representation consequence once the operator is explicitly selected. It does not follow from singlet closure alone, and its interpretation as binding energy, attraction, repulsion, or dynamics requires an additional physical law.
