# CH6 — Catenation, Branching, Isomerism, and Functional Groups

## Standing summary

CH6 owns the first recursive chemistry architecture after bonding-site differentiation: a completed bond product may retain engageable residual shell edges, allowing further closure by termination, chain extension, branching, or ring closure. Different closure topologies over the same composition support constitutionally distinct standings, and a local closure texture may be carried as the same functional kind across different larger composites.

The structural package is **Conjectured-strong and Unregistered as a completed physical chemistry theorem**. It states the correct framework-native form of catenation, branching, constitutional isomerism, and functional groups, but no explicit recursive occupant, persistence theorem, carbon-maximality proof, or quantitative group behavior is established.

## Unit boundary

CH6 owns:

1. residual-edge recursion after a bond product;
2. termination, chain extension, branching, and non-aromatic ring closure as topology classes;
3. composition/constitution separation;
4. constitutional isomerism as different closure topology under one composition record;
5. functional groups as carried local closure invariants.

CH6 does not own or derive:

- cyclic delocalization;
- ring-mode spectra;
- aromatic closure;
- the `4k+2` filling result;
- aromatic stabilization or resonance;
- CH7 or later chemistry;
- empirical functional-group tables, reaction rules, group additivity, substituent constants, or quantitative reactivity.

**Boundary rule:** a ring is a recursive topology. Aromaticity is a further cyclic-delocalization construction and belongs to CH7.

## Structural reconstruction

### Residual-edge recursion

A bond product may remain chemically open if one or more shell-edge capacities remain engageable. Reapplication of the joint-closure form can then produce:

- **termination:** no engageable residual edge remains in the selected passage class;
- **chain extension:** one residual edge is completed by a new partner while another remains available;
- **branching:** more than one residual edge remains available at a standing and supports distinct continuation sites;
- **ring closure:** a residual edge closes against an earlier standing in the same finite chain.

This is a topology of repeated closure, not yet a theorem that any physical element or molecule realizes it.

### Catenation

Catenation is the recursive capacity of a bonded standing to participate in another bond while preserving the prior closure sufficiently for the enlarged composite to stand. The proposal is **Conjectured-strong**. It requires explicit admitted occupants, preservation under extension, and a recursion criterion.

Carbon catenation is not derived merely because carbon is empirically familiar or because X1 names carbon as a candidate D7 provisioner. Comparative carbon maximality remains X1-owned and Open.

### Composition and constitution

A composition record counts or types constituents. A constitution record specifies how those constituents are connected by closure relations. Therefore one composition may admit multiple non-isomorphic closure graphs.

The separation between composition and constitution is **Conjectured-strong** as the framework-native basis of constitutional isomerism. It does not yet establish which graphs are admissible, persistent, or realized.

### Functional groups

A functional group is provisionally reconstructed as a local closure texture that:

1. is identifiable within more than one larger composite;
2. retains a specified local relational pattern under changes outside that neighborhood;
3. may persist through passages that do not alter its defining closure relations.

This makes functional-group identity a carried local invariant, not a substance inserted into molecules. The concept is **Conjectured-strong**. Empirical group names and reaction tables do not constitute the invariant, its preservation theorem, or its quantitative consequences.

## Smuggle-watches

1. **A chain drawing is not a recursive occupant.** Familiar structural formulas do not prove admissibility or persistence.
2. **Carbon catenates is not carbon maximality.** The comparative D7 claim remains Open under X1.
3. **A ring is not aromaticity.** Ring topology alone does not supply cyclic delocalization, ring modes, or `4k+2` closure.
4. **An empirical isomer list is not a topology theorem.** Constitution must be defined and admissibility established natively.
5. **A functional-group name is not a carried invariant.** Empirical classifications, reaction patterns, and additivity rules may not be imported as derived.

## Local research frontiers

### CH6-F1 — explicit recursive occupant

**Standing:** Open and Unregistered.

**Current strongest:** residual-edge recursion is structurally specified.

**Missing:** one admitted bond product with a persistent prior closure, an engageable residual edge, and one valid extension passage.

**Dependencies:** X1, CH1-F2, CH3-F3, CH5-F1.

**False-completion warning:** an empirical hydrocarbon chain is not the framework construction.

**Next legitimate research action:** construct the smallest finite standing that preserves one closure while completing a second.

### CH6-F2 — topology, constitution, and isomer admissibility

**Standing:** Conjectured-strong; physical realization Open.

**Current strongest:** composition and closure graph are distinguished.

**Missing:** admissible graph class, equivalence criterion, persistence rule, and two non-isomorphic occupants with one composition record.

**Dependencies:** CH6-F1 and CH4 geometry.

**False-completion warning:** graph enumeration alone does not establish chemical realization.

**Next legitimate research action:** define one finite composition and prove two admissible non-isomorphic closure topologies conditionally.

### CH6-F3 — functional-group invariant and carriage

**Standing:** Open and Unregistered beyond the concept.

**Current strongest:** local closure texture is selected as the candidate invariant.

**Missing:** neighborhood definition, equivalence relation, preservation class, and one passage that changes the host while preserving the local texture.

**Dependencies:** CH6-F1, CH6-F2, and later passage work.

**False-completion warning:** empirical reaction regularity is not an invariant proof.

**Next legitimate research action:** specify one local closure subgraph and one host-changing passage under which it is preserved.

## Downstream use rule

Later chemistry may use catenation, branching, constitution, isomerism, and functional groups only with their Conjectured status attached. CH6 may provide ring topology to CH7, but it provides no cyclic delocalization, aromatic closure, ring-mode spectrum, `4k+2` result, or stabilization theorem.

## Bottom line

CH6 is the recursive-constitution unit: catenation, branching, isomerism, and functional groups. Its architecture is strongly specified, while realization, persistence, comparative maximality, and quantitative chemistry remain unfinished. CH7 is still owed in full.
