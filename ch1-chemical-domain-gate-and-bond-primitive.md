# CH1 — Chemical Domain Gate and Bond Primitive

## Standing summary

CH1 owns the first chemistry-form claim after X1: the selected candidate boundary and the bond as the candidate D1 joint standing. It does not override X1's finding that the framework atomic ground, shell-edge object, boundary validity, and framework nonemptiness remain unconstructed.

The strongest current bond formulation is:

> A bond is one composite joint standing in which residual shell-edge relations of the participants terminate in the composite; it is not an external connector between independently complete substances.

This formulation is **Conjectured-strong as the selected candidate chemistry-D1 form**, conditional on construction of the X1 atomic/shell ground and proof that the selected shell-edge constraint is valid and nonempty. It is **Unregistered as an established chemistry occupant** until those antecedents are discharged.

The ordinary-chemistry passage class provisionally preserves nuclear/element role identity while electronic shell-edge and charge relations are reconfigured. This is a selected and Conjectured scope condition, not a quantitative scale-separation or Born–Oppenheimer theorem.

## Unit boundary

CH1 owns only:

1. the relation between X1's candidate shell-edge constraint and chemistry D1;
2. the bond as a candidate joint standing;
3. the distinction between a selected D1 form and an established occupant;
4. the nuclear-role-preserving passage scope needed to state the candidate chemistry domain.

CH1 excludes:

- CH2 two-center capacity, occupancy, electron ownership, bonding/antibonding, and n-center modes;
- CH3 maintenance-relief and quantitative energy;
- molecular geometry, covalent/ionic interpolation, electronegativity, catenation, aromaticity, reactions, kinetics, thermodynamics, and catalysis;
- new atomic-shell, spectral, Born–Oppenheimer, or quantitative bonding research.

CH2 has not begun.

## Candidate boundary and D1 relation

The selected candidate boundary is:

> admit persistent physical configurations whose residual electronic-shell relations can be jointly completed without changing the nuclear-role identity of the participating standings.

The corresponding candidate D1 form is the bond as one joint standing. The form answers what a chemistry-D1 occupant would be if the candidate boundary is established. It does not itself establish:

- a framework-native atom or shell decomposition;
- a residual shell-edge object;
- validity of joint completion;
- framework nonemptiness;
- a concrete admitted bond occupant.

Accordingly, CH1 may preserve the structural form at Conjectured-strong while withholding registration of an actual chemistry D1 occupant.

## Adversarial distinctions

1. **Candidate form is not established occupant.** A coherent answer to what the bond is does not prove that the framework has constructed one.
2. **Gate failure is not form refutation.** X1's unfilled antecedents limit standing; they do not supply a substantive counterexample to joint standing.
3. **Empirical molecule is not framework nonemptiness.** Recognition data cannot replace the native atomic/shell map.
4. **Signed valence is not a shell edge.** P15's bounded relational orientation does not construct shell capacity or residual occupancy.
5. **Joint standing does not settle CH2.** Capacity, occupancy, ownership, and bonding/antibonding remain separately owned.
6. **Nuclear-role preservation is not Born–Oppenheimer.** The selected passage scope carries no derived timescale, parameter, or error theorem.
