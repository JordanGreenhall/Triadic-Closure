# CH8 — Reaction-Site Taxonomy and Acid/Base Readings

## Standing summary

CH8 owns the structural classification of chemical passages by the site re-apportioned: shell-edge re-partnering, charge transfer, or a mixed passage involving both. It also owns the proposed Brønsted/Lewis unification as two site-readings of closure transfer.

The package is **Conjectured-strong and Unregistered as a completed passage theory**. No explicit framework passage occupant, completeness theorem, mechanism, rate, or quantitative acid/base scale is established.

## Unit boundary

CH8 owns:

1. shell-edge re-partnering passages;
2. charge-transfer passages;
3. mixed passages involving both sites;
4. Brønsted acid/base as the charge-site reading;
5. Lewis acid/base as the shell-edge-site reading.

CH8 does not own or derive:

- no-transmutation or conservation ledgers;
- oxidation-state bookkeeping;
- CH6 functional groups or carried local invariants;
- CH7 cyclic delocalization or aromatic closure;
- CH9 activation ridges, rates, exponential propensity, or catalysis;
- CH10 free-energy direction, multiplicity, equilibrium, or detailed balance;
- mechanisms, pKa values, solvent leveling, redox potentials, or empirical reaction catalogues.

## Passage taxonomy by site

### Shell-edge re-partnering

A shell-edge passage changes which partners jointly complete an engageable edge. Familiar substitution, addition, and elimination families may be tested against this category only after explicit passage constructions exist; their textbook names do not supply the native class.

### Charge transfer

A charge-site passage transfers or relocates a charge edge between standings. This is a structural transfer category only. CH8 does not own oxidation-number notation, electrochemical scales, or a quantitative redox theory.

### Mixed passages

A mixed passage changes both shell-edge partnership and charge-site apportionment. The category prevents forcing empirically coupled passages into an artificial one-site classification.

No completeness theorem has been proved. The three-way partition is the selected candidate taxonomy.

## Acid/base readings

### Brønsted face

Brønsted acid/base is reconstructed as the charge-site reading of a passage involving transfer of H+ as the limiting shell-less closure. This does not derive proton mobility, pKa, solvent behavior, or a kinetic mechanism.

### Lewis face

Lewis acid/base is reconstructed as the shell-edge-site reading in which a held pair is donated into an engageable edge. Donor/acceptor ordering is structural here, not a derived temporal mechanism.

### One architecture, two readings

Brønsted and Lewis descriptions are proposed as two site-readings within one passage architecture. This does not assert that every empirical acid/base process is exhausted by one simple microscopic path.

## Smuggle-watches

1. **Reaction names are not the taxonomy.** Textbook labels cannot reverse-install the site partition.
2. **Transfer is not oxidation-state bookkeeping.** CH8 classifies the passage site; it does not own oxidation numbers.
3. **Acid/base labels are not scales or mechanisms.** pKa, solvent leveling, proton affinity, and kinetic routes remain outside CH8.
4. **A classification is not an occupant.** Balanced equations and familiar reactions do not construct framework passages.
5. **CH8 is not dynamics.** Barrier, rate, and catalyst-cycle claims belong to CH9.

## Local research frontiers

**False-completion warning:** a substitution equation is not the framework construction.

### CH8-F2 — explicit charge-transfer and mixed occupants

**Standing:** Open and Unregistered.

**Missing:** one admitted charge-transfer passage and one admitted mixed passage in a common representation.

**False-completion warning:** oxidation-number changes do not themselves exhibit the transfer.

### CH8-F3 — taxonomy completeness and acid/base realization

**Standing:** taxonomy Conjectured-strong; completeness and realization Open.

**Missing:** a formal passage representation, a decomposition theorem, one explicit Brønsted case, and one explicit Lewis case.

**False-completion warning:** empirical acid/base classifications do not prove the native unification.

## Downstream use rule

CH9 may use the CH8 site classes only as Conjectured structural passage types. It may not cite CH8 as deriving actual passage occurrence, an activation barrier, a rate form, a catalyst cycle, oxidation-state bookkeeping, conservation ledgers, or thermodynamic direction.

## Bottom line

CH8 is the reaction-site taxonomy unit: edge re-partnering, transfer, mixed passages, and Brønsted/Lewis site-readings. Everything beyond that boundary remains outside CH8.
