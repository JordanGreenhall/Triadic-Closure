# P18 Closure-Inherited Metric and 2π^5 Measure

## Scope

P18 owns metric selection for the color closure, normalization of the resulting `SU(3)` closure measure, and the claim that the measure is physically paid by the standing whose closure is being maintained.

P18 does not own the electron ruler, the spatial factor `3`, `6π^5`, the proton/electron mass ratio, baryon splitting, decay, or external-unit calibration. Those belong to P19–P21 or later value machinery.

## Metric selection

The framework already has a primitive Hermitian pairing on standings. That pairing is the admissible metric source for a physical closure. The relevant object is therefore not an abstract copy of the operator group equipped with an independently selected trace metric. It is the color standing and its determinant-preserving completion as one closure.

For a normalized standing in `C^3`, the unit standing-space is the round `S^5`. Completing the standing to a determinant-preserving frame contributes the round `S^3`. Hence

> `Vol(SU(3))_closure = Vol(S^5) × Vol(S^3) = π^3 × 2π^2 = 2π^5`.

Framework state: **Secured relative to the P8 pairing and P9 determinant-preserving closure construction**.

Semantic registration: **Registered, not Sealed** as the closure-inherited physical metric and measure.

## Why the trace metric is not the governing metric

The abstract trace metric yields `√3 π^5`. It measures the operator group as an abstract manifold. P18 instead measures the physical standing under the only metric source supplied by the framework: the pairing borne by standings.

The discrepancy is localized in the determinant-balancing direction

> `λ8 = diag(1,1,-2)/√3`.

Under the abstract trace metric, that direction is counted as an independent operator direction. Under closure inheritance, `det = 1` makes it the dependent completion required to close the frame. The same determinant condition that selects `SU(3)` therefore removes the freedom to choose the trace normalization for the physical closure.

This localization and the resulting ratio between the two measures are **Sealed mathematics given the two metric conventions**. The selection of the closure-inherited convention is **Registered, not Sealed**, because it depends on the framework claim that physical payment follows the standing's inherited pairing rather than an abstract group metric.

## Physical payment

A closure measure is physically paid only when that closure is actually borne by a standing. The `2π^5` is therefore one nontrivial color-closure payment. It is not yet a particle mass and is not automatically multiplied by spatial dimension.

Physical payment at P18 means:

1. the measure belongs to an actual maintained closure rather than to an abstract symmetry space;
2. a trivial color representation contributes no nontrivial color-orbit measure;
3. later mass accounting may spend this measure only once and only where the color closure is actually present.

This claim is **Registered at qualitative accounting scope**. P18 does not derive a complete quantitative mass law.

## Confinement boundary

Structural colored non-closure from P16 prevents treating free color as an independently physical abstract group orbit. That supports the closure rather than operator-group object as the thing measured. It does not derive flux-tube confinement, a linear potential, a mass gap, or any quantitative QCD dynamics.
