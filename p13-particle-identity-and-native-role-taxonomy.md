# P13 — Particle Identity and Native Role Taxonomy

## Controlling specification

P13 contains exactly the native roles named **quark, lepton, electron, proton, neutron, and neutrino**.

P13 does not derive flavor or mark geometry (P14), charge or hypercharge (P15), quantitative QCD dynamics (P16), mass ontology or ratios (P17–P20), beta-decay mechanism (P21), or electroweak breaking, generations, and spectrum (P29).

## 1. Particle is a role-name, not a primitive substance

The framework does not begin from independently existing, swappable particle-substances. A particle name is licensed only when an already-constructed physical standing occupies a repeatable relational role. Identity is therefore the persistence of the role-defining invariant across the declared passage-class, not bare numerical individuality.

P3 supplies the fermionic or bosonic holding mode. P8–P12 supply carrier, color, gauge-source, spinorial, and chirality structures at their recorded grades. P13 asks which bounded combinations license the six inherited role-names without importing their later quantitative properties.

## 2. Quark and lepton

### Quark

A **quark** is a fermionic standing whose This-interior is color-resolved: it occupies a nontrivial color-bearing role rather than a complete color-neutral closure.

This licenses the quark name at the color-bearing constituent-role core. It does not yet specify flavor, charge, mass, generation, confinement dynamics, or a free asymptotic particle.

**Standing:** Registered, not Sealed.

### Lepton

A **lepton** is a fermionic standing whose This-interior is color-trivial: no externally resolved color distinction is carried by the standing.

Color-triviality is the positive role criterion. It does not mean metaphysical absence of the triad, and it does not by itself specify electric charge, weak representation, flavor, mass, or generation.

**Standing:** Registered, not Sealed.

### Split

The quark/lepton split is therefore the distinction between a color-resolved fermionic role and a color-trivial fermionic role. It is not a substance partition and does not yet reproduce the complete Standard Model matter module.

## 3. Electron and neutrino

### Electron

The **electron** is the stable, color-trivial fermionic exterior-standing that can serve as the minimal charged-lepton role once P15 supplies charge. At P13 scope the licensed core is:

- fermionic;

- persistent at the inherited bounded witness scope;

P13 does not derive its charge value, mass, native-ruler status, magnetic moment, generation assignment, or exact electroweak module. Those retain P15, P19, and P29 ownership.

**Standing:** Registered for the bounded role-core; later quantitative identity remains owned downstream.

### Neutrino

The **neutrino** is the color-trivial fermionic role with no established outward charge edge at P13 scope and with chiral/From–With affinity inherited only as a proposed role-reading.

The color-trivial neutral role is Registered as a bounded taxonomy entry. The stronger identification of neutrino as the near-pure From–With or chiral-receipt standing remains Conjectured. P13 does not derive neutrino mass, oscillation, generation count, handed module, or beta-decay production.

**Standing:** Registered for the neutral color-trivial role; Conjectured for the specific From–With/chiral reading.

## 4. Proton and neutron

P10 and the modeled With-to-This closure supply a color-singlet three-factor composite role. P13 may name bounded baryonic roles but may not spend P14 marks, P15 charge values, P17–P20 mass structure, or P21 decay backward as their warrant.

### Proton

The **proton** is the persistent color-singlet fermionic composite role that later receives the outward-relievable valence and native stable-ruler determinations.

At P13 scope, proton identity is Registered only as the stable nucleon-role within the modeled color-singlet closure class. Its exact mark configuration, charge, mass ratio, and stability mechanism retain P14, P15, P19, and P20 ownership.

### Neutron

The **neutron** is the alternate nucleon-role within the same color-singlet fermionic composite class, distinguished from the proton by later mark/valence structure rather than by color-singlet form itself.

At P13 scope, neutron identity is Registered as the companion nucleon-role. Its exact mark configuration, neutral charge, mass excess, metastability, and decay products retain P14, P15, P20, and P21 ownership.

### Nucleon boundary

P13 therefore Registers two named nucleon roles sharing one color-singlet closure type while explicitly leaving the positive construction of their difference to P14 and P15. Their empirical distinguishability is corroborative; it is not used to manufacture the missing mark or charge derivation.
