# Domain Entry Method

This is the sole generic control for entering a successor domain. It governs how the living prior closure is taken into a new domain without importing that domain's unexamined metaphysics. Physics is a worked instance, not the definition of the method.

Read this after [Architectonic Rigor](architectonic-rigor-complete.md), [The Triadic Structure of Relating](triadic-structure-of-relating-rev-canonical.md), [Claim Standing and Warrant](claim-status-vocabulary.md), and [Vertical, Horizontal, and Recursion](vertical-and-horizontal.md). The domain-specific walk begins only after the entry ledger below is opened.

## 1. What domain entry is

A domain is a closure at domain scope: the living inherited whole is read under a selecting constraint, and that constraint closes a determinate territory of the horizontal build. A domain is therefore neither a subject label nor merely a procedure.

The inherited whole does not select its successor by itself. The successor constraint supplies the boundary condition under which the inherited whole is tested and deployed. The first domain closure is constructed at their conjunction; it is not assumed in advance and it may remain Open if no adequate construction is available.

Domain entry is distinct from corpus adjudication. Adjudication establishes which formulations and characteristics are living. Entry may inherit only that adjudicated whole; it may not revive an earlier formulation merely because that formulation is convenient for the target domain.

## 2. The non-importation rule

A successor-domain walk must not allow the target discipline's ordinary ontology to carry inferential load before that ontology has been reconstructed and graded.

This is a rule against **unearned load**, not a wholesale rejection of the target discipline. Empirical observations, successful formal relations, and ordinary domain names may be used when their roles are declared separately:

- an observation can constrain or test a construction without serving as its architectonic premise;
- mathematics can be used as mathematics without converting a mathematical availability into a framework derivation;
- a domain name can be attached only after its content and the constructed content have been compared at the standing claimed;
- an inherited formulation can survive if the reconstruction shows that it carries no unearned surplus in the relevant scope.

Circular redescription is the characteristic failure: the walk imports a target-domain concept and later announces that it has derived that same concept.

## 3. Presupposition clarification

For every load-bearing target-domain concept:

1. **State the ordinary concept precisely.** Identify both its successful content and the surplus metaphysical load it ordinarily imports.
2. **Suspend the surplus.** Do not let the unearned part serve as a premise. Preserve empirical and mathematical content under their own warrants.
3. **Rebuild from the living inherited whole.** Use every relevant registered characteristic, not a hand-picked toolkit. From / With / This, the modal triad, quantity and quality, registration, `J`, `rho`, and later structures are available only insofar as they have already been earned and are relevant.
4. **Test a concrete case.** The case must be rich enough to expose ambiguity, hidden imports, or failure.
5. **Test the inherited problem.** Ask whether it dissolves, survives in framework-native form, or reveals a genuine missing dependency. Dissolution is one possible result, not a result to presume.
6. **Grade the reconstruction.** Record its standing, warrant route, dependency chain, scope, and any remaining surplus.
7. **Govern only the earned scope.** Once adjudicated, the reconstructed concept replaces the unexamined formulation only for the claims it actually resolves and remains defeasible under later repair.

A successful clarification removes imported pseudo-problems where appropriate, preserves genuine problems without disguising them, and supports downstream construction without reintroducing the suspended premise.

## 4. The inherited whole and the successor constraint

The From of a successor domain is the full living prior closure. It is not a buffet of optional handles.

Every living and relevant characteristic of that closure constrains the successor walk unless an explicit dependency or scope analysis shows that it is inapplicable. Superseded, merely historical, or unresolved characteristics do not become live premises by inheritance.

The successor constraint must be stated independently of the result it is supposed to select. It must specify:

- the boundary that distinguishes the proposed domain;
- why the proposed contents fall under that boundary;
- how the inherited characteristics remain jointly coherent there; and
- what would fail the cut.

The constraint selects the domain; it does not by itself prove any named occupancy within the domain.

## 5. Constructing the first closure

The first closure is the minimal content that survives the inherited whole under the successor constraint.

1. List the living inherited characteristics relevant at the boundary.
2. State the successor constraint without using the desired first occupant as a premise.
3. Construct candidates forward from the conjunction of the two.
4. Reject candidates that fail any applicable inherited characteristic or any term of the successor constraint.
5. Show that the survivor closes as one content in the foundational sense of closure.
6. Separate forced form, residual freedom, empirical occupancy, and named recognition.
7. Assign each resulting claim its own standing and warrant route.

Failure to obtain a unique occupant is not permission to choose one. Record the remaining fork, missing premise, or empirical bridge and leave the occupancy at the corresponding standing.

## 6. The three moves at every stage

These moves apply generically after entry. Domain-specific portfolios and examples belong in the relevant domain walk.

### Move 1 — Dependency position

Maintain a dependency portfolio of essential forms. Given what currently stands, identify which forms have their prerequisites satisfied. This identifies candidates for construction, not conclusions. For each absent candidate, record the exact missing dependency and its prospective home.

### Move 2 — Forward composition

Ask what the standing actuals compose into under the living operations. Search is bounded by the dependency portfolio, but every conclusion must be constructed forward. A known mature result may orient attention; it may not be reverse-fitted into the stage.

### Move 3 — Standing and dependency record

For every surfaced result, record:

- its live standing and distinct warrant route;
- its essential form versus later accreted or mature content;
- the premises on which it depends;
- what it now enables; and
- what remains missing, Open, or merely Conjectured.

The three moves cross-check one another: dependency position prevents aimless search, forward composition prevents reverse placement, and the record makes downstream failure traceable.

## 7. Essential form and mature form

Realizability at a stage is tested against an essential form: the least content needed for the structure in question. A mature domain concept may include content accumulated across later stages.

The strip-down test asks which content can be removed while the structure remains the kind of thing claimed. Removed content is not discarded; it is placed at the stage where its dependency becomes available. A familiar mature name must not be sealed onto an essential form unless the bidirectional content-identity test succeeds at the stated scope.

The generic sequence of stages and the maturation of closures are governed separately by the D-series lineage. This method supplies the entry and execution discipline; it does not prejudge a universal D-stage occupancy.

## 8. Required entry ledger

Every domain entry must publish a ledger with these fields:

| Field | Required content |
|---|---|
| Prior closure | canonical source and living characteristics inherited |
| Exclusions | superseded, historical, unresolved, or irrelevant characteristics not used |
| Successor constraint | boundary, inclusion test, and failure test |
| Presuppositions | ordinary content, surplus load, and reconstruction status |
| First closure | forward construction, rejected alternatives, and residual freedom |
| Concrete tests | observations or examples and the role each plays |
| Standing | one live standing per claim, separate from its warrant route |
| Dependencies | upstream premises, downstream enablements, and missing premises |
| Non-importation audit | target-domain content used and the warrant that permits each use |

## 9. Standing of the generic method

| Claim | Standing | Warrant and limit |
|---|---|---|
| Do not use unexamined target-domain metaphysics as a premise | Registered | Methodological repair supported by repeated failure analysis; not an ontological theorem about a target domain |
| Inherit the living relevant prior closure as a whole | Registered | Required to prevent selective inheritance; relevance still requires explicit scope analysis |
| A successor constraint selects a domain closure | Registered | Framework-native extension of closure to domain scope; does not establish any particular successor constraint |
| Use presupposition clarification and the three moves | Registered | Operational method exhibited in more than one domain walk; results remain claim-sensitive |
| A particular first closure or named domain occupancy | Open until constructed | Must be graded in its own lineage; the generic method supplies no occupancy for free |
| Dissolution of a particular inherited problem | Open until tested | Dissolution cannot be presumed from the method |

No generic claim in this document is Registered and Sealed. Physics-specific readings—including self-transforming / invariant / coherent as the physics cut and the three office-readings of physical causation—remain in the physics entry and must be graded there.
