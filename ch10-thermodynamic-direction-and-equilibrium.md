# CH10 — Thermodynamic Direction and Equilibrium

## Standing summary

CH10 owns the specified thermodynamic unit: comparative direction under a stated provision, reversible passage balance, equilibrium as no net passage bias, and the conditional relation between a dimensionless maintenance difference and an equilibrium ratio.

The structural reconstruction is **Conjectured-strong and Unregistered**. The exponential equilibrium form is **Secured conditionally** once a common provision measure, reversible passage pair, and exponential weighting rule are supplied. No numerical thermodynamic scale, entropy functional, free-energy calibration, equilibrium constant, or equation of state is derived.

## Unit boundary

CH10 owns:

1. thermodynamic direction as comparative settlement under stated provision;
2. reversible forward and reverse passage weighting;
3. equilibrium as equality of opposing realized passage propensities and therefore no net passage bias;
4. the conditional exponential ratio form for opposing passage weights.

CH10 does not own:

- CH8 reaction-site taxonomy or acid/base readings;

## Thermodynamic direction

Let `A` and `B` be two admitted chemical standings connected by a reversible passage pair under one fixed provision `Theta`. Let `M_A(Theta)` and `M_B(Theta)` denote their comparative closure-maintenance burdens in that provision.

The candidate thermodynamic direction is toward the standing with the smaller maintained burden under the same provision. This is a comparative settlement claim. It does not say that the passage occurs, how quickly it occurs, or which kinetic route is used.

Define

`Delta M_{A->B}(Theta) = M_B(Theta) - M_A(Theta)`.

Then the structural sign rule is:

- `Delta M < 0`: settlement toward `B` is favored;
- `Delta M > 0`: settlement toward `A` is favored;
- `Delta M = 0`: neither side is favored by the comparative maintenance ledger.

The existence and physical evaluation of `M` remain Open because CH3's native maintenance functional and quantitative scale are unfinished.

## Reversible passage balance

For one reversible pair,

`A <-> B`,

let `w_{A->B}` and `w_{B->A}` denote realized passage weights under the same provision. These weights are not CH9 rate constants by definition; they are the opposed settlement weights relevant to the stationary population balance.

A stationary equilibrium requires

`w_{A->B} P_A = w_{B->A} P_B`,

where `P_A` and `P_B` are the settled shares or occupation weights of the two standings in the stated ensemble description.

Thus

`P_B / P_A = w_{A->B} / w_{B->A}`.

This balance equation is conditional on a legitimate ensemble or repeated-settlement representation. It does not by itself derive one.

## Conditional exponential equilibrium form

Suppose the same dimensionless provision measure `Theta > 0` exponentially weights comparative maintenance burden, so that a standing with burden `M` receives relative weight proportional to

`exp(-M/Theta)`.

Then

`P_B / P_A = exp[-(M_B - M_A)/Theta] = exp(-Delta M/Theta)`.

This exponential ratio is **Secured conditionally** from the stated weighting assumption. CH10 does not derive the weighting law, identify `Theta` with `k_B T`, calibrate units, or import empirical partition functions.

The sign consequence is exact within the conditional model:

- if `Delta M < 0`, then `P_B/P_A > 1`;
- if `Delta M > 0`, then `P_B/P_A < 1`;
- if `Delta M = 0`, then `P_B/P_A = 1`.

## Equilibrium

Equilibrium is the standing in which opposing realized passage propensities balance so that no net passage bias remains at the population level.

This does not mean:

- passages cease;
- forward and reverse microscopic routes are identical;
- kinetic barriers vanish;
- the two standings have equal populations;
- the system has reached the globally least possible burden across every conceivable standing.

Equal population follows only when the opposing weights are equal. Dynamic passage may continue while the aggregate balance is stationary.

## Separation from CH9

CH9 asks whether a passage route is kinetically accessible and with what comparative propensity. CH10 asks which side is favored in maintained settlement and what stationary ratio follows under reversible balance.

A catalyst can alter CH9 route burdens and accelerate approach to equilibrium while leaving the CH10 equilibrium ratio unchanged, provided it restores and supplies alternate routes for both directions without altering the maintained endpoint comparison or external provision.

This invariance is conditional. A real catalyst that changes provision, binds one endpoint, participates in coupled reactions, or is not restored falls outside the ideal statement.

## Smuggle-watches

1. **Direction is not rate.** A favored settlement may be kinetically inaccessible on the relevant timescale.
2. **Equilibrium is not stasis.** Opposing passages may continue while net bias is zero.
3. **Exponential notation is not calibration.** `k_B`, temperature units, partition functions, and empirical constants are not imported.
4. **Maintenance difference is not automatically Gibbs free energy.** The identification requires further construction.
5. **Catalysts do not move the ideal equilibrium merely by lowering activation.** Endpoint/provision changes are a different case.
6. **Balanced equations do not establish equilibrium.** A reversible notation is not a stationary population theorem.
