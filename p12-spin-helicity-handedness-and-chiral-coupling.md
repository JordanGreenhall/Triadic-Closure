## 1. Spin and spinors

Given the oriented spatial rotation structure, the double cover permits spinorial representations. A `2π` rotation may act nontrivially on a spinor while a `4π` rotation returns it to itself. This is the essential spinorial consequence of the lift.

At P12 scope:

- availability of spinorial representations of `Spin(3)` is Sealed mathematics;
- physical deployment of a spinor representation on a stated carrier is Secured conditionally;
- the familiar name **spin-1/2** is Registered for the minimal two-component rotation-lift core;
- no particle species, magnetic moment, spin-statistics theorem, or weak doublet follows.

The two-component spinor is a representation of the spin cover. It is not thereby an internal weak-isospin doublet.

## 2. Helicity

For a state with nonzero spatial momentum `p`, helicity is the projection of spin along the momentum direction:

\[
h = \frac{\mathbf S\cdot \mathbf p}{|\mathbf p|}.
\]

The definition is Sealed mathematics once spin and nonzero momentum are available. Its physical use inherits the P5/P7 carrier and momentum-direction conditions.

Helicity is frame-sensitive for massive states because a boost can reverse the momentum relative to spin. For massless states, helicity is invariant under proper orthochronous Lorentz transformations. P12 records these standard representation consequences without identifying any particle or interaction.

## 3. Handedness / chirality

Chirality is the decomposition of Lorentz-spinor representation content into left- and right-handed sectors. In four-dimensional Lorentzian representation theory, the two Weyl sectors are inequivalent complex representations exchanged by parity.

P12 separates three notions:

- **spin:** representation of the rotation/Lorentz spin cover;
- **helicity:** spin projected along momentum;
- **chirality:** left/right Lorentz representation type.

For massless states, helicity and chirality correlate under the conventional particle/antiparticle orientation assignments. For massive states they are not identical.

The left/right decomposition is Sealed as representation mathematics given the P4/P5 Lorentz structure and an admitted spinor calculus. Physical population of one or both sectors remains a separate claim.

## 4. Chiral coupling

A coupling is orientation-faithful when it distinguishes the two chiral sectors; it is vectorial when it treats them symmetrically. This criterion is Registered for the displayed structural distinction.

P12 does **not** derive that nature must select only one chirality, nor does it derive maximal parity violation or the exact `V−A` weak interaction from lifted rotatability alone. Those stronger claims require an independently established internal weak gauge action and matter representation.

Accordingly:

- chiral/vectorial distinction — Sealed as representation structure and Registered in physical language;
- availability of orientation-faithful coupling — Secured as a possible coupling form;
- actual weak chiral coupling — Open pending the internal weak-source and matter-module bridge;
- maximal chirality / exact `V−A` — Open;
- exact left-doublet/right-singlet module — Open and later-unit constrained.
