## Exact conditional algebra and countermodels

### Local lapse algebra

### Boundary scaling example

For a selected `L x L x L` cubical region cut across one face, the number of nearest-neighbor edges crossing that face is exactly `L^2`, while the number of vertices is `L^3`. This exhibits boundary rather than volume scaling in one model. It does not prove the claim for a recovered P7 carrier, arbitrary horizon cut, or continuum area.

### Carrier versus boost

The rotation generator

`J=[[0,-1],[1,0]]`

satisfies `J^2=-I`; its exponential is compact after the selected normalization. The boost generator

`K=[[0,1],[1,0]]`

satisfies `K^2=I`; its exponential is noncompact. The former preserves `x^2+y^2`, the latter `x^2-y^2`. These facts discriminate forms but do not identify the physical thermal flow.

### Conditional coefficient cancellation

For nonzero `kappa`, the selected premises give

`dS=(1/T)dM=(2 pi/kappa)(kappa/(8 pi))dA=(1/4)dA`.

This is exact algebra and no more.

### Conservation is not unitarity

A growth map `i:C^n -> C^(n+1)`, `i(v)=(v,0)`, preserves all prior coordinates and the standard norm, yet it is not surjective and therefore is not a unitary operator between one fixed state space and itself. Even stronger, monotone conservation alone supplies no complex vector space or linear map. Thus framework distinction conservation cannot discharge the unitarity claim by naming.
