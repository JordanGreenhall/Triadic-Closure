## Seal adjudication (2026-06-21)

Worked as Item 1 of [physics-domain-work-plan](physics-domain-work-plan.md). Net grade: **Registered, not sealed** (confidence >90% in the grade). The three gates resolve unevenly.

**Gate 2 — exact `|ψ|²` from the pairing (conditional; prior grade corrected).** Given a selected M6 rule calculus closed under the complex scalar operations and a separately licensed positive-definite Hermitian physics pairing, Gleason's theorem plus the frame-function argument forces the quadratic form `C(k)=|h(σ,k)|²` on dimension ≥ 3. M5 constructs a perfect rational bilinear form; M6 gives a non-degenerate Hermitian extension only at that calculus grade, and neither unit supplies positivity. The earlier claim that pairing-origin and conservation already fixed the required positive bilinear therefore spent unearned surplus. Gate 2 remains conditional on rule-calculus closure, the positive-definite pairing construction, and the dimension ≥ 3 restriction.

**Gate 1 — frequency = weight (Open; the blocker).** Identifying the realizability weight with an observed long-run frequency is exactly the Born-rule-from-frequency problem. Nothing in the closure machinery forces a frequentist reading of `C(k)`: the law fixes a *measure*, not that repeated actualizations sample it. This is the load-bearing gap and is not closed.

**Gate 3 — one non-physics end-to-end instance (not done).** The Boltzmann/statistical-mechanics instance that would show the weighting law operating outside the quantum setting was not carried out.

**Verdict.** Gate 1 remains Open, Gate 3 remains undone, and Gate 2 is mathematically strong only after its positive-definite pairing input is separately licensed. The overall grade stays **Registered, not sealed**, now with both the frequency bridge and the positive-pairing dependency explicit.
