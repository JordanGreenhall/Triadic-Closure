# CH3 — Maintenance Relief and Energetic Ordering

## Standing summary

CH3 owns the proposed structural interpretation of chemical stabilization and the unresolved energetic ordering of the CH2 mode fork.

The strongest current formulation is:

> Chemical bonding is proposed as a joint standing whose realized texture relieves closure-maintenance load relative to the relevant separated alternative; antibonding is the corresponding mode whose texture does not provide that relief or increases the comparative maintenance burden.

This is **Conjectured as a structural interpretation** and **Unregistered as a derived quantitative chemistry law**. CH2 supplies a symmetric/antisymmetric mode decomposition, but symmetry type alone does not determine energetic order. No framework-native chemical maintenance functional, coupling, normalization, binding-energy scale, or comparison theorem is currently constructed.

## Unit boundary

CH3 owns only:

1. maintenance relief as the selected non-teleological interpretation of chemical stabilization;
2. the relation between the CH2 mode fork and the proposed bonding/antibonding reading;
3. the missing native comparison functional;
4. the boundary between structural interpretation and numerical energy.

CH3 excludes CH4 molecular geometry, CH5 covalent/ionic and electronegativity, later chemistry, and new quantitative electronic-structure research.

## Authority and lineage

- P17 governs mass as closure maintenance at its bounded structural scope. It does not supply a chemical interaction functional.
- CH1 governs the candidate bond as one joint standing.
- CH2 governs capacity, ownership status, and the exact conditional two-center mode decomposition.
- CHEM-006 retains maintenance relief as a structural interpretation and explicitly leaves quantitative energy scale Open.
- The CH3 authority record preserves these scopes without reading energy ordering backward into CH2.

## Structural interpretation

“Relief” is comparative, not teleological. It means that, under a still-owed common functional, one admitted joint texture would carry less closure-maintenance load than a specified separated or rival texture. The framework does not claim that nature pursues a goal or that every passage globally minimizes one scalar.

A valid comparison requires:

- a common admitted state/configuration space;
- a framework-native maintenance or interaction functional;
- a specified reference configuration;
- a rule connecting the functional to passage admissibility or persistence;
- normalization and coupling where magnitudes are claimed.

These are not currently jointly available.

## Mode fork and energetic labels

CH2 conditionally Secures the two modes

`|+> = (|A> + |B>)/sqrt(2)` and `|-> = (|A> - |B>)/sqrt(2)`

for two interchangeable center basis states under linearity and inner-product premises. This algebra does not determine which mode has lower maintenance load.

Therefore:

- “symmetric” may not be silently replaced by “bonding” as a theorem;
- “antisymmetric” may not be silently replaced by “antibonding” as a theorem;
- node count, sign pattern, or exchange parity alone does not provide the missing physical functional;
- conventional energetic ordering may be used only as external comparison until natively derived.

## Adversarial distinctions

1. **Interpretation is not functional.** Maintenance language does not itself assign values.
2. **Symmetry is not energetic order.** Exchange parity supplies no sign for an interaction difference.
3. **Relative relief is not absolute energy.** A comparison does not calibrate joules or electronvolts.
4. **Persistence is not minimum.** A standing may be metastable; persistence does not prove global optimality.
5. **Observed bond energy is not native warrant.** Empirical values test or calibrate a completed map; they do not construct it.
6. **CH3 does not establish a CH1 occupant.** A conditional energetic story cannot back-prove chemistry nonemptiness.
