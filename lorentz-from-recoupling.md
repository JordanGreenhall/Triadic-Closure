# Lorentz From Recoupling

This is the supporting theorem record for P5. It states exactly what is native, what is conditional, and what the named external theorem contributes.

## Theorem with conditions

Assume:

1. the P4 partition of one oriented temporal direction and three interchangeable spatial degrees;
2. P5's one-link/one-step identity `c = 1` and direct-bound null relation;
3. a physical rule-presented direction carrier with a continuous `SO(3)` action and its invariant spatial quadratic form; and
4. re-couplings that preserve conditioning adjacency, causal order, the fixed unit, and the constructed null/interval structure.

Then the admissible orientation-preserving re-couplings are exactly the proper orthochronous Lorentz group `SO^+(1,3)`.

## Warrant split

- **Framework contribution:** the conditioning network supplies the null relation, causal order, one-way orientation, and fixed link/step unit.
- **Carrier contribution:** continuous directions and `SO(3)` supply the spatial sum-of-squares. P4's discrete interchangeability alone does not supply this carrier.
- **External mathematics:** Alexandrov–Zeeman supplies the named implication from causal/null-preserving bijections on the given manifold to Lorentz transformations up to translation and dilation.
- **Restrictions:** dilation is excluded because `c = 1` is a structural identity, not a free scale. Translations express network homogeneity and are factored out of the linear re-coupling group. One-way conditioning excludes time reversal and selects the orthochronous component; connected orientation-preserving re-couplings select the proper component.

This is **Secured conditionally (N3)** and **Registered–Sealed on a given carrier/manifold**. Manifold recovery itself is not sealed. Exact local Lorentz symmetry for a continuum recovered from the discrete base is **Secured within its defended-posit ceiling** and **Registered, not sealed**, as recorded by G1.

## What the theorem does not say

- It does not prove that P4's three degrees already form a continuous direction space.
- It does not recover a smooth manifold from the discrete network.
- It does not add a second kind of physical occupant or bearer.
- It does not derive curvature, gravity, global spacetime topology, or arbitrary diffeomorphism invariance.
- It does not turn the Alexandrov–Zeeman import into a framework-native proof.

The local theorem is therefore usable wherever its carrier/manifold conditions are explicit. It may not be cited as unconditional closure of physical spacetime.
