# P19 Native Electron Ruler and Proton/Electron Ratio

## Scope

P19 owns the native electron ruler, the factor-three spatial seating of one paid color closure, the exact `6 pi^5` With–This factor, and the bounded proton/electron mass-ratio correction.

P19 does not own baryon closure or proton/neutron differentiation as such, neutron mass splitting, decay, external-unit calibration, Higgs/Yukawa mass generation, generations, or the full numerical particle spectrum.

## Native electron ruler

The electron role is color-trivial. Its color orbit is therefore a point and contributes no nontrivial P18 color-closure measure. Its displayed bare recurrence supplies the minimal positive mass-bearing repeat relative to which other closure loads are compared.

Framework state: **Secured at the displayed recurrence and ratio-normalization scope**.

Semantic registration: **Registered, not Sealed** as the native ruler

> `m_e = 1`.

This is an internal normalization. It does not derive the measured electron mass in electron-volts, kilograms, or any other external unit.

## Spatial seating of one paid color closure

P18 supplies one actually maintained nontrivial color closure with measure

> `2 pi^5`.

The With-flattening separates the same triad into three independent spatial bearings, while the This-flattening gathers it into one color closure. The one gathered closure is therefore borne once along each of the three equal exterior bearings.

The join is additive:

> `3 x 2 pi^5 = 6 pi^5`.

Product is excluded because there is one color closure, not three independent closures. Distribution is excluded because the construction bears the full closure equally in each independent spatial direction rather than dividing a conserved quantity among directions.

Framework state: **Secured relative to P18 and the exact-three With result**.

Semantic registration: **Registered, not Sealed** as the With–This color-seating factor.

The standalone `6 pi^5` is not the complete proton/electron mass ratio.

## Canonical proton/electron ratio

The canonical relation is

> `m_p / m_e = 6 pi^5 [1 + c(3 pi^4)^-2]`, with `3/2 <= c <= 9/4`.

The status of its parts is:

- `6 pi^5`: exact within the registered P18/P19 construction;
- positive multiplicative correction: Registered;
- second-order scale `(3 pi^4)^-2`: Registered;
- coefficient interval `3/2 <= c <= 9/4`: Registered;
- exact internal selection of `c`: Open.

The measured ratio corresponds to approximately

> `c = 1.6076`,

or

> `epsilon_FW = c(3 pi^4)^-2 approximately 1.8825 x 10^-5`.

Those measured values are empirical placement within the registered interval, not framework derivations of `c`.

These are three readings of one closure, not three masses to add.
