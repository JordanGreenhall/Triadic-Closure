# The D3 Color Theorem, Conditional Form

### Submitted for adjudication. The claim is a conditional with one explicitly located selection. The question for the mediator is whether the conditional is sound and the selection correctly placed — not whether SU(3) is forced unconditionally. It is not, and the proof says where and why.

> **P3 scope control.** [P3](boson-fermion-and-holding-statistics.md) does not establish physical occupants. Wherever this conditional theorem assumes inhabited physical sectors, that occupancy is an additional hypothesis rather than an inherited P3 result. P3 also supplies no matter, mediation-carrier, composite, spin, or gauge role.

## 0. Statement

**Theorem (D3, conditional).**
Let the physics branch selection **(S)** hold: physics is the sub-branch of mathematics studying self-transforming structure (contents bearing a continuous change-generator with a conserved invariant). Let the D1/D2 physical registration be in force (Section 1). Let **(A)** be the selection that the D3 mediation carrier is built on the antisymmetric sector of the inherited D1 exchange-split. Then the internal gauge group of D3 same-kind mediation is

  G = Aut_ℂ(M, h, ε)⁰ = SU(3).

**Modal status.** Given (S), the explicitly hypothesized D1/D2 physical actuals, and physical occupancy of the relevant sectors, this source claims every later step is forced except (A). P3 does not supply those physical-occupancy hypotheses. (A) then selects the antisymmetric sector for the mediation carrier.

**What is NOT claimed.** That (A) is forced. That SU(3) is the symmetry of From/With/This (it is not; see Section 3). That the color identification of M is derived (it is recognition, zero load).

## 1. Inherited structure (D1/D2 actuals, taken as given here)

The theorem takes the following as hypotheses. P3 establishes only the algebraic content identified below; the physical actuals are not established by P3:

- **(I1)** A complex vector space of physical states; superposition available. [D2: the continuous flow U(θ) = e^{iθG} requires complex linearity.]
- **(I2)** A nondegenerate **positive-definite** Hermitian pairing h. [D1 registration; positivity from probability-readability. Stated explicitly to fix the unitary group as compact U(n), not U(p,q).]
- **(I3)** A constitutive orientation/involution J with J² = id and mathematically inhabited eigensectors λ = +1 (symmetric) and λ = −1 (antisymmetric), plus the theorem's additional hypothesis that the needed physical sectors are occupied. [P3 supplies the algebraic sectors, not physical occupancy.]
- **(I4)** Interaction-events exist as physical actuals (occurrences within the D1 registration), and unitary reversibility constrains admissible transformations. [D2.]
- **(I5)** D3 demand: interaction-events must compose into higher-order closures of the **same physical kind**.

## 2. The two sectors are inhabited; neither is forced as the mediation carrier

By (I3), the exchange split has mathematically inhabited symmetric (+1) and antisymmetric (−1) sectors, while physical occupancy is separately hypothesized. Nothing in (S) or (I1)–(I5) selects which sector carries the D3 mediation structure. The carrier selection is explicit as (A); the physical-occupancy premise remains independently conditional under P3.

## 3. The carrier M is horizontal; the offices are not mixed

From/With/This are the fixed vertical grammar. They are forcing functions, not basis vectors; a transformation F ↦ aF + bW + cT is inadmissible and is **not** part of this theorem. The object SU(3) acts on is the **horizontal D3 mediation carrier** M — the carrier of mediation alternatives by which already-actual interaction-events (I4) compose as same-kind closures (I5). M inherits (I1)–(I4): it is complex, carries h, is constrained to unitary reversibility, and carries the exchange split. The automorphism question below concerns frames of M, never the offices. (This is what dissolves the standard objection that a continuous group cannot preserve signature-distinct offices: the offices are not in M.)

## 4. The closure-form ε and its forced properties

D3 (I5) requires a **whole-reading**: a map that assigns to a complete frame of mediation contributions the single same-kind closure they compose. Write it ε(m₁, m₂, m₃) for a frame of contributions in M. Its properties:

- **(P1) Scalar.** The closure stands as one. [Forced: I5, same-kind closure is a single actual.]
- **(P2) Complex-multilinear.** M is complex-linear (I1) and closure must respect superposition in each contribution. [Forced: I1.]
- **(P3) Nonzero.** D3 closure actually registers, else no composition occurs and (I5) fails. [Forced: I5.]
- **(P4) Vanishing on coincidence under (A).** A frame in which two contributions coincide presents no genuine multiplicity to hold. In the antisymmetric sector (A), coinciding contributions compose to zero — this is exactly the inhabited −1-sector behavior of (I3) (the established Pauli/fermionic actual): identical antisymmetric contributions annihilate. [Forced *given (A)*: it is the defining behavior of the sector selected.]

**Lemma 4.1 (antisymmetry).** A multilinear form that vanishes whenever two arguments coincide is alternating.
*Proof.* Standard. For ε with (P2) and (P4): 0 = ε(…, m_i + m_j, …, m_i + m_j, …) = ε(…,m_i,…,m_j,…) + ε(…,m_j,…,m_i,…) (the coincident terms vanish by P4), so ε(…,m_i,…,m_j,…) = −ε(…,m_j,…,m_i,…). ∎

Therefore, **given (A)**, ε is scalar, complex-trilinear, alternating, and nonzero:

  ε ∈ Λ³M*, ε ≠ 0.

The arity is three because the closure reads a complete mediation frame as one whole (I5) and is not a product of lower-arity operations: by Lemma 4.1 ε is alternating, and any decomposable trilinear (a bilinear times a linear, the only way to build trilinear from binary pieces) is not totally alternating. So no binary-built form satisfies (P1)–(P4); the alternating closure is not reducible to iterated binary brackets. [This replaces the earlier, defective "irreducible arity" argument: irreducibility is a *consequence* of alternating + nonzero, not an independent premise.]

## 5. Dimension

**Lemma 5.1.** Λ³(V*) = 0 if dim_ℂ V < 3.
*Proof.* An alternating k-form vanishes on any linearly dependent k-tuple; in dimension < k every k-tuple is dependent. ∎

By Section 4, ε ∈ Λ³M* and ε ≠ 0, so by Lemma 5.1:

  dim_ℂ M ≥ 3.

**Minimality.** (S) and the D3 demand introduce no actual requiring dim_ℂ M > 3: the closure-form (P1)–(P4) is the complete D3-specific datum, and it is saturated at dimension 3 (Λ³ of a 3-space is 1-dimensional — a single closure-line, no surplus). Any dimension > 3 carries mediation directions unused by the forced structure, excluded by minimality absent a further actual forcing them. Hence

  dim_ℂ M = 3,  M ≅ ℂ³.

**Note on the ℂ²/symmetric alternative (the precise location of the dependence on (A)).** Without (A) — i.e. if the mediation carrier were built on the symmetric sector — (P4) fails: coinciding symmetric contributions do not annihilate, ε is symmetric not alternating, Sym³((ℂ²)*) ≠ 0, and dimension 2 survives with a smaller group. The step dim ≥ 3 holds **iff** ε is alternating, and ε is alternating **iff** (A). This is the whole content of the conditionality, isolated to one biconditional.

## 6. The group

The forced structure on M is: complex 3-space (Section 5), positive-definite Hermitian h (I2), nonzero alternating ε (Section 4). Admissible gauge transformations preserve exactly this structure and add no unforced invariant (admissibility = maximal-compatible: withholding a structure-preserving transformation without a constraint excluding it is privilege by fiat).

- Preserving h (positive-definite, I2): G ⊆ U(3). [Compact, by I2 positivity.]
- For g ∈ U(3), the induced action on ε ∈ Λ³(ℂ³)* is by the determinant character: g·ε = det(g)·ε. [Λ³(ℂ³)* is 1-dimensional; U(3) acts on it by det.]
- Preserving ε exactly: det(g) = 1.

  G = U(3) ∩ SL(3, ℂ) = SU(3).

**On "exactly" vs "up to phase" (anticipating the standard objection).** The question is whether ε is preserved exactly or only up to a phase (which would leave U(3) admissible). Here the resolution is structural and does not appeal to a separately-postulated calibration: ε is the *closure datum itself* — the registered fact "these three contributions compose to this one whole." A phase multiplying ε is a registered phase on the composed whole that is absent on the contributions; by conservation of registered distinctions (a symmetry may move within registered structure but may not manufacture a registered distinction), such a phase is inadmissible as a symmetry. Hence exact preservation, det = 1. [This step uses conservation, an established invariant of the object, not a new premise. A mediator who rejects the conservation principle rejects it here; a mediator who grants it gets det = 1.]

## 8. The exact question for the mediator

Two questions, separable:

**(Q1) Is the conditional sound?** Granting (S), the D1/D2 actuals, and (A): does SU(3) follow with no further selection? The proof claims yes, with the only non-elementary steps being Lemma 4.1 (standard), Lemma 5.1 (standard), the determinant action on Λ³ (standard), and the conservation argument for exact preservation (§6, the one place a framework principle rather than linear algebra is invoked).

**(Q2) Is the selection correctly located and minimal?** Within this source's explicit physical-occupancy hypotheses, the proof asks whether (A) is the remaining choice of antisymmetric mediation carrier. P3 independently shows only that both mathematical sectors are inhabited; it does not discharge the physical-occupancy hypothesis or select a carrier. The biconditional in the Section 5 note (dim ≥ 3 ⇔ ε alternating ⇔ (A)) is the source's later conditional claim.

If the full physical-occupancy hypotheses and (A) are accepted, this source submits its later conditional `SU(3)` result. P3 neither supplies those hypotheses nor adjudicates that gauge-theoretic conditional.
