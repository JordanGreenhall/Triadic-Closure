# CH7 — Cyclic Delocalization and Aromatic Closure

## Standing summary

CH7 owns the cyclic-delocalization and aromatic-closure specification: a cyclic equal-center model, its ring modes, the conditional closed-shell `4k+2` filling arithmetic, and the proposed interpretation of aromaticity as ring-closed multi-center holding.

The exact ideal cyclic mode structure and its `4k+2` closed-shell count are **Secured conditionally inside the stated finite model**. Physical aromatic closure, an admitted delocalized occupant, energetic stabilization, heterocycle extension, and quantitative chemistry remain Conjectured or Open.

## Unit boundary

CH7 owns:

1. cyclic delocalization as one candidate standing distributed around a closed center-cycle;
2. the ideal equal-center, equal-coupling cyclic mode model;
3. the ring-mode decomposition and degeneracy pattern of that model;
4. the conditional `4k+2` closed-shell filling count;
5. aromatic closure as the proposed chemical interpretation of closed cyclic mode filling.

CH7 does not own or derive:

- CH6 catenation, branching, constitution, isomerism, or functional groups;
- a physical nearest-neighbor interaction law or exact equal coupling in actual molecules;
- aromatic stabilization energy, bond equalization magnitude, ring current, magnetic response, spectroscopy, or reaction preference;
- heterocycle, fused-ring, non-planar, antiaromatic, or excited-state generality;
- reaction taxonomy, kinetics, thermodynamics, or later chemistry.

## Boundary principle

A ring is a CH6 topology. Aromaticity begins only when a cyclic multi-center holding and its filling condition are separately constructed. Therefore ring closure alone does not establish cyclic delocalization or aromatic closure.

## Ideal cyclic model

Let `n` equivalent center-modes be arranged on a cycle and let the model admit one common nearest-neighbor coupling. The cyclic symmetry diagonalizes the linear mode space into `n` ring modes. In the standard finite-cycle representation the mode labels are the discrete phases around the cycle.

Under the ideal equal-center/equal-coupling assumptions:

- there is one lowest nondegenerate mode;
- the remaining modes occur in phase-conjugate pairs, except for the even-`n` opposite-phase mode when present;
- two fermionic occupants may fill each spatial mode under the inherited holding rule;
- filling the lowest mode and then complete degenerate pairs yields closed shells at `2 + 4k = 4k+2` occupants.

This is a conditional arithmetic result. It does not establish that an actual chemical ring realizes the model, that the displayed ordering is physically selected, or that closed filling produces a specified stabilization energy.

## Aromatic closure

The proposed framework interpretation is that aromaticity is one closure held across the full cycle rather than a sequence of independently owned pair bonds. The cycle is closed both topologically and modally: the standing returns through all centers and its occupied mode set closes without a partially filled degenerate pair.

This interpretation is **Conjectured-strong**. Physical realization requires an admitted multi-center standing, a native interaction/maintenance functional, an occupancy rule, persistence, and a readout connecting the standing to an actual molecular ring.

## Smuggle-watches

1. **A ring is not aromaticity.** CH6 topology does not supply CH7 cyclic holding.
2. **`4k+2` is not Hückel theory in full.** The count does not import empirical parameters, orbital energies, or stabilization values.
3. **Mode arithmetic is not a physical occupant.** A diagonalized finite matrix does not establish a molecule.
4. **Closed filling is not energetic stabilization.** The sign and magnitude require the CH3 functional.
5. **Equal-center mathematics is not heterocycle generality.** Unequal sites and couplings are outside the theorem.
6. **Resonance drawings are not ownership proof.** Familiar representations may illustrate but cannot construct the standing.
