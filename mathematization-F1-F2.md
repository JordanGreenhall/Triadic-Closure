# M1 — Formal Representation of Triadic Structure

This document is the canonical M1 result. It gives an early mathematical carrier for the horizontal build and states exactly what follows from that representation. It does not identify the carrier with physical spacetime, prove a unique formalization, or import later metric and weighting structure.

## 1. Inherited structure

The following living characteristics are used:

- a closure can be taken at unit-altitude in a turn-over;
- iterated turn-overs accumulate a horizontal lineage and an address;
- From makes each conditioning step oriented;
- `J` is a within-stage vertical orientation-reversal, while `rho` concerns between-stage horizontal coherence;
- registered distinctions are retained unless later identification is earned;
- the mathematics available at the gate is rule-given, not an unrestricted completed continuum.

These are inherited constraints. They do not determine one uniquely privileged mathematical carrier.

## 2. The conditioning carrier

Define a directed carrier `N` for the unary lineage walk:

- a **vertex** is a standing represented by a finite unary turn-over address;
- a directed edge `a -> b` records that `b` is obtained from `a` by one turn-over;
- the address length `depth(a)` counts the directed turns in the represented history;
- registered office-content may label a vertex when that content is actually inherited at the relevant grade.

`N` is a representation of the horizontal build, not a graph embedded in a presupposed space. Its definition contains no external coordinates, background clock, metric, dimension, or dynamics.

The representation has explicit limits:

- unary addresses are the current carrier; injectivity for compound nesting remains Open;
- two vertices are not declared identical merely because a chosen notation gives them similar labels;
- the formalization does not prove that every actual closure has a finite unary address;
- other equivalent or richer carriers are not excluded.

## 3. Office formalization and axis placement

The offices are not three stocks stored at each vertex. They enter the representation through distinct roles:

| Framework structure | Formal role in M1 | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| From | orientation of a conditioning edge | horizontal readout of an office-signature; not identical to `J` |
| With | admissible holding or adjacency structure over standings | no metric follows without an additional construction |
| This | closure represented as a standing vertex | vertexhood is a carrier role, not the essence of This |
| `J` | within-stage reversal of orientation | vertical; not graph evolution |
| `rho` | coherence across stage presentations or passages | horizontal; category-grade coherence laws remain Open |

This is a typed representation: no one graph feature exhausts an office, and no office is reduced to a graph feature.

## 4. What is intrinsic at M1

A quantity is **M1-native** only if it is defined from the carrier plus living inherited structure, with every additional choice declared.

### 4.1 Depth

For a represented finite address, `depth(a)` is its address length. It is an oriented integer count within `N`. This establishes a combinatorial grading.

It does not yet establish physical time, proper time, a universal clock, or a continuum limit. Those are later recognitions or constructions.

### 4.2 Directed reachability

The shortest directed path length `d_to(a,b)`, when a directed path exists, is an asymmetric reachability count. It is not a symmetric With-metric.

A symmetric graph distance can be selected by forgetting edge orientation and taking shortest paths in the underlying undirected adjacency graph. That construction is mathematically legitimate, but it is an additional selection with a declared information loss. Its identification with spatial distance is a physics claim, not an M1 result.

### 4.3 Background-dependent quantities

Before a metric, measure, or clock has been constructed, rate and density cannot be used as primitive quantities on `N`: their denominators are absent from the M1 carrier.

This is a stage-relative non-importation rule, not a permanent ban. A later lineage may define a framework-native clock, region measure, continuum approximation, rate, or density; once that construction is Registered, the derived quantity is licit within its earned scope.

### 4.4 Coherence participation

`rho` makes coherence a relevant inherited structure, but M1 does not yet supply a scalar coherence-participation functional. Such a functional requires a specified state/configuration space, pairing or measure, normalization, and standing object. Its construction and weighting interpretation belong to M3.

## 5. Step atomicity

In the carrier definition, one edge represents one completed turn-over. Therefore depth changes by one along an edge and has no carrier vertex at a fractional edge-depth.

This is **representational atomicity**. It establishes the discrete combinatorics of `N`; it does not prove:

- that a turn-over has no internal architectonic structure;
- that physical time is fundamentally discrete;
- that no refinement of the carrier is possible;
- that all vertices have finite degree; or
- that a rule-given continuum cannot arise as a later representation or limit.

Any downstream argument that needs physical clock atomicity must supply the physics registration and the bridge from one carrier edge to one physical step. It may not cite M1's definition as that bridge.

| Claim | Standing | Warrant and limit |

No M1 claim is Registered and Sealed. The carrier is early formal scaffolding whose later use must preserve its stated limits.

## 8. Downstream contract

M2 may build additional closure, composition, conservation, and persistence machinery on `N` only by declaring every new structure. M3 must construct rather than merely name coherence participation. M4 and the physics lineages may use rates, densities, metrics, or continuum objects only after their denominators and limiting bridges have been earned.
