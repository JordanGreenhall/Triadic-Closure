# Appendix — Generated Structures and Domain Form

This appendix supplies the detailed arguments supporting document 01, sections 6–9. Document 01 governs every claim’s standing and scope. The appendix develops the generated floor structures, axes, D-series, and mathematics gate without adding a separate theory layer.

## 1. How floor-level generation proceeds

The demonstrations begin from actual standings, the three offices, the held/registered distinction, and the modal engine. A proposed structure is admitted only when its basis and construction are available at that level. Later disciplinary notions may be used as recognitions after the fact, but they do not supply the construction.

## 2. Self-relation and other-relation

A standing may be held under a basis that registers sameness of character with itself, or under a basis that registers a relation between distinct standings. This yields self-relation and other-relation at the level of registered character.

The result does not yet establish literal numerical reflexivity. Writing R(x,x) can conceal the unearned assumption that the same actual occupant appears twice in one holding. What is established is sameness of registered character or role within a holding. Literal holding of a numerically identical standing with itself remains open and nothing later may depend on it.

## 3. Quantity and quality

Quantity is quality-blind holding of qualitatively distinct standings. The occupants remain distinct by their actual lineages, while the chosen basis disregards every difference relevant to their particular character. The holding can therefore register sameness under one respect without erasing the occupants’ distinctness.

Quality is registered non-interchangeability of character. Two contents may stand in parity while differing in the respect under which they are actual. The distinction between quantity and quality is thus not a choice between bare identical units and intrinsic substances. It arises from the relation between what is held and what the basis registers.

Quantity and quality are two flattening readouts, not the root binary of the architecture. The third readout is ordering, supplied by From’s one-wayness.

## 4. Ordinal succession

From provides directed succession. A standing can be taken, then another, with the order of the takings registered. Reversal changes the order-character. Finite oriented chains therefore supply ordinal grading before unordered cardinality.

This is why counting-in-sequence can be generated while counting an unmarked symmetric multiplicity remains open. Ordered position is registrable through role in the conditioning structure. An unordered collection of identically-charactered positions has no admissible internal sorter unless count or designation has already been imported.

## 5. Containment, comparison, and predication

Containment order arises when the registered basis of one content includes that of another. The relation is not spatial enclosure but implication among registered characters.

Comparison does not require extracting members from their holdings. Two intact standings can be held under a comparative basis and their registered characters related. This preserves the reality of the holdings instead of treating comparison as a view from outside every With.

Predication is the standing of a registered character of a content under a basis. Subject and predicate are not independently complete substances joined by an external copula. Predication is a holding in which a content and one of its registered respects stand together determinately.

## 6. Compound bases and connective structure

Conjunctive bases are available when several registered conditions are held together as one admission rule. A content stands under the compound basis only when it satisfies each component condition.

Disjunctive bases are not generated at the same floor-level strength. A join that admits either of several conditions requires a sorter or closure rule not supplied merely by conjunction. Inside the mathematical domain some practical uses of disjunction can be bypassed by discrimination of registered character, oriented construction, or sector decomposition, but the floor-level disjunction joint remains open.

Pruned binary holdings support connective form. Directed and symmetric binary relations generate families recognizable as conjunction-like, implication-like, equivalence-like, and exclusive-alternative-like structures. A privileged truth value is not forced at the floor. Within the mathematical boundary, dependence on a marked designated value is independently excluded.

## 7. Lineage, address, and non-commutative generation

A completed closure may be turned over and taken as a unit for a new determination. Repeated turn-overs generate a finite address: a record of which aspect of which prior closure was taken at each step.

Because From is one-way, the order of turn-overs matters. In general FT and TF are different addresses. Identifying them would require an inverse or commutativity not supplied by the construction. Every finite unary address can be walked, and distinct unary addresses remain distinct under the non-commutative rule. Injectivity for arbitrary compound nesting remains open.

Lineage has two readings at the mathematics gate. Event-history—the particular occurrences by which an item arrived—is excluded. Lineage-structure—the registered order-type of the takings—is retained.

## 8. The axes and the images of the triad

The admissibility axis is From-read: a constraint distinguishes admitted from excluded. The realizability axis is With-read: the actual field holds what can co-stand from S. An actual This lies at their intersection: admitted under the boundary and realized from the standing.

The triad presents two complementary images.

### 8.1 The marked triad

From, With, and This retain their distinct office characters. The marked image records conditioning, holding, and determinate standing as mutually composed but non-interchangeable.

### 8.2 The flattenings

Each flattening blinds one office-character and exposes a symmetry signature.

- **From-flattened:** ordered succession remains asymmetric.
- **With-flattened:** positions become interchangeable under the holding basis.
- **This-flattened:** positions stand symmetrically in standing but remain non-interchangeable in kind.

Ordering, quantity, and quality are the three floor-level readouts of these flattenings. A later domain may recognize them under richer names, but the symmetry signatures constrain every valid identification.

## 9. Strata and domains

A stratum is a stabilized level in the horizontal build: closures at one altitude stand as units for a higher construction. A domain is selected by a boundary that determines what counts as a content of that domain. A domain may contain several strata, and the same standing may participate in several domains under different bases.

A domain opens with four elements: an inherited whole, a selecting constraint, a boundary-With, and a bootstrap to its first content. The inherited whole is not dismantled into convenient primitives; all of its registered structure remains binding.

## 10. The D-series

The D-series is a phase diagnostic and path-schema.

- **D1:** first distinction or content under the boundary.
- **D2:** relation or multiplicity among D1 contents.
- **D3:** recursion, turn-over, or self-composition.
- **D4:** admissible passages and registered invariants.
- **D5:** the mature field of admissible variation.
- **D6:** persistent closures across that field.
- **D7:** a successor-facing selection within D6.

The dependency order is strict. D2 requires standings to hold. D3 requires a holding that can be taken as a unit. D4 requires lineage-related stages across which sameness can be registered. D5 requires an invariant whose variation-space can stand as one field. D6 requires a field across which persistence can be stated. D7 requires a persistent closure capable of serving as the standing ground of a successor.

Forced dependency and forced form do not imply forced occupancy. A boundary may admit no content. A domain may terminate before a later phase. Occupancy is established only by construction and trace: exhibit the mature object, decompose the capacities it actually bears, and show that each phase is present in dependency order without imported intermediates.

The series makes no claim to be a complete inventory of every possible phase. It states a walked path with a precise falsification surface: a required missing adjacency, imported content at a phase, or an authored phase form that cannot be reproduced from the available stock.

At domain grade, pacing matters. A phase is occupied when its form stands over the relevant whole stock, not when one first specimen appears. D6 is the field of persisting closures and admissible passages held as one. D7 is not a universal summit; it is the location whose composition-character provisions a successor, if such a location exists.

## 11. The mathematics gate

The mathematical domain is selected at the held/registered seam. A content is mathematical when its identity conditions are exhausted by registration.

The boundary excludes:

- event-particularity and embodied occurrence;
- which-taking and arrival-history;
- designation among registrably identical positions.

It retains:

- vertical office-form;
- conditionedness and orientation;
- registered order-type and character.

A boundary cannot exclude vertical constitution without excluding relating itself. Orientation is form rather than event: “arrived one-way” is a registered structural character, while “arrived through these particular occurrences” is event-history.

Three consequences follow.

1. Mathematical identity is basis-registered identity; isomorphism-invariance follows from the boundary rather than being imported from mathematical practice.
2. Event-lineage is excluded, while lineage-structure remains available as order-type.
3. The gate is portable because every holding in every domain has a registered side. Mathematics applies broadly because it takes that registered side as its content-kind.

## 12. Boundary-registration and the unmarked dyad

Before a first mathematical content stands there is no internal mathematical basis. D1 therefore begins by boundary-registration: the mathematical boundary itself is the first available registering act.

The first content is one unmarked dyad with orientation-form. The cut has two mutually exclusive sides of identical position-character. The positions do not collapse because the distinction is itself registered and held as multiplicity. They are not marked as this copy and that copy; any plus/minus or zero/one labels used in calculation are scaffolding. Every valid construction must be exchange-equivariant.

The dyad exhibits weak discernibility at the exact recognition mapping: the positions share monadic character and are discerned by an irreflexive relation. No additional philosophical doctrine enters with the name.

## 13. The walked mathematical object

The mathematical walk yields:

- signature-distinguished triadic factors;
- vertical survey and horizontal generation;
- constitutive orientation-reversal J;
- self-conjugate and strictly paired sectors;
- ordinal grading, positive integers, and formal differences;
- rational same-summand decomposition into swap-fixed and swap-negated sectors;
- free modules and a non-degenerate J-compatible pairing;
- rule-given real and complex analysis;
- conservation of registered distinctions;
- rho-coherence across the accumulated build.

The object is generated from the boundary and the floor structures, not posited as familiar mathematics in advance.

## 14. The two-sector theorem

J is orientation reversal defined on registered formation. Double reversal returns the content, so J is constitutively involutive. Both self-conjugate contents and strictly paired contents exist.

Over a base in which two is invertible, an exchange property satisfying \(\lambda^2=1\) has exactly the eigenclasses \(+1\) and \(-1\). No third class is generated. The sectors are direct-sum complements rather than topologically separated regions. Sector membership is a per-content structural fact determined by construction.

In physics these sectors may be recognized as the symmetric and antisymmetric modes. The recognition contributes no physics to the mathematical theorem.

## 15. What physics may inherit

The mathematics gate supplies orientation and J, two exchange sectors, conservation of registered distinctions, discrete grading, rational linear composition, a non-degenerate J-compatible pairing, and rule-given complex structure.

It does not supply physical time, spacetime, dimension, dynamics, energy conservation, empirical constants, or the completed rule-free continuum. Every successor-domain use of those contents requires its own construction or explicit external translation.

## 16. Boundaries

This appendix does not settle literal numerical reflexivity, floor-level disjunction, arbitrary compound-address injectivity, unordered cardinality, the empty order-character, pairing values, categorical survey-finality, full D6 coherence laws, or any particular domain’s D-series occupancy. Those standings remain governed by document 01 and the current frontier ledger.
