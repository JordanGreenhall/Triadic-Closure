# Vertical, Horizontal, and Recursion

This is the governing F3 placement control. Every content participates in both dimensions at once: vertical office-constitution and horizontal position in a build. Asking whether an object is “vertical or horizontal” is a category error; the useful question is which aspect of it is being read on which axis.

## Vertical: synchronic constitution

Vertical names the co-constitutive relation among From, With, and This by which each office is itself the relating of the other two. It is synchronic and constitutive: the same office grammar holds at every actual closure, independent of age, scale, complexity, or historical position.

Vertical includes:

- office-composition and admissibility under a boundary;
- closure read as closure;
- From's cut, orientation, and polarity;
- `J` as within-stage orientation reversal;
- mutual, baseless office-constitution;
- the marked image in which the three offices retain their different signatures.

“The same at every layer” means that the same grammar constitutes every closure. It does not mean that closures have identical content, internal structure, development, or standing.

## Horizontal: diachronic build

Horizontal names succession, lineage, recursive taking, nesting, and the re-texturing of realizability by what has already stood. It is diachronic: history is content on this axis, and registered distinctions are not erased by later construction.

Horizontal includes:

- monotonic accumulation of actuals;
- `Realizability(S)` as conditioned by the standing state;
- stride and build history;
- recursive taking of a completed closure as a unit in a later closure;
- layers, nesting, altitude, and strata;
- `ρ` as coherence across stages of the build;
- the flattened images produced when office-kind is not registered across recursive taking.

The visual up/down of cell → tissue → organ → organism → network → ecology does not make that tower vertical. It is a horizontal history of closures taken as units at later altitudes.

## Recursion and the no-collapse rule

Vertical mutual constitution and horizontal recursion are different structures.

- Vertically, an office is a relating of the other two offices. This has no historical first or last layer and generates no diachronic variety.
- Horizontally, a completed determination is taken as a unit in a later determination. This accumulates lineage, constraints, and new closure-content.

Do not turn vertical constitution into an infinite stack of smaller objects. Do not turn horizontal nesting into deeper or more fundamental being. A first actual may be horizontally empty while remaining fully vertically constituted.

The useful location vocabulary is:

| Term | Axis | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| depth | vertical | office-decomposition without a finite base |
| stride | horizontal | one completed determination in the build |
| layer | horizontal | a completed closure used in later composition |
| altitude | horizontal | position in the layer tower |
| stratum | horizontal | a stabilized band of units and vocabulary |
| standing/building view | horizontal | readback of what has arrived / trace of how it was built |

## `J` and `ρ`

`J` and `ρ` do not compete as two operators of one kind.

- **`J` is within-stage and vertical.** It recognizes orientation reversal at a boundary. Double reversal returns the orientation, so involutivity is constitutive at this level. `J` has no build history of its own.
- **`ρ` is between-stage and horizontal.** It names the coherence by which stages, passages, and finite-depth constructions fit into a non-collapsing build. In framework terms, the running realizability constraint—actuals cohering into the conditions on what may stand next—is `ρ` at work. Its detailed categorical laws belong to the mathematical units.

They meet in an actual closure without becoming interchangeable. `J` constrains each stage synchronically; `ρ` coheres the succession diachronically. Imposing `J` on the build as though it erased history would collapse lineage. Treating `ρ` as the constitutive polarity of a single stage would misplace coherence.

## Marked and flattened registers

F2 establishes that the triad is available in two images:

- the **marked image**, read within-stage on the vertical, preserves the different signatures of From, With, and This;
- the **flattened images**, read across recursive taking on the horizontal, register the offices without registering their difference in kind, while preserving the symmetry character of each source office.

The three flattened readouts are From → ordered succession, With → interchangeable quantity/extent, and This → non-interchangeable quality/internal distinction. This is the generic framework structure. Time, space, color, charge, or any other domain name requires a separate domain-specific derivation and standing.

## Closure, domain, and scale

Closure has one foundational meaning: From/With/This standing as one. An actual, an atom, a domain, or another higher closure may instantiate that same structure at different horizontal scopes. A domain is not a second procedural sense of closure and is not a fourth vertical office. Its boundary and accumulated territory locate it horizontally under the inherited office grammar.

## Failure tests

An account fails F3 if it:

- calls a scale hierarchy, layer tower, or nested actual “vertical” merely because it has an up/down presentation;
- treats `J` as a history-transform or `ρ` as a within-stage polarity;
- makes one axis optional for an actual content;
- makes a later, richer layer more vertically constituted than an earlier one;
- treats recursion as a second office grammar;
- lets a physics or chemistry reading define the generic axes retroactively.
