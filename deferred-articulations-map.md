# Deferred Articulations: Dependency Map and Work Frontier

A standing note. The framework has sealed many concepts **in essence** while explicitly deferring their **articulation** (specific terms, spectrum, values). Articulation is the frontier — that is where the real quantitative work is. The rule: do not force articulation at the top of the funnel. When an articulation is needed, descend its dependency chain until reaching something already articulated or the most fundamental thing; begin work there and build back up.

This note maps the chain beneath the Hamiltonian, the concept whose articulation we need for masses. It must be checked and extended against the transcript record (not from in-context memory, which has proven unreliable across context-resets).

## The recurring fault this addresses

Essences were sealed; articulations were honestly marked deferred. Quantitative claims that had to be retracted or demoted include the unsupported exact/numerical Lambda coefficient, confirmatory present-magnitude language, stronger dynamical claims, Σ|h|² for the holding, and coupling constants: each reached into a **deferred articulation** and supplied a number by fit, borrow, or narration rather than by building. [P28](p28-lambda-cosmological-closure-magnitude-and-dynamics.md) nevertheless preserves Registered macro `Lambda ~ R_H^-2` scaling and separately grades the coefficient, empirical translation, and dynamics. The discipline: when you reach a deferred articulation, recognize it as the frontier, descend to its floor, and build — do not fudge a value at the top.

## The chain beneath the Hamiltonian

From the record, the sealed Hamiltonian is **essence only**: "the conserved coherent total generator of the field's joint self-transformation, generating temporal succession, conservation = time-translation invariance — full form, not full articulation." The strip-down test explicitly deferred "kinetic + potential + interaction terms, **spectrum**, continuum-filling" as articulation.

The masses are the **spectrum** of the Hamiltonian (closure = eigenstate of the step-generator; mass = eigenvalue; lepton = lowest; ratios = eigenvalue ratios). So:

- **H spectrum (the masses)** depends on →
- **H articulation** (the actual operator: kinetic/potential/interaction terms) depends on →
- **the per-object generator, articulated** (what one object's self-transformation is as an operator, not just "a generator") depends on →
- **the single-This invariant / generator / slot triad, articulated** (the structure of one standing) depends on →
- **the measure**: the specific relationship between the temporal-oriented count and the spatial-unoriented count (the record defers "the specific relationship between the temporal-oriented and spatial-unoriented measures" to "later").

## The floor

The measure is **partially articulated**:
- Its **ratio** is done: c = the link/step identity = 1 (temporal count and spatial count are one conditioning counted two ways; their ratio is the unit). This is a real articulation — a value, not an essence.
- Its **single-step scale** is **not** articulated: how much one count-step is. This is exactly where mass-value and the coupling constants broke. It is either the one contingent input (the ruler) or something deeper fixes it. This question is the genuine floor.

## Where real work begins

Not at the Hamiltonian. The lowest **un**-articulated link that everything above needs is the **single-This invariant/generator/slot triad** — the articulation of one standing's own structure — sitting directly on the measure-floor (ratio c=1 done; single-step scale open).

Concretely, the buildable next step is: articulate one standing (one This) as an actual object with its generator, invariant, and slots specified from framework primitives — at which point the per-object generator is real, the Hamiltonian as an operator becomes reachable, its lowest eigenstate either is or is not the lepton, and eigenvalue ratios become real mass ratios. The single-step scale either falls out, or is identified as the one contingent ruler (against which the lepton = 1).
