## 1. The setup: what is held fixed and what varies

The proton and the neutron are the two nucleons. In framework terms each is a single
baryonic closure — three relata gathered into one interior color singlet. The question of
the neutron is the question of the **difference** between two closures that are nearly the
same.

Three faces of the closure must be separated cleanly, because the neutron question lives in
exactly one of them.

- **Interior / color (This-face).** Both nucleons are the *same* color singlet — the
  totally antisymmetric gathering of three interior roles. The color closure is identical
  for proton and neutron. **[Registered]** It is therefore *blind* to the proton–neutron
  difference: whatever distinguishes them is not here. The interior capacity is the

  (the exact With-This factor, not the complete proton/electron ratio; see [mass-derivation-three-faces](mass-derivation-three-faces.md)).

- **Spin.** Because color is antisymmetric, the flavor–spin content must be totally
  symmetric. This makes spin a **gate**, not a continuous contributor:
  - Three identical flavors (the all-up and all-down contents) are forced to total spin
    3/2; there is *no* spin-1/2 channel for them. **[Registered]** They are structurally
    excluded from the nucleon (spin-1/2) floor — not "expensive," but absent.
  - Mixed-flavor contents (two-of-one, one-of-other) admit both spin 3/2 and spin 1/2.
    The like-pair locks to spin-1; the odd relatum opens 3/2 ⊕ 1/2; the nucleon takes 1/2.
  - The two surviving mixed configurations are **spin-equivalent** by mirror construction.
    **[Registered]** Spin therefore contributes *equally* to proton and neutron and
    **cancels in the difference**. It is a controlled constant for the n–p question and the

- **Exterior / flavor (With-face).** With color closed (shared) and spin flat (cancels),
  the sole remaining distinction is **flavor**, identified framework-natively as the
  **exterior-coupling face**: how each relatum reaches outward. One flavor primitive is
  more outward-turned, the other more inward-turned. This is distinct *in kind* from color
  (interior gathering) but rides the same three carriers — coupled, distinct. **[Registered
  as the locus; structure below at varying status.]**

**Consequence.** The proton–neutron difference lives entirely in the With-face. The fork is
exactly the two spin-allowed mixed configurations: `P = uud` and `N = udd`. Everything that
follows is about these two access configurations and the geometry of the space they live in.

## 2. Why both exist, and why the proton is preferred

Both configurations are **realizable** — both are genuine standings. The neutron is not a
configuration that falls apart on formation; it persists. (Empirically: free neutrons exist.)
**[Registered: both realizable.]**

The historical proposal treated them as unequal under With-face conditioning: the proton presents
a **satisfiable net outward edge**, while the neutron presents **no net satisfiable edge**. It then
inferred that the available outward valence reduces proton maintenance. Current P20 rejects any
move from exterior occupancy to intrinsic load and retains this inference only as a Conjectured
occupancy-independent bridge target.

Mass is internally-borne maintenance. Therefore:

- The **proton is the deeper, more-relieved fixed point** — the attractor.
- The **neutron is the shallower, fully-internalized standing** — metastable.

**Historical candidate, not current warrant.** The occupancy language in the preceding paragraph
does not establish intrinsic maintenance order. Current P20 retains this only as a Conjectured
candidate and requires an occupancy-independent bridge.

**This is not charge-as-mass** (an incommensurable category error: charge is not a quantity
of mass). Charge enters *only* as the marker of which edges face outward (relievable,
off-books) versus inward (borne, mass-bearing). The neutron is heavier precisely because
neutrality denies it the relief outlet. Charge is a relief valve, not a cost. **[Registered:
mechanism and sign — historical label superseded; current status Conjectured.]**

This also explains why the earlier charge-based attempts all failed with the wrong sign:
every rule that treated charge as an interior cost predicted the proton heavier, because the
proton carries more outward charge. The correct reading inverts this — the outward charge is
*relief*, so the charged proton is *lighter*. The neutron, neutral, eats its full load.

## 4. The two foundational measures (used below)

### 4.1 The electron is `2π` — the bare exterior standing volume

The electron has **trivial color interior** (the 0-dimensional SU(3) orbit, a point). It has
no interior closure. What it has is the pure exterior face: the bare From-With recurrence —
a single exterior turn. Its volume is therefore one circle, **`2π`**.

This factors the proton cleanly:

```
proton volume   = 2π × 6π⁵ = 12π⁶
electron volume = 2π × 1    = 2π
With-This factor = 12π⁶ / 2π = 6π⁵   (not the complete m_p/m_e ratio)
```

The proton is the bare exterior turn (`2π`) **carrying** the full interior color-seating

closure volume).** The interior-only ratio is `6π⁵/2π = 3π⁴`. **[Registered for the With-This factor. The full ratio includes the theorem-bounded From-With bracket; only the exact selection of `c` remains Open.]**

### 4.2 The normalization `3π⁴`

Because `3π⁴ = 6π⁵/2π` is the proton-interior-per-electron-circle normalization, expressing the
splitting against it is the natural native measure. Define the dimensionless splitting
