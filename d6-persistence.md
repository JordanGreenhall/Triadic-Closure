# P6 — Persistence, Stability, and the Decay Boundary

This is the canonical P6 record. It separates four questions that the earlier D6 material compressed:

1. what the D6 phase-form is;
2. which physical constructions actually satisfy it, at what scope and conditions;
3. what the familiar names persistence, bound state, stability, metastability, and decay may mean;
4. what is additionally required for a domain-grade persistence field, a universal stability claim, an observed decay rate, an atom, a spectrum, or a stable-closure hierarchy.

P6 normalizes those boundaries. Walk order is not epistemic authority. A result first articulated in a later unit may establish or constrain P6 whenever its constitutive content satisfies the P6 criterion. What may not be spent backward is genuinely additional structure on which that result depends.

## Unit boundary

P6 owns:

- invariant-preservation across an exhibited D5 variation-field;
- recognition of every physical construction, wherever articulated, that satisfies that criterion;
- the persistent-composite criterion;
- structural stability and metastability criteria, conditional on an exhibited perturbation and transition structure;
- the structural distinction between persistence and decay;
- the boundary between an escape weight and an observed decay rate;
- the identity-continuity reading licensed by preserved invariants.

P6 does not by itself supply:

- P7 carrier, topology, smoothness, or manifold recovery;
- P8–P16 gauge groups, confinement dynamics, particle identities, charge assignments, or specific matter taxonomy;
- P17–P21 mass, baryon particulars, proton/neutron structure, beta-decay products, or quantitative lifetimes;
- atoms, shell structure, the periodic landscape, Higgs structure, generations, or the numerical particle spectrum.

But those later constructions may bear evidence for P6 if they exhibit a closure, its identity-constituting invariant, a declared variation-class, and preservation or return across that class. Their later location neither disqualifies nor upgrades them.

## Authority and applicability adjudication

The generic D6 construction in the canonical foundation governs the phase-form: given a D4 invariant and a standing D5 field of admissible variation, D6 consists in carrying that invariant coherently across allowed passages through the field. That construction is current and survives.

The older physics walk added stronger claims: exact `3+1` from bound-state stability, atoms, confinement, the particle spectrum, and a minimal Higgs realization. Those additions do not survive merely as assertions. Exact `3+1` is governed by P4; atoms, the periodic landscape, Higgs particulars, and the stable-closure/value cascade require their own constructions.

However, the later [With-to-This Closure](with-to-this-closure.md) result exhibits a concrete physical model in which the singlet/complete-closure class is preserved and restored under two declared perturbation/return flows. That downstream result is constitutively applicable to P6. It witnesses scoped, conditional physical persistence and stability for the modeled baryon closure. It does not establish universal QCD confinement, persistence across every physically relevant passage, domain-grade physics D6, or the most basic stable ruler.

The correct discipline is therefore neither forward-only exclusion nor unrestricted backward import. For every later result ask:

> Does the result exhibit the P6 invariant and preservation structure, or does it require genuinely additional ontology that is being smuggled into P6?

Recognition may run backward through the written walk. Constitution does not.

## 1. D6 phase-form

Let a physics construction supply:

- a D4 invariant `I`;
- a D5 variation-field `V` whose members and passages are explicitly admitted by the relevant physical construction;
- a standing closure `x` whose lineage-related presentations can be compared under `I`.

Then `x` persists over `V` exactly when every admitted passage in the declared scope preserves the registered invariant by which `x` is re-identified.

Persistence is therefore preservation across allowed variation. It is not a substance enduring inside an independently given time-container. P5 supplies finite succession and re-resolution; P6 asks whether an invariant survives the declared class of those passages.

This is a conditional phase construction. The generic D-series secures its form and dependency order; it does not guarantee an unrestricted or domain-complete physical population.

## 2. Physical P6 witnesses

Physical P6 occupancy is claim- and scope-specific, not all-or-nothing.

The modeled baryon closure supplies one current witness:

- **closure:** the gauge-equivalence class of complete color-singlet triads;
- **identity-constituting invariant:** complete closure / singlet registration under the selected color-overlap structure;
- **declared variation-class:** the reported binding-coherence and conditioning-degree flows and their tested perturbations;
- **preservation/return:** random tested starts and perturbations flow or return to the singlet while octet and decuplet components drain toward it.

Within that model and those flows, physical persistence and perturbative stability are Secured conditionally; the familiar baryon/bound-state language is Registered only for the displayed core and inherits every dependency of the gauge, closure, and modeled-dynamics construction.

This does not establish:

- that the baryon is the earliest or most basic persistent physical closure;
- persistence under every admissible higher-body or physically relevant QCD passage;
- universal confinement dynamics;
- the totality of persisting physical closures as one domain-grade D6 field;
- a stable ruler or value-calibration anchor.

Accordingly, the previous blanket statement “physics D6 occupancy is Open” was too strong. The correct record is: **scoped physical occupancy exists; unrestricted/domain-grade occupancy and the basic stable-ruler problem remain Open.**

## 3. Persistent composite and bound-state core

A composite satisfies the P6 persistent-composite criterion when its constitutive relation remains registered across the declared allowed passages. The constituents need not remain featureless or motionless; what must survive is the invariant by which the composite stands as the same closure.

The inherited name **bound state** is Registered for this persistent-composite core. It is not Registered–Sealed. Mature bound-state language commonly carries additional content not supplied by P6: a specified Hamiltonian, normalizable state, spectral threshold, localization, binding energy, asymptotic channel structure, and quantitative lifetime.

P6 licenses the statement:

> Wherever a composite closure is shown to preserve its constitutive invariant across a declared admitted variation-field, it is a persistent composite and may be called a bound state within that mapped core.

The modeled baryon satisfies this condition at its reported flow scope. No claim about atoms, nuclei generally, or all hadrons follows without their own exhibited structures.

## 4. Stability

Persistence and stability are distinct.

- **Persistence** asks whether the invariant is preserved across the declared passages.
- **Stability** additionally asks what happens under a declared class of perturbations.

Given an exhibited perturbation class, a comparison structure, and a transition or return rule, a persistent class is stable when sufficiently admitted perturbations return to that class or remain within its invariant-preserving basin.

This is a Secured conditional classifier. The inherited name **stability** is Registered for that return/retention core. The modeled singlet result satisfies it for the tested perturbations and flows. P6 does not thereby supply:

- a topology or metric beyond the one used by the owning model;
- universal dynamics or every physically relevant perturbation;
- attraction rates or basin measures;
- energy minimization in general;
- a proof that every familiar standing is stable.

“Coherence-maximal” may not be substituted for a missing return dynamics, but an actually exhibited return dynamics must not be ignored because it appears later in the written walk.

## 5. Metastability and decay

Given an exhibited passage structure, a standing is metastable when it preserves its identifying invariant over a restricted passage-class but admits at least one allowed escape passage into a differently registered closure-class.

Decay, at P6 scope, is that loss or reconfiguration of the prior invariant through an admitted escape passage. The names **metastability** and **decay** are Registered for this structural core.

P6 does not establish without an owning construction:

- that a standing is a local maximum;
- that an energy or coherence barrier exists;
- the identities or multiplicities of decay products;
- conservation-law bookkeeping for a particular channel;
- a weak-interaction mechanism;
- an absolute or relative rate.

Later decay constructions may satisfy these conditions and thereby instantiate P6; their later label is irrelevant, while their actual dependencies remain binding.

## 6. Escape propensity versus decay rate

An escape set may receive a relative weight only when an applicable weighting construction supplies a measure over the admitted alternatives. P6 adds no new weighting theorem. Any use of the realizability-weighting law inherits that page’s exact conditions and unresolved record, including the positive-pairing dependency and the open frequency interpretation.

Even a justified measure over escape alternatives is not yet a decay rate. A rate or lifetime additionally requires:

- an exhibited transition rule or hazard per step;
- a demonstrated link between the measure and repeated actualization frequencies;
- a clock or step calibration;
- for absolute rates, the relevant scale and coupling data.

Accordingly:

- structural escape-accessibility is available conditionally;
- a relative escape propensity is conditional on the separately warranted weighting construction;
- decay rates and lifetimes remain Open unless a later owning construction actually supplies those further structures.

## 7. Identity through succession

Within P6 scope, continuity of identity means continuity of the registered invariant across lineage-related passages. This licenses the familiar phrase **identity over time** only for the invariant-continuity core, since P4/P5 supply the relevant succession language.

The registration is not sealed. It does not settle every metaphysical or empirical criterion of identity, nor imply that all properties remain unchanged.

## 9. Superseded P6 routes

Do not use P6 alone to derive or support:

- exact spatial dimension or `3+1`;
- a universal inverse-square binding law;
- atoms or the periodic table;
- universal confinement dynamics;
- the particle spectrum;
- electroweak breaking or a Higgs doublet;
- mass values, coupling values, decay products, or lifetimes;
- the claim that every fixed point is attracting;
- the claim that realizability weighting by itself produces observed frequencies.

Do not infer from this exclusion that later-established reality is barred from satisfying P6. The modeled baryon attractor is the canonical counterexample: it is articulated later but supplies a scoped P6 witness.

## 10. Frontier

### P6-F1 — Domain-grade persistence and the basic stable ruler

**Open question:** What is the relevant totality of persisting physical closures, and which closure—if any—is the most basic stable ruler for the value cascade?

**Established:** The D6 phase-form; scoped physical occupancy and stability in the modeled baryon case; P5 finite succession and no-traveler re-resolution.

**Missing:** A justified account of the relevant persistent population standing as one field; identification of the earliest/basic stable closure; and the calibration role required by the value program.

**Affected downstream claims:** stable-closure/value cascade, mass calibration, atoms, spectra, chemistry handoff, and every claim requiring a basic stable ruler.

**False-completion risk:** treating one scoped witness as the total D6 field or assuming that the first later-analyzed closure is ontologically basic.

**Next legitimate research action:** survey all existing downstream closure results for P6 witnesses and dependency order in reality; then determine whether any is basic enough to ground calibration.

### P6-F2 — Quantitative stability and decay

**Open question:** What transition, perturbation, weighting, and calibration structures convert structural persistence/escape into a quantitative stability basin and decay rate?

**Established:** Conditional structural classifiers; one tested stability witness for the modeled singlet; the weighting page supplies only its separately bounded measure construction.

**Missing:** General perturbation geometry where claimed, transition or hazard law, frequency bridge, step/clock calibration, and scale/coupling inputs.

**Affected downstream claims:** lifetimes, branching ratios, barrier language, quantitative stability, and specific decay channels.

**False-completion risk:** calling a relative weight a frequency or lifetime, or generalizing one tested attractor beyond its model class.

**Next legitimate research action:** examine later concrete decay and closure results for actual transition laws and rates, irrespective of their P-number, while retaining every genuine dependency.
